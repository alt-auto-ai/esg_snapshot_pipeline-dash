## **_Strengthening Governance Through Psychology and Structured Design of Human–AI Decision-Making._**
The Company Secretary is becoming the go to strategic partner in the psychology of AI, influence, and boardroom deliberation. AI doesn’t just change corporate process or add information to board decisions. It alters how humans think, speak, defer, and disagree. Increasingly, the Company Secretary will become the central architect of how humans and AI deliberate and decide together in modern organisations. 
Technology is reshaping how executives lead and how organisations govern. A Company Secretaries who understands both human _and_ technological dimensions will be best placed to strengthen compliance, manage risk, and support sustainable decision-making in an increasingly complex corporate environment.
Company Secretaries sit at the intersection of governance, information flow, regulatory obligations, and executive decision-making. In the future of work, leading Company Secretaries will be uniquely placed to proactively work with technology and leadership to advance and influence organisational performance. 
## **Join us for a discussion exploring:**
1. Executive relationships with technology; the psychology of decisions with AI, and the effect on risk culture. 
2. Company secretaries as a deliberation architect between technology, governance frameworks, and executive accountability. 
3. From process-driven to strategic synthesis: elevating from governance executor to Human-AI decision architect. 
Through expert insight and interactive discussion, this session explores how Company Secretaries can position themselves as trusted advisors in technology-enabled governance. It will examine how to align emerging technologies with risk culture, governance frameworks and evolving regulatory expectations.
Our Discussion Group will, as always, address cutting edge issues and case studies in corporate governance and risk management.
