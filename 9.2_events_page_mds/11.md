##  About 
Quantum technologies are moving from scientific potential towards real-world deployment, promising breakthroughs with far-reaching economic and societal benefits, while raising pressing questions related to digital security, privacy and national security. The field is characterised by interdependences, including shared challenges in science and engineering, and globally dispersed supply chains and talent pools, among others. 
This 2026 OECD Ministerial Council Meeting side event of the [OECD Global Forum on Technology](https://www.oecd.org/en/networks/global-forum-on-technology.html), co-organised with the support of Finland, Japan, Korea and the United Kingdom, will explore countries’ strategic visions for unlocking the transformative potential of quantum technologies while managing their risks, both domestically and through international co-operation.

##  Global Forum on Technology

GFTech is a venue for regular in-depth dialogue to foresee and get ahead of long-term opportunities and risks presented by technology. ##  Related policy areas 
  * [ Quantum technologies ](https://www.oecd.org/en/topics/sub-issues/quantum-technologies.html)
Quantum technologies exploit the unique behaviours of particles at atomic scales to gather, process and transmit information. They promise to ramp up innovation across sectors like health care, finance, and industry, while helping to tackle global challenges such as climate change and food security. However, these technologies also pose risks to digital security and privacy, including threats to current cryptographic methods that protect communications and transactions. 
Learn more
  * [ Emerging technologies ](https://www.oecd.org/en/topics/emerging-technologies.html)
Emerging technologies, from synthetic biology and neurotechnology to artificial intelligence, immersive and quantum technologies, are characterised by rapid development and uncertainty in trajectory and impact. A challenge for policymakers is to enable innovation where technologies deliver social and economic benefit, while meeting the governance imperatives that anticipate risks, protect established rights and human agency. 
Learn more
  * [ Technology policy ](https://www.oecd.org/en/topics/technology-policy.html)
Technological innovation is an engine of human well-being and economic activity, but also raises concerns for individuals and society. Governments use a mix of policies targeting specific technologies to steer their responsible development and use. This includes national plans that provide strategic orientation and support measures for research, innovation and diffusion activities. Policies also promote ethical practice through regulations and guidelines. 
Learn more

#  Webinar: AGM readiness in a digital age 
3 June 2026  2:00pm - 3:30pm 
National, Virtual Event 
Member $0
Non-Member $45
[Register](https://www.governanceinstitute.com.au/events/e10536-webinar-agm-readiness-in-a-digital-age/)[Become a member](https://governanceinstitute.com.au/join-now/)
This webinar will focus on AGM preparation for company secretaries and governance professionals, recognising that the AGM remains one of the most visible and highest risk moments in the governance calendar. As expectations around transparency, engagement and accountability continue to rise, technology and AI are playing an increasingly important role in how AGMs are planned, conducted and managed.
The session will explore key AGM content, themes and expectations, including what boards, shareholders and regulators are currently focused on, alongside practical considerations such as agenda setting, disclosures, shareholder engagement, resolutions, and managing complex or contentious issues. Particular attention will be given to the use of technology in AGMs, including hybrid and virtual meeting formats, digital voting, real time shareholder questions, record keeping and cyber considerations.  
  
Drawing on recent AGM experience, the webinar will also examine how emerging tools, including AI enabled document review, drafting support, meeting preparation and issue tracking, can assist company secretaries and governance teams to prepare more effectively, identify risks earlier and respond more confidently to stakeholder scrutiny. The session will highlight common challenges, emerging trends and lessons learned, and provide practical insights that participants can apply immediately to strengthen AGM readiness while maintaining appropriate governance, oversight and control.
## What's in it for you:
Learning aims and objectives
• Clarify current AGM expectations of boards, shareholders and regulators   
• Support effective AGM preparation and risk management for company secretaries and governance teams   
• Explore how technology and AI can enhance AGM readiness, efficiency and oversight   
• Identify practical ways to use digital tools for agenda planning, disclosures, voting and engagement   
• Highlight emerging AGM trends, challenges and lessons learned from recent meetings   
• Equip participants with practical insights they can apply immediately in the AGM cycle
