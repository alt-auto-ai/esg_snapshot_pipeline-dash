## **Topic:**
Public sector organisations are under increasing pressure, managing multiple, overlapping risks while delivering essential services to the community. This discussion group will explore whether the public sector has the capacity, capability and frameworks needed to respond effectively.
We’ll discuss how data and information are being used to develop insights that support decision‑making, and whether gaps still exist that make it harder to act with confidence and speed. We’ll also look at how principles from CPS 230, while designed for APRA regulated entities, can be applied as best practice for public sector and non‑financial organisations to strengthen operational resilience.
With climate risk, cyber threats, workforce pressures and supply‑chain disruption front of mind, we’ll explore how prepared the public sector really is and what this means for communities and society more broadly.
This will be an interactive discussion designed to share experiences, challenge thinking, and highlight practical steps public sector organisations can take to build resilience now
## Speaker Highlight:
**Kumar Srinivasan FGIA, Director Risk & Insurance, University of Technology Sydney**
![](https://www.governanceinstitute.com.au/app/uploads/2026/03/Kumar.jpg)
Kumar Srinivasan is a board-level governance, risk and compliance leader with 25 years experience guiding organisations through complexity and change and overseeing complex public infrastructure, utilities and education portfolios in Australia.
As Director of Risk & Insurance at the University of Technology Sydney, he shapes resilient strategies that protect research innovation and commercial partnerships while embedding risk intelligence into every decision across the institution.
Before joining UTS, Kumar served as Chief Risk Officer at Sydney Metro and held senior roles at Transport for NSW and Sydney Water, he also led major GRC transformations.
A Fellow of the Governance Institute of Australia and Engineers Australia, he’s widely recognised for fostering risk-aware cultures and helping leaders turn uncertainty into opportunity.
