## About this talk
We are pleased to invite you to our upcoming Sustainability Webinar: Proxy Voting Season Preview, where our global corporate governance specialists will discuss emerging trends and key themes shaping the 2026 proxy voting season. This session will provide valuable insights for clients, prospects, consultants, and other stakeholders looking to stay informed on governance developments and voting considerations. Presenters Andrew Droste leads Columbia Threadneedle’s Corporate Governance team. He previously headed North America Stewardship at the Carbon Tracker Initiative and advised corporate leaders on governance and sustainability at Russell Reynolds Associates. Andrew also served on investment stewardship teams at BNY Mellon and Nuveen. In addition, he is an officer in the U.S. Army Reserve, advising on economic, financial, governance, and regulatory matters. Yu-Ting Fu supports ESG engagement and corporate governance analysis across APAC. She joined from Federated Hermes, where she focused on Asia based ESG engagement. Her background includes sustainability research and earlier work as a social worker supporting indigenous and vulnerable communities. David Patt leads corporate governance voting in the UK and engages financial sector issuers across Europe. He previously served at UK Government Investments, advising Ministers on governance, stewardship, and ESG strategy. Before UKGI, he spent over a decade at LGIM in senior ESG, stewardship, and policy roles. Nicole Lantigua focuses on voting, engagement, and stewardship research across North America and Latin America. She previously held investment stewardship roles at BlackRock and Vanguard and gained corporate experience at SMBC. Her work spans voting analysis, ESG integration, and market specific engagement insights. Coen Kleinegris is a corporate governance analyst covering the European market, with prior experience at MN’s Responsible Investment team and the Dutch financial market authority.
