##  About 
As global demand for critical minerals intensifies, new opportunities and risks are emerging across supply chains. With production and processing highly concentrated in a handful of countries, disruptions and geopolitical tensions can have far-reaching consequences for energy security and economic stability worldwide. 
Through its Critical Minerals Initiative, the OECD aims to help countries diversify their supplies, capture greater value from their resources and ensure that supply chains support sustainable, inclusive and resilient growth. 
The **OECD Critical Minerals Forum** will explore how countries can work together to build resilient critical mineral supply chains while supporting resource-rich countries develop local value and diversify their economies.
The Forum will provide a platform for high-level cross-regional dialogue between OECD Members, partner countries and key stakeholders from the private sector and civil society dedicated to developing solutions to shared challenges in the critical minerals space.
**This event is part of the OECD Emerging Markets Forum Series organised by the OECD Istanbul Centre.**

##  Forum Structure 
###  High-Level Dialogue 
The high-level session will bring together Ministers and senior officials from OECD and non-OECD countries, as well as senior representatives of the private sector and civil society to discuss strategic priorities and collective action on critical minerals.
###  Regional Breakout Sessions 
Parallel breakout sessions will focus on regional interests in critical minerals and cooperation opportunities:
Block 1 Central Asia – Mobilising investment while managing the risks in critical minerals supply chains: an ambitious agenda
Block 2 LAC – Addressing the missing links: from a lithium and copper provider region into a high-value industrial hub for battery and electric vehicle production?
Block 3 South East Asia focus - Regional perspectives on developing critical mineral strategies: Reliable, responsible, resilient by design?
Block 4 Africa / MENA – Joining the dots: Minerals, Connectivity and Africa's industrial future
###  Technical-Level Dialogue 
Technical-level dialogue led by OECD experts will explore targeted cooperation opportunities and policy implementation strategies:
Topic 1 Trade Dimensions of Critical Minerals
Topic 2 Regional Connectivity and the Lobito Corridor
Topic 3 Mitigating Material Criticality through Circular Economy Strategies 
Topic 4 Traceability and Responsible Business Conduct
