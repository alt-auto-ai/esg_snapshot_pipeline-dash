## Annual General Meetings - Is All the Fuss Worth It?
For many organisations, the Annual General Meeting sits somewhere between sacred ritual and administrative headache – a mere formality or a genuine governance moment that rightfully earns its place in the calendar.
On paper, the case for the AGM is straightforward enough eg shareholder accountability, director election, financial transparency and a forum for members to have their say. In practice, it can feel like a great deal of effort for a room that's half-empty, a webcast nobody watches and questions which could have easily been answered by email.
But is that a reason to question the AGM or simply a sign that we're not doing it well?
This session invites company secretaries to reflect honestly on the AGM in their own context - what it costs (in time, resource, and goodwill), what it actually delivers and whether the current format is fit for purpose. Are hybrid and virtual models improving access or just reducing friction? Is member disengagement a structural problem or a communications one? And if we were designing the AGM from scratch today, would we end up in the same place?
Come ready to share your experiences the smooth, the chaotic and the memorably awkward.
Join your company secretary peers for an informal roundtable exchange of ideas and practical advice. This is your unique opportunity to discuss current trends and issues in your role.
The discussion will be followed by an opportunity to build your company secretary network over drinks and nibblies.

# Launceston Directors' Briefing: Culture in the Boardroom
3rd June 2026 
Launceston, TAS
#### Culture is one of the most powerful drivers of organisational performance, risk and reputation yet it remains one of the most difficult areas for boards to govern effectively. In an environment of heightened scrutiny, shifting workforce expectations and complex stakeholder demands, directors are increasingly expected to move beyond surface‑level reporting and develop a deeper line of sight into organisational culture.

This director briefing will explore the board’s role in shaping, testing and sustaining culture from the top. Through practical insights and real‑world examples, the session will examine how boards can move from intent to impact embedding culture into governance frameworks, decision‑making and accountability mechanisms. Directors will consider how culture shows up under pressure, how it is reinforced through incentives and behaviours, and how boards can respond when values and outcomes diverge.
Designed for experienced and emerging directors, this briefing will provide a practical forum to reflect on how culture is governed in the boardroom, and what effective oversight looks like in practice.
