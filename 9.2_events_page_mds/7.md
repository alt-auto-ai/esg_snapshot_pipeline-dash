#  NSW Roundtable: AASB S2 – Lessons from the first wave of mandatory climate statements

**This event is exclusively for in‑house governance professionals; consultants and advisory firms are not permitted to attend, and registrations that do not meet this requirement may be cancelled.**
**What 30 June Reporters Should Do Differently – Member-only event**
The first mandatory climate statements under AASB S2 are now in the public domain. Collectively, they provide powerful insight into how organisations are interpreting financial materiality, quantifying climate-related impacts, applying scenario analysis, and demonstrating board oversight in a mandatory environment.
In this exclusive roundtable, RSM’s ESG and Climate Services team will present its comprehensive analysis of all published Group 1 disclosures moving beyond benchmarking to identify leading practices, recurring gaps, areas of inconsistency, and themes.
RSM will also facilitate a focused discussion on what these early disclosures signal about director accountability, integration with enterprise risk management, internal controls, and assurance readiness.
**Learning aims and objectives**
This session will provide practical and relevant insights to help 30 June reporters and Group 2 reporters strengthen oversight, enhance disclosure quality, and approach their first reporting cycle with greater confidence.
## **Speaker Highlights:**
**Nicole Mohan, Partner, RSM**
**![](https://www.governanceinstitute.com.au/app/uploads/2026/04/Nicole-Mohan-1.jpg)**
Nicole Mohan is a Partner in our ESG & Climate Services Division and RSM’s National Sustainability Lead where she plays a key role in driving RSM’s own sustainability agenda and developing initiatives to help the adoption of sustainable practices and values across RSM.
She helps organisations identify and evaluate potential climate-related risks and opportunities, integrating these insights into their strategic planning and operations. By adhering to recognised frameworks, she ensures clients’ sustainability reports meet regulatory requirements and industry expectations. Nicole supports businesses to enhance their resilience to climate impacts, improving transparency, and demonstrating their commitment to sustainable practices.
Nicole is a Category 2 Registered Greenhouse and Energy Auditor and has managed numerous engagements under the National Greenhouse and Energy Reporting (“NGER”), Safeguard Mechanism, Renewable Energy Target (“RET”) and Emission Reduction Fund (“ERF”) schemes.
