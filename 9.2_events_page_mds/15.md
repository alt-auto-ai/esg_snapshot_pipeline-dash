Overview
Cyber Security is not just technical. Join practitioners, leaders and students to explore how people make security initiatives succeed.
Cyber Security initiatives rarely fail because of technology.
They fail because people were never brought on the journey.
Multi-factor authentication. Security policies. Identity platforms. Monitoring tools. AI governance.
The technical solutions are often sound.  
But without adoption, understanding, and leadership support - they struggle to land.
This interactive workshop explores the ****intersection between cyber security and change management**** , and why both disciplines must work together to make security initiatives succeed.
Hosted by ****Cyber Security Champions of Tomorrow (CSCoT)**** , this session brings together cyber practitioners, change managers, leaders, and emerging professionals to unpack what it really takes to implement security change inside organisations.
The evening is designed as a ****hands-on working session**** where participants will:
• Explore real-world cyber change scenarios  
• Learn practical frameworks such as ****ADKAR through a cyber security lens****  
• Work in mixed-discipline groups to solve common security adoption challenges  
• Exchange perspectives between cyber practitioners and change professionals  
• Build relationships across disciplines and experience levels
#### Who Should Attend
This workshop is designed for a ****diverse audience**** , including:
• Cyber security practitioners struggling to gain buy-in for security initiatives  
• Change managers involved in digital transformation projects  
• Leaders responsible for bridging technical and business perspectives  
• Early-career professionals building cross-functional cyber skills  
• Career changers exploring the intersection of people, technology, and governance
If you have ever thought:
__"Why is this security initiative so hard to implement?"__
This session is for you.
📍 ****Hybrid event**** – attend in person in Brisbane or join online via Zoom.
The workshop will include:
  * Light food and drinks
  * Short mentor insights
  * Interactive table discussions
  * Real-world cyber change scenarios
  * Networking with practitioners and mentors
