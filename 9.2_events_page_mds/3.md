**Topic: Lessons from The Star - Rethinking the Role of the Chief Risk Officer**
The recently released 500‑page Star decision offers one of the clearest examples of how governance and risk oversight can break down when the Chief Risk Officer is not fully empowered or supported. In this discussion, we’ll unpack what the inquiry revealed about ineffective escalation, filtered reporting, fragmented AML/CTF responsibilities and the CRO’s limited ability to challenge commercial pressures. We’ll explore why risk warnings failed to reach the board with the urgency required, how cultural and structural barriers undermined independence, and what it truly means for a CRO to act as a guardian of organisational integrity. This session invites participants to reflect on the practical lessons for their own organisations — from strengthening escalation pathways to ensuring the CRO has the authority, access and cultural backing needed to lead risk, culture and compliance in a meaningful way.
