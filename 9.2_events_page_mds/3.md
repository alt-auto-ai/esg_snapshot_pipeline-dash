Frontline environmental defenders (or 
Defenders stand on the front lines of the global climate fight while facing corruption, intimidation, threats and even murder ([research](https://www.wri.org/research/connecting-climate-action-and-environmental-human-rights-defense) shows they remain largely shut out of decision-making and formal climate governance - while often facing criminalization in their home countries. As a result, governments and multilateral institutions lose out on crucial local knowledge and decades-old ecosystem expertise that can improve climate outcomes, while defenders are left to brave risks alone.
