##  About 
The OECD Centre for Entrepreneurship, SME’s, Regions and Cities (CFE) helps local, regional, and national governments unleash the potential of entrepreneurs and small and medium-sized enterprises, promote inclusive and sustainable regions and cities, boost local job creation, and support sound tourism policies. In 2019, the Working Party on Rural Policy adopted the [OECD Principles on Rural Policy](https://www.oecd.org/en/topics/regional-rural-and-urban-development.html). 
The Principles are designed to assist national, regional and local governments in implementing more effective rural policies that will lead to improved quality of life and a more resilient, sustainable and inclusive future for all. This workshop is part of a three-part dialogue focusing on Rural Communication, within a broader series of activities aimed at gaining insight into how OECD member countries are putting the Principles into practice.
##  Context 
Rural proofing is a process designed to inform policy decisions. To influence outcomes, it must deliver the right evidence, strategically engage stakeholders and provide information in forms that policymakers can actually use. 
Rural proofing relies on an understanding of rural realities and of the policies that aim to achieve them; this is essentially rural proofing intelligence. It provides the essential knowledge needed to help policymakers make evidence-informed decisions about rural places. While some data may be new, most already exist; in fact, many countries already collect territorial or regional data that could be leveraged for rural proofing purposes. This workshop explores rural proofing intelligence in depth and provides examples of different types of evidence that can be used to understand rural areas better
##  Topics for discussion 
  * Identifying the components of rural proofing intelligence
  * Leveraging existing data that could serve rural proofing purposes
  * Rural intelligence can help shift the narrative from rural as a burden to rural as an equal partner
  * Having the right intelligence helps ensure that rural proofing becomes a meaningful process
