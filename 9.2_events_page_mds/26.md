Overview
Learn about Coastal Restoration and Harbour Health with marine scientists.
Join Woollahra Council and Project Restore scientists from the Sydney Institute of Marine Science (SIMS) for an educational event that brings our local marine biodiversity to life, inspired by local harbour restoration work!
Learn about Project Restore, a multi-habitat marine restoration initiative in Sydney Harbour. Project Restore is part of the Seabirds to Seascapes program which is an exciting 4-year program working to protect, rehabilitate and sustainably manage New South Wales’s natural marine ecosystems. Project Restore aims to restore the endangered __Posidonia australis__ seagrass meadows, rehabilitate kelp-dominated rocky reefs, and install Living Seawall panels and Reef Pods across different sites within the harbour.
Woollahra has become the area where most of the Project Restore action is taking place and we are inviting the local community to join this forum/workshop and hear all about the exciting restoration work that is taking place in your local area. You will also hear about Council’s effort to improve water quality and biodiversity in our marine environment.
In this workshop, you’ll hear about our local marine habitats, how they have been locally degraded, why it is important to bring them back. We will cover restoration plans, with details on the restoration locations, the process of implementing an innovative multi-habitat approach, and how Project Restore are monitoring the restoration sites to ensure the project is a sustainable success. This presentation will be followed by a Q&A session.
  

****What to Expect********?**** Learn about local marine biodiversity, a current restoration project and the importance of protecting our waterways.
****Who should attend?**** This free event is designed for adults to learn, create, and celebrate local biodiversity, species habitats, habitat restoration initiatives and marine stewardship. Suitable for +14years.
