Overview
Join SIL Providers, Support Coordinators, SDA Providers, Allied Health professionals and prepare for upcoming 2026 changes
The Supported Independent Living (SIL) sector is entering one of the most significant structural transformations since the introduction of the NDIS.
The SILSDA Adelaide Maximising SIL Impact Summit 2026 brings together national leaders, providers, regulators, allied health professionals, housing stakeholders, and operational decision-makers to examine the future of SIL delivery in an increasingly regulated, evidence-driven, and outcomes-focused NDIS environment.
This Summit It is a practical, frontline strategy forum designed for organisations navigating immediate reform pressure, compliance scrutiny, workforce challenges, funding uncertainty, and operational risk.
As the NDIS moves toward tighter eligibility thresholds, commissioned service models, increased audit enforcement, and stronger integrity controls, SIL providers are being required to fundamentally rethink service delivery, rostering models, governance frameworks, participant evidence, and long-term sustainability.
The 2026 Summit provides delegates with the strategic insight, operational guidance, and cross-sector collaboration needed to adapt, reposition, and remain viable in a rapidly changing disability support landscape.
****What to expect****
This high-impact conference will explore the major reforms, operational challenges, and strategic shifts reshaping Supported Independent Living across Australia.
Delegates will gain practical insight into:
****Key Conference Topics****
  * The NDIS Integrity & Safeguarding Bill and expanding compliance powers
  * Increased enforcement, audit activity, and governance expectations
  * The transition toward commissioned SIL services and capped growth models
  * The redesign conversation of Supported Independent Living under the new NDIS framework
  * Functional assessment bottlenecks impacting SIL access and SDA occupancy
  * Workforce readiness, competency requirements, and screening obligations
  * The Rise of Head Lease SIL Housing: Activating vacant SDA Homes and expanding sustainable SIL Operations
  * Hospital discharge pressures and participant transition pathways
  * Long-term sustainability strategies for providers operating in a controlled NDIS market
  * Tribunal and review system redesign impacting funding certainty


****Who should attend?****
This Summit is essential for professionals and organisations operating across the disability housing and support ecosystem, including:
  * SIL providers and operational leaders
  * SDA providers, developers, and housing operators
  * Support coordinators and case managers
  * Allied health professionals involved in functional assessments
  * Hospital discharge and transitional care teams
  * NDIS compliance, audit, and governance specialists
  * Plan managers and funding administrators
  * Disability housing investors and impact housing developers
  * Legal, advisory, and regulatory professionals in the NDIS sector
  * Government and policy stakeholders


****Networking & Sector Collaboration****
The summit concludes with extended networking opportunities, bringing together leaders from SIL, SDA, allied health, hospitals, housing, compliance, and government sectors.
Build valuable industry connections, explore collaborative opportunities, and engage in critical conversations shaping the future of disability support and supported housing in Australia.
