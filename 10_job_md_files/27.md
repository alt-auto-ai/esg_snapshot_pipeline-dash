About the department
  

The Department of Industry, Science and Resources and our broader portfolio are integral to the Australian Government’s economic agenda. Our purpose is to help the government build a better future for all Australians through enabling a productive, resilient and sustainable economy, enriched by science and technology. We do this by:
  

  * Growing innovative & competitive businesses, industries and regions
  * Investing in science and technology
  * Strengthening the resources sector.


  

The APS and the department offer a clear direction and meaningful work. You will be able to create positive impact in people’s lives whilst contributing to improved outcomes for Australia and our people.
  

If you would like to feel a strong connection to your work and you are accountable, committed and open to change, join us in shaping Australia’s future.
  

Please see the [APSC’s APS Employee Value Proposition](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https%3A%2F%2Fcontent.apsjobs.gov.au%2Fwork-with-us) for more information on the benefits and value of employment within the APS.
  

About the team
  

Our department/branch has a commitment to inclusion and diversity, with an ambition of being the best possible place to work. This reflects the importance we place on our people and on creating a workplace culture where each and every one of us is valued and respected for our contribution. Our ideal candidate adds to this culture and our workplace in their own way.
  

Our ideal candidate
  

Our ideal candidates would have the following skills, capability and experience:
  

  * Highly motivated, and capable of managing and building high performing teams that achieve agreed outcomes
  * Be a strategic thinker and planner
  * Strong leadership and mentoring skills
  * Analytical and problem-solving skills, with proven experience in questioning current processes and looking for opportunities to improve and redesign current systems and processes
  * A talented customer centric communicator, listener and relationship builder
  * Experience and understanding of whole of government & department policies including government financial framework and PGPA Act.
  * Well-developed written and verbal communication skills
  * Confident in using, and the ability to understand Financial Management Information systems
  * Demonstrated experience in management and/or financial accounting in a government or commercial setting
  * Ability to manage competing priorities and tight deadlines
  * Emotional maturity, resilience, and patience to deal with diversity within the workplace, and
  * An intermediate to advanced Excel and Microsoft Office suite user including the use of AI as a support tool.


#### The key duties of the position include:
**The key duties of the position include:**
  

Assistant Finance Managers manage the delivery of effective, responsive and consistent financial services to our client division(s) to support the best use of government resources. As an Assistant Manager at the Executive Level 1 in the Finance Business Partner section, you will be responsible for:
  

  * Actively partnering with key stakeholders (Senior Executives and Managers) to gain an understanding of their business, and facilitate cooperation, trust and credibility.
  * Developing and coordinating budgets and forecasts based on operational needs of divisions.
  * Preparing and presenting timely financial reports, with meaningful analysis that speaks to performance, key risks and opportunities.
  * Managing and supporting staff in the delivery of professional, high quality, timely and customer focussed financial services.
  * Contributing to process improvement activities to support the consistent and efficient delivery of corporate services.
  * Delivery of other financial services as directed.

Department of the Treasury
### APS5-APS6 Investment Review Officers
Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC, Sydney NSW
**Foreign Investment Division** are seeking applications for Investment Review Officers to fill positions within **Investment Review Branch.**
  

Treasury’s Investment Review Branch critically assesses foreign investment applications to enhance Australia’s economy and protect Australia’s national interest and national security. 
  

As a case officer in the Investment Review Branch, you will regularly prepare advice for the Treasurer, Treasury’s senior executives and the Foreign Investment Review Board on complex foreign investment proposals involving national security and other national interest issues. 
  

The work of the Investment Review Branch stands at the intersection of government and industry. You will work with Commonwealth, State and Territory agencies and the National Intelligence Community to identify and mitigate potential foreign investment risks. You will also work closely with foreign investors and their advisers, building deep links with the private sector. Within Treasury, you will work with our compliance, assurance, operational and policy teams to assess proposals and be part of our continuous improvement programme. 
  

Your work will inform decisions on individual investment proposals to ensure they are consistent with Australia’s national interest and our national security. 
#### The key duties of the position include:
Investment Review Officers at the**APS5** level will: 
  * Manage a portfolio of active, moderately complex foreign investment proposals in a fast-paced, dynamic regulatory environment. 
  * Have accountability for tasks and decisions and drive foreign investment assessments through to completion. 
  * Develop and maintain working relationships with internal and external stakeholders in progressing the assessments and delivering high quality advice and decisions. 
  * Actively support your manager in setting work priorities, addressing risks, and administering foreign investment proposals in line with legislative and policy requirements. 
  * Contribute to various business and process improvement initiatives. 


  

Senior Investment Review Officers at the **APS6** level, under limited direction, will: 
  * Manage several highly complex foreign investment proposals in a fast-paced, dynamic regulatory environment, including providing timely and accurate specialised advice. 
  * Develop and support complex relationships with internal and external stakeholders, including anticipating and responding to stakeholder needs and expectations and deal with conflicting positions. 
  * Contribute to and assist your manager in setting work priorities, addressing risks, and administering foreign investment proposals in line with legislative and policy requirements. 
  * Contribute to various business and process improvement initiatives. 


  

Investment Review Officers at the **APS5 level** and **APS 6 level** may also be required to support foreign investment operations, for example responding to foreign investment enquiries, administering fees and payments, as well as assisting stakeholders with information technology systems support. 
  

**Desirable Skills / Experience**
  

__Successful applicants will:__
  * Assess and critically analyse foreign investment matters to identify risks to Australia’s national interest and national security, and regulatory risk and address them through appropriate risk mitigation strategies. 
  * Engage a broad range of internal and external stakeholders, including investors, their advisers, and Australian, state and territory government agencies. 
  * Develop and deliver advice for Treasury Ministers and other decision makers to support the Government’s foreign investment policy objectives and meet community expectations about the regulation of foreign investment in Australia. 
  * Be an active contributor in a flexible, diverse, supportive, and high-performing team that is driven by achieving quality outcomes.
