### Senior Internal Auditor
Brisbane QLD, Canberra ACT
**Why YOU are important to us and what impact will you have on the People, Property and Security Division and the departments’ goal.**
We support our people across the employment life cycle, enabling the effective management of people-related risk and building future ready leaders, people and culture. We prevent, detect and respond to misconduct, fraud and corruption, deliver objective and risk-based internal audit, assurance and advisory services, and provide support to our two independent Inspectors-General. We ensure the effective management of our property portfolio, facilities management, security, sustainability, facilities emergency management, and a range of corporate site services and commercial contracts nationwide.
  

The **Security, Integrity and Assurance branch** is responsible for the delivery of the department’s integrated protective security and integrity frameworks. We prevent, detect and respond to security incidents, suspected misconduct, fraud and corruption and develop unique insights to proactively identify and mitigate risks to the department and its operations. Responsible for the provision of independent, objective and risk-based internal audit, assurance and advisory services, we also provide independent support to the Inspector-General of Biosecurity and Inspector-General of Animal Welfare and Live Animal Exports.
  

As an Internal Auditor, you will use your auditing expertise to deliver independent, objective, and risk-based assurance aimed at evaluating and enhancing the department’s operations. This position requires a strong sense of accountability, adaptability, integrity and the ability to collaborate effectively with a diverse range of stakeholders.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**This is where you play an important role.**
As the Senior Internal Auditor, you will:
  * Support the delivery of the annual internal audit program by conducting internal audits in accordance with professional auditing standards and internal frameworks.
  * Prepare high quality audit reports that clearly communicate issues, root causes, risks, and recommendations for improvement.
  * Build and maintain strong working relationships across the department.
  * Be adaptive and involved in all aspects of the work undertaken by the internal audit function.


**Please note** that the merit pool established through this selection process, which is valid for a period of eighteen months from the date the vacancy was advertised in the Public Service Gazette, may also be used to fill future ongoing vacancies in the branch where the duties are of a similar nature.
#### Eligibility
**Our ideal candidate will have:**
  * Demonstrated knowledge of internal auditing standards, risk frameworks, and governance principles.
  * Demonstrated ability to conduct internal audit engagements within a complex organisation.
  * Demonstrated skills in written and oral communication, consultation and negotiation that will facilitate the achievement of internal audit objectives.
  * Demonstrated research and critical thinking skills and the ability to develop innovative, sensitive and practical solutions and provide advice on complex audit issues.
  * Demonstrated ability to work effectively in a team environment and contribute to a continuous learning culture.
  * Integrity, professionalism, and a commitment to ethical conduct.


  

**_The successful candidate will have:_**
At least 2 years’ experience in an internal or external audit role.
RecruitAbility applies to this vacancy. Under the RecruitAbility scheme you will be invited to participate in further assessment activity for the vacancy if you choose to apply under the scheme; declare you have a disability; and meet the minimum requirements for the job.
