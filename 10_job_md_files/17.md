### Finance Business Partner
Adelaide SA, Brisbane QLD, Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC, Sydney NSW
**Why YOU are important to us and what impact will you have on the Finance & Investment Division and the departments’ goal**.
The Finance and Investment Division ensures the department remains financially sustainable, compliant and well-equipped to meet its goals. We provide expert financial advice, tools and support to help teams manage budgets, make informed decisions and use public resources responsibly. By partnering across the department, we help align financial processes with strategic priorities. We also support ministers and stakeholders with clear, accurate financial insights that drive effective policy and program delivery.
  

The Financial Management and Investment Branch provides expert financial support across accounting, budgeting, tax, loans and compliance. Our team offers tailored advice, training and hands-on assistance to help staff manage resources effectively and meet financial obligations. We ensure public funds are used responsibly and transparently, supporting the department’s financial sustainability. By partnering with teams across the department, we help align financial practices with strategic goals and enable informed decision-making at every level.
  

The Finance Business Partner Section provides strategic and operational financial advice to divisions across the department. The section prepares monthly financial management reports, monitors budgets and forecasts aligned to business plans, and acts as a key liaison between business areas and the Chief Finance Officer (CFO).
#### The key duties of the position include:
**This is where you play an important role.**
In this role, you will provide strategic and operational financial advice across budgeting, forecasting, reporting, and resource management. Working closely with business areas, you will deliver accurate financial information, support budget development and monitoring, and contribute to the continuous improvement of financial management practices.
  

You will work collaboratively within the Finance Business Partner team, under the direction of the Assistant Director, to deliver client focused solutions and high quality financial outcomes.
  

The duties will include but are not limited to the following: 
  

• Provide strategic and operational financial advice and analysis on divisional or group financial performance.
• Deliver accurate, timely and value adding financial information and independent advice to internal stakeholders, including regular financial, resource and business reporting.
• Support the preparation and analysis of budgets and forecasts as well as the monitoring of operational and capital expenditure.
• Contribute to the identification and implementation of improved financial management practices, ensuring compliance with relevant legislative and policy requirements.
• Work collaboratively within the section/team to support a high performing, client focused and solution driven team with clear accountabilities and expected behaviours.
  

**Please note** that this recruitment process will **initially be used to fill two ongoing and one non-ongoing vacancies and that the roles may be able to be performed from any of the locations listed above**. The merit pool established through this selection process, which is valid for a period of eighteen months from the date the vacancy was advertised in the Public Service Gazette, may also be used to fill **future ongoing and non-ongoing vacancies in the branch** where the duties are of a similar nature.
#### Eligibility
**Who we are looking for**
**_Knowledge and experience_**
The successful applicants will demonstrate:
  

• experience in budgeting, forecasting and financial reporting together with strong analytical skills to identify financial risks, pressures and opportunities
• an understanding of cost recovery principles and Commonwealth appropriation frameworks, including departmental and administered appropriations, special appropriations and special accounts
• experience undertaking complex financial analysis and providing clear, evidence based advice to a range of stakeholders
• knowledge of the _Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013_ (PGPA Act) and its application to public sector financial management and accountability
• experience working collaboratively across an organisation, including supporting stakeholders through change
• experience using financial management information systems (e.g. SAP or TechnologyOne) to support financial reporting and decision making.
  

**_Skills and capabilities_**
The successful applicants will demonstrate the ability to:
  

• interpret and clearly communicate complex financial information to stakeholders with varying levels of financial literacy
• explain the connection between financial information, operational activities and organisational objectives
• deliver high quality outcomes through initiative, sound judgement and a commitment to continuous improvement
• apply strong communication and interpersonal skills to build productive working relationships
• manage competing priorities and deadlines while maintaining professionalism and resilience under pressure.
