### APS5-APS6 Analysts and Investigators
Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC, Perth WA, Sydney NSW
**Foreign Investment Division** are seeking applications from experienced and motivated team members for analyst and investigator positions in the **Compliance Enforcement and Regulatory Practice** **branch.**
Depending on the role, responsibilities and duties may include:
  

  * Supporting compliance activities to help ensure Australia’s foreign investment laws are followed.
  * Contributing to the assessment and investigation of potential non-compliance matters.
  * Analysing compliance issues and recommending follow-up actions.
  * Undertaking triage and/or preliminary analysis of compliance reports and referrals.
  * Analysing data to identify trends, risks or emerging issues in compliance activity.
  * Helping to develop internal guidance and processes to support consistent and effective regulation.


#### The key duties of the position include:
Working under supervision, **APS5 Analysts/Investigators** and **APS6 Senior Analysts/Senior Investigators** may: 
  

  * Manage a range of cases at one time, including more complex matters, projects or investigations.
  * Interpret and apply legislation and regulatory frameworks when assessing complex or specialised issues, to support evidence-based recommendations.
  * Contribute to regulatory outcomes by analysing information and data, planning and conducting investigations, gathering evidence, and supporting enforcement processes. 
  * Contribute to regulatory risk assessments to identify priorities for compliance activities and evaluate their effectiveness.
  * Help develop and put in place policies, processes and systems that support the day to day administration of Australia’s foreign investment framework.
  * Contribute to business improvement initiatives across the branch or division.


  

APS6 Senior Analysts/Senior Investigators are expected to work with a higher level of independence and take responsibility for managing their work and decision making.
  

**Desirable Skills / Experience**
  * Experience working with legislation to support regulatory decisions and outcomes.
  * Strong written and verbal communication skills, including the engage effectively with a wide range of stakeholders.
  * The ability to build positive working relationships and work collaboratively to achieve timely and practical outcomes.
  * Well-developed problem-solving and critical thinking skills.
  * High attention to detail in analysis and both written and verbal communication.
  * Good organisational skills and the ability to manage competing priorities.
  * Sound judgment and resilience to achieve objectives in a fast-paced or challenging environment.
  * The ability to work independently, show initiative and deliver results.

### Advisory Panel Secretariat
Sydney NSW
The Advisory Panel Secretariat at ARENA delivers high quality and comprehensive secretarial services to the Advisory Panel which is ARENA’s independent investment committee. This function plays a critical role in supporting ARENA’s investment processes ensuring robust and efficient assessment of projects across the portfolio. The Advisory Panel Secretariat collaborates closely with the Investment and Portfolio group as well as other teams within ARENA including finance and procurement. Reporting directly to the Investment and Program Director, the Panel Secretariat must maintain high standards of service with a focus on integrity and quality assurance. 
Sitting at the interface between the Advisory Panel, Senior Management, and the Investment and Portfolio group, the Panel Secretariat is critical in enabling ARENA’s investment decision making, requiring excellent organisational skills, strong attention to detail and sound judgement. Through high quality process management, the Panel Secretariat plays an important role in ensuring the ongoing processing of ARENA’s investment pipeline at ARENA Panel meetings on a monthly basis. Success in the role will also require a high level of strategic awareness and understanding of ARENA’s operating context as well as maintaining awareness of shifts across investment and strategic priorities and programs. 
#### The key duties of the position include:
In this role, you will: 
  * Act as the key point of contact for Advisory Panel members, providing guidance on ARENA processes.
  * Coordinate end-to-end logistics for monthly Panel meetings (agenda development, paper distribution, meeting coordination, travel, and catering).
  * Review and improve the efficiency and effectiveness of Panel processes and operations, including through the use of digital and AI tools.
  * Liaise with teams across the Investment and Portfolio group, ARENA Executives, and the Advisory Panel Chair on meeting inputs, processes, and outcomes.
  * Manage the Panel conflict-of-interest register seeking advice from ARENA’s legal team and delegate approval on determinations in accordance with ARENA’s conflict of interest policy.
  * Allocate Panel member assessment tasks in ARENA’s Grant Management System (a Salesforce platform).
  * Support internal monthly reporting on Panel operations and assessment outcomes.
  * Draft and quality-assure correspondence, reports, briefings, and other materials, identifying themes across investment portfolios and opportunities for improvement.
  * Support Panel member contracting, including renewals and procurement activities, payments and invoice processing.
  * Develop and maintain guidelines and procedures that support the functions of the role.
  * Provide ad hoc support to the Investment and Portfolio team, as required.
