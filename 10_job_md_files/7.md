### Branch Head - People and Payroll
Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC, Sydney NSW
**Position Overview**
The Entity is a non-Corporate listed entity for the purposes of the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013 (PGPA Act). The Entity supports the operations of the FCA, FCFCoA and the NNTT. The Chief Executive Officer and Principal Registrar is the accountable authority for the Entity having responsibility for the appropriations for the FCA, FCFCoA and NNTT and the provision of corporate services to the Courts and Tribunal.
  

Corporate services include financial management, information technology, human resources management, property and facilities management, risk management, contract administration, communications and information management and library services.
  

**Purpose of position**
The Branch Head, People and Payroll leads the Entity’s workforce strategy and core people functions, including Strategic Workforce Management, Employee Relations, Payroll, People Data, WHS, Recruitment and Organisational Psychology. The role ensures high-quality HR services, robust governance, and evidence-driven workforce planning. It provides authoritative advice to senior leaders, strengthens organisational capability, and supports a safe, inclusive and high-performing workplace.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**Key Responsibilities**
  

_Leadership, people and culture_
  * Lead the People and Culture Branch, setting direction and ensuring coordinated delivery across all core functions.
  * Manage branch performance, risk, resources and capability to deliver high‑quality outcomes.
  * Provide visible leadership across the Entity on workforce and culture matters.
  * Build a constructive, accountable and professional branch culture aligned to APS Values.


  

_People Strategy and Workforce Capability_
  * Set and deliver enterprise people strategy that supports organisational and judicial outcomes.
  * Provide strategic direction on workforce capability, leadership development and talent management.
  * Ensure people strategies are aligned to future organisational needs and risks.
  * Employment Governance and Workforce Integrity
  * Lead APS employment governance including workplace relations, WHS governance, classification and policy settings.
  * Ensure the application of employment frameworks is lawful, consistent and supports organisational integrity.
  * Provide authoritative advice on high‑risk or sensitive matters involving conduct, performance or employment decisions.


  

_Organisational Design and Workforce Reform_
  * Oversee organisational design and workforce architecture to strengthen capability and clarity of roles.
  * Lead people related reform programs that improve operational effectiveness and workforce sustainability.
  * Ensure structural and workforce changes are well-planned, evidence based and effectively implemented.
  * Workforce Planning, HR Systems and People Data
  * Lead enterprise workforce planning, forecasting and workforce analytics.
  * Maintain governance of payroll, HR systems and people data, ensuring accuracy, compliance and appropriate controls.
  * Ensure high-quality workforce insights are available to support executive decision making.
  * Culture, Inclusion and Wellbeing
  * Lead initiatives that strengthen workplace culture, inclusion, diversity and psychological and physical wellbeing.
  * Ensure frameworks, reporting and actions support a respectful and safe work environment.
  * Promote practices that enable a positive and high performing workplace.


  

_Stakeholder Engagement and Collaboration_
  * Build trusted relationships with senior leaders, the CPSU, APS agencies and external workforce partners.
  * Represent the Entity in APS wide workforce forums, committees and system initiatives.
  * Influence broader APS workforce practice through effective engagement and collaboration.


#### Eligibility
**Selection Criteria**
  * Proven ability to lead complex, multidisciplinary teams and deliver enterprise outcomes
  * Strong strategic capability, including workforce planning and organisational design
  * Deep understanding of employment frameworks, industrial relations and WHS in a complex
  * environment
  * Experience overseeing HR systems, payroll and workforce data to inform decision-making
  * Highly developed stakeholder engagement and influencing skills
  * Sound judgement, integrity and the ability to operate effectively in complex and sensitive environments


#### Notes
**Eligibility**
Employment with the Entity is subject to the conditions prescribed in the Public Service Act 1999 (Cth) and the following:
  * Australian citizenship – the successful applicant must hold Australian citizenship 
  * Security and character clearance – the successful applicant must satisfy a Police Records Check and may be required to obtain and maintain a security clearance 
  * Successful applicants engaged into the APS will be subject to a probation period

#### Getting to know the Federal Court of Australia
The Chief Executive Officer and Principal Registrar of the Federal Court, together with officers and staff identified under the Federal Court of Australia Act 1976, the Federal Circuit and Family Court of Australia Act 2021 and the Native Title Act 1993, constitute a single Statutory Agency for the purposes of the Public Service Act 1999. Employees are engaged to work in support of the following courts or tribunal: • Federal Court of Australia, • Federal Circuit and Family Court of Australia, and • National Native Title Tribunal. Employees are covered by the Federal Court of Australia Enterprise Agreement 2024-2027. The Federal Court and the Federal Circuit and Family Court each maintain a distinct statutory identity, with separate functions and judicial independence.
