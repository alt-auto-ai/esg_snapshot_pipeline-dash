# Fossil Fuels Campaigner
Australian Marine Conservation Society
[Sydney NSW](https://au.seek.com/Campaigner-jobs/in-Sydney-NSW-2000) (Hybrid)
[Conservation, Parks & Wildlife (Farming, Animals & Conservation)](https://au.seek.com/jobs-in-farming-animals-conservation/conservation-parks-wildlife)

Posted 1d ago
[Quick apply](https://au.seek.com/job/91826671/apply)
Save
## **Join Australia’s leading ocean conservation organisation**
  * The Australian Marine Conservation Society (AMCS) is Australia’s voice for the ocean.
  * For more than 60 years, we’ve led campaigns to protect reefs, marine wildlife and coastlines.
  * We’re seeking a passionate and strategic Fossil Fuels Campaigner to help stop offshore fossil fuel exploration and development that threatens marine ecosystems, coastal communities and our climate.


## **The Opportunity**
  * Deliver outreach, organising and campaign initiatives that build public pressure and drive change.
  * Work with communities, Traditional Owners, allies, industry stakeholders and internal teams to grow momentum and deliver impactful campaigns.


## **What You’ll Be Doing**
  * Deliver campaign outreach and mobilisation activities across Australia
  * Build and strengthen relationships with community groups, partners and stakeholders
  * Support campaign strategy development and execution
  * Work with communications specialists to create compelling public-facing content
  * Coordinate actions, events and advocacy opportunities
  * Represent AMCS in meetings, forums and public settings


## **What You’ll Bring**
  * Experience in campaigning, organising, advocacy or alliance-building
  * Strong written and verbal communication skills
  * Confidence facilitating meetings, workshops or public speaking
  * Experience using digital tools and online platforms for engagement
  * Excellent stakeholder engagement and relationship-building skills
  * Understanding of climate, energy or environmental issues
  * Passion for ocean protection and environmental justice


## **Role Details**
  * Location: Ideally Sydney (Gadigal Country) or Melbourne (Wurundjeri and Boon Wurrung Country) based with hybrid working options
  * Employment Type: Full-time (38 hrs/week) | 2-year fixed-term contract
  * Part-time considered: 0.8 FTE (30.4 hrs/week)
  * Salary: $88,809 – $101,607 + 12.5% superannuation (pro rata if part-time)
  * Classification: Level 4 (aligned to SCHADS Award)


## **How to Apply**
  * Submit your CV / Resume and a cover letter (maximum 2 pages).
  * Applications close: 5:00pm AEST, 31 May 2026
  * Enquiries: 
  * Find out more about us https://www.marineconservation.org.au/

## **Join Australia’s leading ocean conservation organisation**
  * The Australian Marine Conservation Society (AMCS) is Australia’s voice for the ocean.
  * For more than 60 years, we’ve led campaigns to protect reefs, marine wildlife and coastlines.
  * We’re seeking a passionate and strategic Fossil Fuels Campaigner to help stop offshore fossil fuel exploration and development that threatens marine ecosystems, coastal communities and our climate.


## **The Opportunity**
  * Deliver outreach, organising and campaign initiatives that build public pressure and drive change.
  * Work with communities, Traditional Owners, allies, industry stakeholders and internal teams to grow momentum and deliver impactful campaigns.


## **What You’ll Be Doing**
  * Deliver campaign outreach and mobilisation activities across Australia
  * Build and strengthen relationships with community groups, partners and stakeholders
  * Support campaign strategy development and execution
  * Work with communications specialists to create compelling public-facing content
  * Coordinate actions, events and advocacy opportunities
  * Represent AMCS in meetings, forums and public settings


## **What You’ll Bring**
  * Experience in campaigning, organising, advocacy or alliance-building
  * Strong written and verbal communication skills
  * Confidence facilitating meetings, workshops or public speaking
  * Experience using digital tools and online platforms for engagement
  * Excellent stakeholder engagement and relationship-building skills
  * Understanding of climate, energy or environmental issues
  * Passion for ocean protection and environmental justice


## **Role Details**
  * Location: Ideally Sydney (Gadigal Country) or Melbourne (Wurundjeri and Boon Wurrung Country) based with hybrid working options
  * Employment Type: Full-time (38 hrs/week) | 2-year fixed-term contract
  * Part-time considered: 0.8 FTE (30.4 hrs/week)
  * Salary: $88,809 – $101,607 + 12.5% superannuation (pro rata if part-time)
  * Classification: Level 4 (aligned to SCHADS Award)


## **How to Apply**
  * Submit your CV / Resume and a cover letter (maximum 2 pages).
  * Applications close: 5:00pm AEST, 31 May 2026
  * Enquiries: 
  * Find out more about us https://www.marineconservation.org.au/

## **Join Australia’s leading ocean conservation organisation**
  * The Australian Marine Conservation Society (AMCS) is Australia’s voice for the ocean.
  * For more than 60 years, we’ve led campaigns to protect reefs, marine wildlife and coastlines.
  * We’re seeking a passionate and strategic Fossil Fuels Campaigner to help stop offshore fossil fuel exploration and development that threatens marine ecosystems, coastal communities and our climate.


## **The Opportunity**
  * Deliver outreach, organising and campaign initiatives that build public pressure and drive change.
  * Work with communities, Traditional Owners, allies, industry stakeholders and internal teams to grow momentum and deliver impactful campaigns.


## **What You’ll Be Doing**
  * Deliver campaign outreach and mobilisation activities across Australia
  * Build and strengthen relationships with community groups, partners and stakeholders
  * Support campaign strategy development and execution
  * Work with communications specialists to create compelling public-facing content
  * Coordinate actions, events and advocacy opportunities
  * Represent AMCS in meetings, forums and public settings


## **What You’ll Bring**
  * Experience in campaigning, organising, advocacy or alliance-building
  * Strong written and verbal communication skills
  * Confidence facilitating meetings, workshops or public speaking
  * Experience using digital tools and online platforms for engagement
  * Excellent stakeholder engagement and relationship-building skills
  * Understanding of climate, energy or environmental issues
  * Passion for ocean protection and environmental justice


## **Role Details**
  * Location: Ideally Sydney (Gadigal Country) or Melbourne (Wurundjeri and Boon Wurrung Country) based with hybrid working options
  * Employment Type: Full-time (38 hrs/week) | 2-year fixed-term contract
  * Part-time considered: 0.8 FTE (30.4 hrs/week)
  * Salary: $88,809 – $101,607 + 12.5% superannuation (pro rata if part-time)
