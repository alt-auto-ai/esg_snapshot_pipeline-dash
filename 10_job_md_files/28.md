### Assistant Director - Property Services
Adelaide SA
**Why YOU are important to us and what impact will you have on the People, Property and Security Division and the departments’ goal.**
  

We support our people across the employment life cycle, enabling the effective management of people-related risk and building future ready leaders, people and culture. We prevent, detect and respond to misconduct, fraud and corruption, deliver objective and risk-based internal audit, assurance and advisory services, and provide support to our two independent Inspectors-General. We ensure the effective management of our property portfolio, facilities management, security, sustainability, facilities emergency management, and a range of corporate site services and commercial contracts nationwide.
  

We provide a safe, reliable, and efficient environment that enables staff to work effectively. We are customer-focused, proactive, and dedicated to delivering quality services and solutions.
  

The Assistant Director – Property Services (South) leads a small, high-performing team delivering property and facilities services across the South Australian property portfolio. The role provides strategic and operational oversight of Property Service Provider performance, ensuring facilities management and accommodation services are delivered in accordance with Whole-of-Government Property Service Provider Arrangements. The position is also responsible for managing the department’s national fleet arrangement, local business continuity coordination, and related corporate services. Working closely with internal and external stakeholders, the Assistant Director supports implementation of the department’s strategic approach to accommodation utilisation, facilities services, and broader property management outcomes. This role is suited to an experienced property or facilities professional with strong leadership and stakeholder engagement capabilities, who can operate effectively in a dynamic environment. It offers the opportunity to influence service delivery outcomes and contribute to national programs. This recruitment activity will be used to establish a merit pool for an upcoming vacancy.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**This is where you play an important role.**
As the **Assistant Director - Property Services** , you will:
  

• support the department’s facilities management services, ensuring safety and compliance, and optimising occupancy
• oversee the delivery of preventative and reactive maintenance activities, including building services, inspections, and condition assessment reports
• work in collaboration with the National Capital Works team, and key stakeholders, to ensure projects are delivered through the department’s annual Property Capital Works Program
• undertake procurements of goods and services, in alignment with Commonwealth Procurement Rules
• manage delivery of departmental business functions provided to the regions
• provide strategic advice and assistance to the Senior Executives and stakeholders
• communicate and making balanced decisions using professional judgement, evaluating available information, factoring in risks and being sensitive to the context
• contribute to the identification, development, and implementation of opportunities to improve business management practices and processes 
• provide issue resolution and communication for internal and external stakeholders and escalate issues that have a wider departmental impact on staff or business continuity
• provide leadership to Property Services Branch staff, including coaching, capability development and problem solving. 
  

**Please note** that the merit pool established through this selection process, which is valid for a period of eighteen months from the date the vacancy was advertised in the Public Service Gazette, may also be used to fill future ongoing vacancies in the branch where the duties are of a similar nature.
#### Eligibility
Our ideal candidate will have an ability to:
  

• operate under broad direction, exercise a degree of independence, and perform a leadership role
• work positively and collaboratively, with a focus on customer service and maintaining open lines of communication
• demonstrate effective communication skills (oral, written, interpersonal and negotiation), including experience liaising with internal and external stakeholders
• work within established legislative and policy frameworks
• coordinate work, manage competing priorities and prioritise tasks to meet deadlines
• demonstrate analytical, interpretive and problem-solving skills
• employ well-developed interpersonal skills to manage and resolve situations requiring sensitivity to individual and/or workgroup concerns
• communicate in plain English, ensuring appropriate audience impact
• lead, coach and mentor staff.
  

You will also have a comprehensive understanding of:
  

• The Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013
• The Commonwealth Property Management Framework
• Construction Project Management
• Legislation and industry standards relating to property, particularly the Building Codes of Australia
• Facilities Management.
