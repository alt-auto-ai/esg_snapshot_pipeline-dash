### Senior Data and Research Analyst (Affirmative Measures)
Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC
**About the Role**
  

We are currently seeking a Senior Data and Research Analyst to join us in the Productivity Commission’s Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Stream.
  

As a member of our dedicated Reporting team, your work will contribute to progressing matters of importance that inform policy and improve outcomes for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people. This includes reporting on governments’ progress towards their commitments under the National Agreement on Closing the Gap and, developing and maintaining the [Closing the Gap Information Repository](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.pc.gov.au%2Fclosing-the-gap-data%2Fdashboard%2F).
#### The key duties of the position include:
Your ability to undertake research and data analysis specific to the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Stream, will be a key strength. You have strong conceptual and analytical skills and demonstrated experience working with data, preparing quality research papers, case studies and technical specifications. Essentially, you demonstrate and are committed to building meaningful relationships with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people as well as a working knowledge of Indigenous Data Sovereignty and Indigenous Data Governance. You also have knowledge of and understand First Nations cultures, histories and perspectives. 
  

An academic qualification in an area such as public policy, social policy, economics, law, statistics and data analysis, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander studies is desirable.
  

For comprehensive details about the role, responsibilities and selection criteria, please read the Position Description. We encourage applications from candidates that are passionate about the role, but may not meet all of the all the selection criteria. Transferable skills and experience will be highly regarded.
  

* A merit pool of suitable candidates may be established and used to fill similar vacancies, either on an ongoing or non-ongoing basis.
#### Eligibility
To be eligible for employment with the Productivity Commission, applicants must:

  * be willing to undergo a security and character clearance (which may include, but not limited to, completing an identity verification check, police check, and obtaining/maintaining an Australian Government security vetting clearance if required by the role)
  * provide evidence of mandatory qualifications.


#### Notes
To be eligible to apply for a position advertised under Affirmative Measure – Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander peoples, you will need to confirm all of the following: 
  

  * You are of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Australian descent. 
  * You identify as an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander Australian or both. 
  * You are accepted as an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander Australian. 


  

You can confirm your heritage with either a letter from an Indigenous organisation or Community Elder, or an appropriate statutory declaration.

#### Getting to know the Productivity Commission
The Productivity Commission is the Australian Government's independent research and advisory body on a range of economic, social and environmental issues affecting the welfare of Australians. Its role, expressed simply, is to help governments make better policies in the long-term interest of the Australian community.
