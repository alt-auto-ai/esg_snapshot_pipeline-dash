### EL1 Senior Data and Research Analyst (Identified)
Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC
**About the Role**
We are currently seeking a Senior Data and Research Analyst to join us in the Productivity Commission’s Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Stream.
  

As a member of our dedicated Reporting team, your work will contribute to progressing matters of importance that inform policy and improve outcomes for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people. This includes reporting on governments’ progress towards their commitments under the National Agreement on Closing the Gap and, developing and maintaining the [Closing the Gap Information Repository.](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.pc.gov.au%2Fclosing-the-gap-data%2Fdashboard)
#### The key duties of the position include:
Your ability to undertake research and data analysis specific to the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Stream, will be a key strength. You have strong conceptual and analytical skills and demonstrated experience working with data, preparing quality research papers, case studies and technical specifications.
  

Essentially, you demonstrate and are committed to building meaningful relationships with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people as well as a working knowledge of Indigenous Data Sovereignty and Indigenous Data Governance. You also have knowledge of and understand First Nations cultures, histories and perspectives. 
  

An academic qualification in an area such as public policy, social policy, economics, law, statistics and data analysis, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander studies is desirable.
  

Diversity and inclusion are valued at the Productivity Commission, and we welcome applications from people of all backgrounds. We particularly encourage applications from Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people, who bring significant capabilities and perspectives to our work. 
  

For comprehensive details about the role, responsibilities and selection criteria, please read the Position Description.
  

* A merit pool of suitable candidates may be established and used to fill similar vacancies, either on an ongoing or non-ongoing basis.

**Identified position**

#### Getting to know the Productivity Commission
The Productivity Commission is the Australian Government's independent research and advisory body on a range of economic, social and environmental issues affecting the welfare of Australians. Its role, expressed simply, is to help governments make better policies in the long-term interest of the Australian community.
