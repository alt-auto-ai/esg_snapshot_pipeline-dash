Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water (DCCEEW)
### Fire Management Coordinator - Identified
Kakadu NT
**Identified position**
This position is an ‘Identified’ position which signifies that the role has a strong involvement in issues relating to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people. The successful applicant must have an understanding of the issues affecting Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander people and an ability to communicate sensitively and effectively with Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander People.
  

**Who we are**
Australia’s natural and cultural heritage is unique. Our land and seascapes are distinctive, home to plants and animals found nowhere else in the world, and to some of the oldest living cultures on earth. These environments and cultures are an essential part of our national identity, and visitors travel from across the country and the world to experience them. The Director of National Parks (the Director) is responsible for six national parks, the Australian National Botanic Gardens, 58 Australian Marine Parks and the Heard Island and McDonald Islands Marine Reserve established under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999. Parks Australia is the federal park agency that supports the Director, and they are a division of the Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water (the Department). We work to showcase these natural and cultural wonders to the world, demonstrating to all why these places are so special, and inspiring communities to become more invested in their care and future.
  

Kakadu National Park is a world-heritage listed site for its natural and cultural values, and one of three national parks, leased by their Aboriginal owners to the Director of National Parks, and jointly managed by Traditional Owners and Parks Australia. Parks Australia assists with ongoing management of the park’s natural and cultural heritage. In addition to ongoing management of the park’s natural and cultural heritage, this branch also manages strategic priorities for Parks Australia, managing a range of strategic planning and major capital projects across the three jointly managed parks.
  

The Country Section is responsible for the protection of the Kakadu National Park’s biodiversity assets and natural values. The section does this by providing strategic advice and support to the operations section on fire, feral and weed management, managing Country through a significant fire program; implementing and assisting significant species projects; responding to the threat of climate change; data and knowledge management; and planning. The section is an important part of creating an inclusive cross-cultural working environment. For more information about us please visit our website.
  

For more information about us please visit our [website](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.dcceew.gov.au%2F).
#### The key duties of the position include:
Under limited direction, and in partnership with Traditional Owners, Rangers and other park staff, lead the planning, delivery and continuous improvement of biodiversity and culturally focused fire management activities across Kakadu National Park. The role provides specialist operational and technical advice to the Country Manager, Park Manager and broader park leadership to support risk-based decision-making, effective stakeholder engagement and delivery of the park’s fire management objectives.
This includes to: 
  * Lead and coordinate consultation with Traditional Owners and relevant stakeholders to develop, implement and report on annual fire plans, operational burn programs and late dry season response plans (including tools and briefings). 
  * Plan, lead and quality-assure strategic prescribed burning programs (wet season and early dry season), including aerial and ground ignition, ensuring appropriate risk assessments, permits/approvals, resourcing and safety controls are in place and applied. 
  * Monitor, interpret and communicate late dry season fire activity using web-based tools ([https://e.g.,](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https://e.g.,) NAFI) and other data sources, providing timely advice to support operational decisions and escalation. 
  * Lead and coordinate elements of late dry season preparedness, response and suppression activities across park districts, including liaison with senior staff and partner agencies, and contributing to incident management arrangements as required. 
  * Maintain and oversee the integrity of fire management spatial and operational datasets (GPS/ArcGIS and relevant systems), ensuring appropriate records management, version control and reporting outputs. 
  * Prepare and coordinate annual monitoring, evaluation and performance reporting for the fire program and intersecting carbon credit schemes, including analysis of spatial and operational data and recommendations to improve outcomes. 
  * Coordinate and contribute to research and monitoring activities relevant to fire management, ensuring findings are documented and translated into practical improvements to planning and operations. 
  * Lead contributions to on-park planning and governance forums to review and update the park’s Fire Management Strategy and supporting plans, procedures and operational guidance.
  * Provide day-to-day leadership and culturally safe supervision for staff, including allocating work, supporting performance and wellbeing, and encouraging Traditional Owners and other relevant Aboriginal people to participate in all aspects of fire management. 
  * Support implementation of Traditional Owners’ vision for the park by facilitating two-way knowledge exchange between Indigenous and non-Indigenous staff and ensuring cultural considerations are embedded in operational planning and delivery. 
  * Mentor, coach and train Rangers, junior staff and seasonal personnel to build fire management capability, safe work practices and effective use of equipment and resources, including through on-the-job instruction and structured learning opportunities. 
  * Model and promote safe working practices and actively manage WHS risks in accordance with the Work Health and Safety Act 2011 and relevant departmental and park policies, including reporting hazards and incidents and supporting corrective actions.
  * All duties will be performed in accordance with the Park’s Lease Agreement, the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 (EPBC Act) and Management Plan.

#### Eligibility
**Citizenship -** to be eligible for employment with the Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water you must be an Australian citizen.
  

**Security Clearance -** this position requires a Baseline Vetting security clearance. You will be required to obtain and maintain a clearance at this level. 
  

**Pre-employment checks -** your suitability for employment will be assessed through a pre-employment screening process. This process includes a requirement to undergo and satisfy a National Police Check, referee checks, character clearance and where required a pre-employment medical assessment, specified mandatory qualification(s) validation and a probation period. 
  

**Mandatory qualifications**
**The successful applicant must possess the below units of competency or have the ability to obtain them.**
  * AHCFIR501 - Manage wildfire hazard reduction programs.
  * AHCFIR502 - Plan prescribed burning for fuel, ecological and cultural resource management.
  * PUASS00080 – Prescribed Burn Crew Leader.
  * PUASS00067 - Complex Prescribed Burn Officer.
  * PUASS00068 – Complex Prescribed Burn Planner.
  * PUAFIR008 - Operate aerial ignition equipment in an aircraft.


  

**Additional requirements**

  * hold and maintain a current manual driver’s licence and ability to drive a 4WD vehicle,
  * meet physical fitness minimum standards to undertake work within the role including firefighting as needed,
  * note that relocation assistance is subject to negotiation,
  * note that the ability to perform shift work is a requirement when applying for this position(s),
  * note that the physical requirements of the role(s) involve a combination of outdoor and office-based work,

  * wear appropriate protective personal equipment and clothing to comply with departmental Work Health & Safety policies and procedures
