Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water (DCCEEW)
### Assistant Director - Regulatory Support
Various locations - ACT ACT, Various locations - NSW NSW, Various locations - NT NT, Various locations - QLD QLD, Various locations - SA SA, Various locations - TAS TAS, Various locations - VIC VIC, Various locations - WA WA
**Who we are**
  

Australia’s natural and cultural heritage is unique. Our land and seascapes are distinctive, home to plants and animals found nowhere else in the world, and to some of the oldest living cultures on earth. These environments and cultures are an essential part of our national identity, and visitors travel from across the country and the world to experience them. 
  

The Director of National Parks (the Director) is responsible for six national parks, the Australian National Botanic Gardens, 58 Australian Marine Parks and the Heard Island and McDonald Islands Marine Reserve established under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999. Parks Australia is the federal park agency that supports the Director, and they are a division of the Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water (the Department). We work to showcase these natural and cultural wonders to the world, demonstrating to all why these places are so special, and inspiring communities to become more invested in their care and future.
  

The Strategy and Indigenous Protected Areas Branch provides strategic oversight and policy support for managing Australia’s Commonwealth parks and reserves and Indigenous Protected Areas, with a strong focus on supporting Indigenous land management and protection of cultural heritage. It works to improve legislative and regulatory frameworks, support cultural and environmental stewardship, and foster partnerships with Indigenous communities to ensure sustainable and transparent outcomes across Commonwealth protected areas.
  

The Legislation Policy and Regulatory Support Section is responsible for leading Parks Australia legislation and joint management reform and providing regulation and compliance support for terrestrial and island parks, to support regulatory maturity and best practice. The section is comprised of three teams that work collaboratively to provide strategic oversight, policy development and capability support for the management of Australia’s Commonwealth Reserves. 
  

  * The legislation policy team leads the development of the policy settings to support the expectations of Traditional Owners and stakeholders in legislation and joint management reform, in association with the broader reform agenda for the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999. 
  * The compliance support team leads the development of park-specific compliance plans and risk assessments, policies and procedures for best practice compliance approaches, and delivers on-park training to wardens and rangers. 
  * The regulatory support team supports the development of consistent and best practice regulatory approaches to authorisations and permits across terrestrial, and island parks and supports the development of systems and policies to support effective regulatory practices. 


  

The section also administers, on behalf of the Department, the permitting regime for access to biological resources in Commonwealth areas. 
  

Our team has a strong focus on enabling the achievement of shared goals through collaboration, relationship building and a strong commitment to continual improvement. As a geographically hybrid team we prioritise maintaining strong team cohesion and accountability while supporting individual preferences and needs.
#### The key duties of the position include:
Under broad direction from the Director (Legislation Policy and Regulatory Support), lead a small team to contribute to supporting regulatory maturity and best practice approaches to regulation with Parks Australia. This will include establishing and driving a work program that provides policy advice on the Director’s regulatory responsibilities to multiple audiences, deliver support to meet regulatory best practice, and develop consistent policy, procedures, systems and processes for regulatory practices.
  

Your responsibilities will include: 
  

  * Research and analysis, and development of policy advice, on matters related to legislation and environmental regulation. 
  * Building strong partnerships and coordinating with internal and external stakeholders to support policy development and support systems. 
  * Collaborating with Traditional Owners and Parks Australia staff on regulatory improvement. 
  * Developing and implementing policies, guidelines, systems and procedures that support regulatory best practice. 
  * Developing documents such as reports, briefs, ministerial correspondence and talking points to provide high quality advice to the Senior Executive and the Minister. 
  * Assessment of applications for permits and other approvals under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 and regulations. 
  * Preparation and monitoring of project documentation. 
  * Contributing to a respectful, open and high-performing team culture.
