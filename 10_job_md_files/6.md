Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry
### M365 Collaboration Analyst
Canberra ACT
**Why YOU are important to us and what impact will you have on the ICT Services Division and the departments’ goal.**
  

The **ICT Services Division** delivers secure and modern technology solutions that enable the department to operate effectively 24/7. We manage ICT systems that support collaboration, innovation, and service delivery across Australia. Our goal is to provide a reliable ICT environment that empowers staff to focus on outcomes, and operational excellence. By continuously improving our platforms and tools, we help the department stay connected, and future ready.
  

We manage the ICT platforms and systems that support the department’s core operations. Our team ensures these platforms are secure, scalable, and designed for long-term sustainability. We support innovation through DevOps practices and modern testing approaches, enabling faster and more reliable delivery of digital solutions. From Information Management to ERP Systems, and Web and Collaboration systems, we provide the digital foundation that helps staff work efficiently and deliver services that support the department.
  

As the **M365 Collaboration Analyst** , you will play a key hands-on role supporting and improving the department’s Microsoft 365 collaboration environment. Working closely with experienced team members, you will help support platforms such as Microsoft Teams, SharePoint Online and Microsoft Copilot, contribute to service improvements, and assist in delivering practical solutions for users across the department. This role is well suited to someone who enjoys problem-solving, learning new technologies, and working collaboratively in a supportive technical team.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**This is where you play an important role.**
  

As the M365 Collaboration Analyst, you will:
  * provide technical support, problem solving, advice and training to users of Microsoft Teams, SharePoint Online and Microsoft Copilot
  * assist with monitoring service dashboards and task queues, supporting the timely resolution of service requests and incidents
  * contribute to the development and maintenance of standard operating procedures, user guidance and administrative documentation
  * support continuous improvement activities across collaboration platforms to improve reliability, usability and user experience
  * work with internal stakeholders and suppliers to support the delivery of small projects and enhancements, including testing and implementation
  * share knowledge with team members and contribute to a positive team culture that supports learning and capability development


  

**Note:** The merit pool established through this selection process, which is valid for a period of eighteen months from the date the vacancy was advertised in the Public Service Gazette, may also be used to fill future ongoing vacancies in the branch where the duties are of a similar nature.
#### Eligibility
**Our ideal candidate will have:**
  * Demonstrated experience supporting and administering Microsoft 365 collaboration tools, such as Microsoft Teams, SharePoint Online and/or Copilot.
  * Strong analytical and problem-solving capability, with the ability to investigate issues, identify opportunities, and propose practical solutions.
  * Proven ability to manage competing priorities and deliver high-quality outcomes within agreed timeframes in a service delivery environment.
  * Clear and confident communicator, able to explain technical concepts in plain English to a range of technical and non-technical stakeholders, both verbally and in writing.
  * Works collaboratively, shares knowledge, seeks feedback, and demonstrates initiative, resilience and a willingness to learn new tools and technologies.


RecruitAbility applies to this vacancy. Under the RecruitAbility scheme you will be invited to participate in further assessment activity for the vacancy if you choose to apply under the scheme; declare you have a disability; and meet the minimum requirements for the job. For more information visit: This vacancy has been identified to participate in VetPaths.
