### Agency Security Officer
Belconnen ACT, Melbourne VIC, Pyrmont NSW
The Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA) is an independent Commonwealth statutory authority responsible for the regulation of broadcasting, radiocommunications, telecommunications and some online content.
  

This work underpins our vision for a connected, informed and entertained Australia.
  

  

**Who we are**
  

The **Corporate and Research Division** partners with business areas across the ACMA, to provide enterprise wide enabling services and strategic advice so that the ACMA can achieve its purpose. The Division facilitates this through the delivery of key corporate functions including financial management, property services, security, procurement advice, performance reporting, research and data, digital and technology services, human resources, communications, and governance services. 
  

The**Finance, Reporting and Operations Branch** is responsible for ensuring the financial sustainability and security of the ACMA through financial, procurement, facilities, travel and physical security services. The Branch proactively engages with business areas to provide advice, and financial and business performance reporting to drive strategic decision making and support the broader organisation. The Branch also provides assurance over the various revenue streams collected on behalf of the Commonwealth and leads the development of a range of budget and financial governance arrangements.
  

The **Facilities, Procurement and Security Section** provide expert advice and support to the agency and its staff across a wide range of services, issues and processes including: 
  * management of the ACMA’s and eSafety’s procurement and ensures compliance with the Commonwealth Procurement Rules and the PGPA Act 
  * management of the ACMA’s portfolio of offices and other properties 
  * provision of facilities services to enable the operation of the ACMA work environment 
  * advice and documentation to ensure that the ACMA complies with best procurement practice and Commonwealth Procurement Rules 
  * managing the ACMA’s protective security framework, other than ICT security 
  * providing support, education, and training to staff on Contract, Procurement and tendering processes 
  * provision of high-quality contract and procurement specialist advice to the agency and provide leadership across the team and Branch. 


  

  

**About the role**
  

The **Agency Security Officer** , under the direction of the Agency Security Advisor, is responsible for protecting personnel, information, and assets by identifying, assessing, and managing security risks and threats specific to eSafety.
  

The role also supports compliance with the Protective Security Policy Framework (PSPF), including reporting on personnel and physical security, and assists in the coordination, implementation, and testing of emergency management procedures. This includes ensuring that security policies, procedures, and documentation remain current and aligned with government and legislative requirements.
  

In addition, the position is responsible for administering and managing security clearance processes, including handling clearance applications, monitoring ongoing suitability, and liaising with stakeholders under the direction of the ACMA Agency Security Advisor to support a secure operating environment.
  

#### The key duties of the position include:
  * Assessing and managing threats to personnel, assets, and workplaces.
  * Liaising with law enforcement, intelligence agencies, and other relevant authorities.
  * Managing and administering security clearance processes.
  * Providing security advice, briefings, and incident reporting.
  * Supporting PSPF compliance and reporting requirements.
  * Coordinating emergency management procedures and exercises.
  * Maintaining and updating security policies, procedures, and documentation.
  * Building and maintaining effective relationships with internal and external stakeholders and service providers.
  * Contributing to process improvement and continuous enhancement of security practices.

**The Role**
We are seeking dynamic, solutions focused individuals to join our high performing teams as Base Support Officers and Estate Management & Planning Officers, roles central to keeping Defence sites safe, secure, and effective. As a Base Support Officer, you will excel in stakeholder engagement, anticipate and coordinate base services, and support facilities maintenance, asset improvement, and operational integration. You will build strong relationships across military, civilian, and contractor groups, contributing to infrastructure management, safety, security, and emergency response while supporting daily operations and planning programs.
  

As an Estate Management and Planning Officer, you will play a key role in ensuring the Defence estate is planned, managed, and maintained to meet safety, compliance, and sustainability requirements. You will support infrastructure projects, advise on risks and future needs, contribute to planning frameworks, and work with contractors and planners to deliver strategic estate outcomes in a collaborative environment.
  

**About our Team**
Defence’s primary mission is to protect Australia and its national interests, promote security and stability in the world and support the Australian community as directed by the Government. 
  

The South East Zone is one of three zones within the Service Delivery Division that provide essential, on-the-ground services and support to Defence personnel around Australia. We enable Defence personnel to achieve their mission through delivery of excellent services on Defence Bases and we play a role in helping the Government achieve its strategic objectives.
  

One of the best things about our team is the variety of our work; every day is different, with new challenges to solve and interesting work crossing our desks. To achieve the level of service delivery needed, we work collaboratively with our Defence colleagues and industry partners. Our team works with representatives from industry to foster creativity and explore ideas that improve service delivery for Defence bases.
  

**Our Ideal Candidate**
We are seeking adaptable, solutions focused individuals who remain calm under pressure and demonstrate a strong customer service mindset. A quick thinker, capable of working collaboratively across a range of military, civilian, and contractor stakeholders, while also taking ownership of your responsibilities. The ideal candidate is proactive in managing risks and safety, comfortable in an operational environment, and ready to respond effectively to incidents or emerging issues.
  

Successful applicants will have a proven ability to achieve organisational goals while contributing to a high performance service delivery culture. You will bring well developed communication, organisation, and negotiation skills, along with the ability to influence stakeholders and adapt positively to change. Strong prioritisation skills and the ability to interpret policy into practical outcomes are essential. Most importantly, we are seeking someone with a positive attitude, strong team focus, and the ability to navigate the stresses of a busy role with good humour.
