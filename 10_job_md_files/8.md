### Assistant Director, Technical Business Analyst
Adelaide SA, Brisbane QLD, Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC, Sydney NSW
  * **Do you want to work at an agency where each day reveals new opportunities to work on some of the biggest issues facing consumers and businesses and have opportunities to pursue new and exciting pathways?**
  * **Do you want to work at a place where smart and dedicated people from all walks of life collaborate to work on meaningful matters?**
  * **Do you want to work in a high performing cross-functional team on Energy Made Easy and Energy Plan Connect, delivering better outcomes for Australian energy consumers?**


  

**Then this is the place for you!** At the AER, we work to make energy consumers better off, now and in the future. We focus on ensuring a secure, reliable and affordable energy future for Australia as it transitions to net zero emissions. Our high performing culture is built on a foundation of care, support and inclusion. It’s why we’re driven by connection and collaboration, sharing our skills, knowledge and support with each other freely and frequently. It’s why we prioritise the wellbeing of our people and empower them to work autonomously, flexibly and courageously. We believe the best version of you is the one that’s true to who you are – and that’s the you we want working alongside us.
  

We value flexibility, this role can be performed at any of the AER offices located in Adelaide, Brisbane, Canberra, Melbourne, or Sydney and are available on a full-time, part-time or job-share basis.
  

**Applicants from diverse backgrounds including First Nations peoples, people with disability and those from different cultural backgrounds are encouraged to apply.**
#### The key duties of the position include:
  * Collaborate with internal and external stakeholders to understand their drivers, goals and objectives, and effectively manage their requirements and expectations.
  * Elicit, document and gain approval of business processes and requirements.
  * Perform gap analysis to identify differences between current state and desired future state.
  * Perform options analysis to bridge identified gaps and present potential solutions for assessment and selection.
  * Translate business requirements to user stories, acceptance criteria, technical specifications and test cases.
  * Support user acceptance testing (UAT) and manage defects through to resolution.
  * Identify and manage risks and issues related to initiative and project objectives and delivery.
  * Assist in project closure by overseeing acceptance, transition to routine operations, ROI achievement, and lessons learned.


#### Eligibility
In order to succeed in the role, you will have:
  * Proven experience as a Technical Business Analyst or similar role working on complex technology projects.
  * The ability to independently manage and complete multiple large projects on schedule, demonstrating initiative and adapting effectively to changes.
  * Experience in applying structured analysis techniques and critical thinking to form views and provide advice for effective evidence-based decision making.
  * The capability to understand and apply complex policy and technical concepts to IT solution design, development, and subsequent implementation activities.
  * The proficiency to identify, assess and manage risks, including implementing appropriate mitigation strategies.
  * Clear, concise and articulate oral and written communication with a commitment to achieving high quality outcomes.
  * A proven ability to engage effectively and work cohesively with stakeholders, and as part of a geographically dispersed team.
  * A customer focus to ensure our products meet end user needs and requirements.
  * Experience with Agile Project Methodology, with a preference for experience using Atlassian Service Suite.
  * A well-developed understanding, or ability to quickly acquire an understanding of the Australian energy sector and the role, purpose and objectives of the AER.

#### Getting to know the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission
The ACCC is an independent statutory authority that administers the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 and other Acts. The ACCC works to promote effective competition and fair trading in the market place to benefit consumers, business and the community, and efficiency in the delivery of certain infrastructure services. The AER is a part of the ACCC. The AER is Australia's national energy market regulator and has an independent board. The AER shares employees, resources and facilities with the ACCC.
