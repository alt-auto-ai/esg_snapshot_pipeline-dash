**About BHP**
At BHP we support our people to grow, learn, develop their skills and reach their potential. With a global portfolio of operations, we offer a diverse and inclusive environment with extraordinary career opportunities. Our strategy is to focus on creating a safe work environment where our employees feel strongly connected to our values and objectives, and where the capability of our people is key to our success.
Come and be a part of this success.﻿
  
  

﻿**About the Role**
This **Specialist Environment** role is required to deliver environmental functional expertise for Copper South Australia to successfully meet environmental regulatory and reporting requirements to enable business continuity and maintain our Licence to Operate. Key to this role will be - supporting the delivery of the Environment components of the Integrated Management System, environmental reporting, assessments and site monitoring activities as required, providing specialist advice on environmental regulatory matters and supporting key stakeholders in providing, and analysing environmental data to meet reporting obligations.
The role will be a permanent opportunity within the Planning, Technical & Environment team **based in Adelaide on a 5/2 roster with regular travel to site**.
**Roles and Responsibilities:**
  * Comply to and consistently follow the standards and procedures designed to enable you to work safely and effectively every day
  * Environmental Reporting to meet regulatory and BHP requirements
  * Support the development and operation of the EMS for CuSA
  * Support operational site compliance and monitoring activities for CuSA
  * Developing work instructions and databases to effectively manage environmental procedures and data
  * Manage, maintain and update GIS and G-LUP data when required.
  * Analyse environmental data and processes to identify and improve systems, process and procedures.
  * Undertake assigned tasks in accordance with 1SAP Work Management process
  * Demonstrate an overriding commitment to health, safety, environmental responsibility and sustainable development.
  * Identify and implement opportunities to improve productivity, efficiency and cost effectiveness.
  * Participate in workplace investigations as required.
  * Maintain strong working relationship with relevant external and internal stakeholders to achieve required environmental outcomes.

**About You**
  * Proven commitment to look after your own wellbeing and the confidence to speak up when others put themselves in harm's way
  * Adhere to and demonstrate BHP Charter Values.
  * Tertiary qualification in Environmental Science, Engineering, or equivalent
  * Professional with a minimum 3 years' experience in environmental science/management related work
  * Ability to analyse environmental data
  * Proficiency in the use of ESRI ArcGIS and spatial data management.
  * Ecology / botany experience will be highly regarded


**Applications close 5pm Wednesday 25th March 2026.**

These benefits include flexible working options, a generous paid parental leave policy, other extended leave entitlements and parent rooms.

Our strategy is to focus on creating a safe work environment where our employees feel strongly connected to our values and objectives, and where the capability of our people is key to our success.
Come and be a part of this success.﻿
  
  

﻿**About the Role**
This **Specialist Environment** role is required to deliver environmental functional expertise for Copper South Australia to successfully meet environmental regulatory and reporting requirements to enable business continuity and maintain our Licence to Operate. Key to this role will be - supporting the delivery of the Environment components of the Integrated Management System, environmental reporting, assessments and site monitoring activities as required, providing specialist advice on environmental regulatory matters and supporting key stakeholders in providing, and analysing environmental data to meet reporting obligations.
The role will be a permanent opportunity within the Planning, Technical & Environment team **based in Adelaide on a 5/2 roster with regular travel to site**.
**Roles and Responsibilities:**
  * Comply to and consistently follow the standards and procedures designed to enable you to work safely and effectively every day
  * Environmental Reporting to meet regulatory and BHP requirements
  * Support the development and operation of the EMS for CuSA
  * Support operational site compliance and monitoring activities for CuSA
  * Developing work instructions and databases to effectively manage environmental procedures and data
  * Manage, maintain and update GIS and G-LUP data when required.
  * Analyse environmental data and processes to identify and improve systems, process and procedures.
  * Undertake assigned tasks in accordance with 1SAP Work Management process
  * Demonstrate an overriding commitment to health, safety, environmental responsibility and sustainable development.
  * Identify and implement opportunities to improve productivity, efficiency and cost effectiveness.
  * Participate in workplace investigations as required.
  * Maintain strong working relationship with relevant external and internal stakeholders to achieve required environmental outcomes.

**About You**
  * Proven commitment to look after your own wellbeing and the confidence to speak up when others put themselves in harm's way
  * Adhere to and demonstrate BHP Charter Values.
  * Tertiary qualification in Environmental Science, Engineering, or equivalent
  * Professional with a minimum 3 years' experience in environmental science/management related work
  * Ability to analyse environmental data
  * Proficiency in the use of ESRI ArcGIS and spatial data management.
  * Ecology / botany experience will be highly regarded

**About BHP**
At BHP we support our people to grow, learn, develop their skills and reach their potential. With a global portfolio of operations, we offer a diverse and inclusive environment with extraordinary career opportunities. Our strategy is to focus on creating a safe work environment where our employees feel strongly connected to our values and objectives, and where the capability of our people is key to our success.
Come and be a part of this success.﻿
  
  

﻿**About the Role**
This **Specialist Environment** role is required to deliver environmental functional expertise for Copper South Australia to successfully meet environmental regulatory and reporting requirements to enable business continuity and maintain our Licence to Operate. Key to this role will be - supporting the delivery of the Environment components of the Integrated Management System, environmental reporting, assessments and site monitoring activities as required, providing specialist advice on environmental regulatory matters and supporting key stakeholders in providing, and analysing environmental data to meet reporting obligations.
The role will be a permanent opportunity within the Planning, Technical & Environment team **based in Adelaide on a 5/2 roster with regular travel to site**.
**Roles and Responsibilities:**
  * Comply to and consistently follow the standards and procedures designed to enable you to work safely and effectively every day
  * Environmental Reporting to meet regulatory and BHP requirements
  * Support the development and operation of the EMS for CuSA
  * Support operational site compliance and monitoring activities for CuSA
  * Developing work instructions and databases to effectively manage environmental procedures and data
  * Manage, maintain and update GIS and G-LUP data when required.
  * Analyse environmental data and processes to identify and improve systems, process and procedures.
  * Undertake assigned tasks in accordance with 1SAP Work Management process
  * Demonstrate an overriding commitment to health, safety, environmental responsibility and sustainable development.
  * Identify and implement opportunities to improve productivity, efficiency and cost effectiveness.
  * Participate in workplace investigations as required.
  * Maintain strong working relationship with relevant external and internal stakeholders to achieve required environmental outcomes.

**About You**
  * Proven commitment to look after your own wellbeing and the confidence to speak up when others put themselves in harm's way
  * Adhere to and demonstrate BHP Charter Values.
  * Tertiary qualification in Environmental Science, Engineering, or equivalent
  * Professional with a minimum 3 years' experience in environmental science/management related work
  * Ability to analyse environmental data
  * Proficiency in the use of ESRI ArcGIS and spatial data management.
  * Ecology / botany experience will be highly regarded
