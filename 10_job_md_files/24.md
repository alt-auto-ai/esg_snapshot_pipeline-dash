### Property Services Manager
Sydney NSW
**Why YOU are important to us and what impact will you have on the People, Property and Security Division and the departments’ goal.**
We support our people across the employment life cycle, enabling the effective management of people-related risk and building future ready leaders, people and culture. We prevent, detect and respond to misconduct, fraud and corruption, deliver objective and risk-based internal audit, assurance and advisory services, and provide support to our two independent Inspectors-General. We ensure the effective management of our property portfolio, facilities management, security, sustainability, facilities emergency management and a range of corporate site services and commercial contracts nationwide.
  

We provide a safe, reliable, and efficient environment that enables staff to work effectively. We are customer-focused, proactive and dedicated to delivering quality services and solutions.
  

The Property Services Manager role is responsible for delivery of the department’s strategic approach to the ongoing management of accommodation use and facilities services for the department’s property portfolio which includes office space, laboratories, quarantine facilities, air and sea ports and detector dog facilities. The role has specific responsibility for supporting the Sydney Regional Office and the Western Sydney International Airport, ensuring facilities and accommodation arrangements at these sites are safe, compliant, fit-for-purpose and aligned to operational requirements.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**This is where you play an important role.**
As the Property Services Manager you will:
  

• support the department’s facilities management services, ensuring safety and compliance and optimising occupancy
• oversee development and delivery of preventative maintenance for the department’s sites including building services, inspections and condition assessment reports
• support capital works project delivery to time, budget and stakeholder requirements
• support departmental business functions provided to the regions
• undertake procurements of goods and services in alignment with Commonwealth Procurement Rules
• engage and collaborate with stakeholders to achieve required outcomes, support positive relationships and ensure communication of upcoming works or procedures
• manage the transition and handover of project and business-as-usual activities
• actively participate in relevant governance meetings and represent the department during working group meetings
• provide issue resolution and communication for internal and external stakeholders and escalate issues that have a wider departmental impact on staff or business continuity
• provide leadership to staff including coaching, capability development and assisting with problem solving/resolution.
  

**Please note** that this recruitment process will **initially be used to fill one ongoing vacancy in Sydney**. The merit pool established through this selection process, which is valid for a period of eighteen months from the date the vacancy was advertised in the Public Service Gazette, may be used to fill future ongoing vacancies in the branch where the duties are of a similar nature.
#### Eligibility
**Who we are looking for**
**_﻿Knowledge and experience_**
The knowledge and experience required for the role includes:
  

• a demonstrated understanding of the _Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013_
• an understanding of legislation and industry standards relating to property, particularly the Building Codes of Australia
• previous experience in facilities management.
  

**Eligibility and other requirements**
The successful applicant must:
  

• hold and maintain a valid Australian motor vehicle drivers licence
• obtain and maintain an Aviation Security Identification Card (ASIC) and a Maritime Security Identification Card (MSIC).
