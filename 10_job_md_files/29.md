Australian Radiation Protection and Nuclear Safety Agency
### Director of Finance (CFO)
Yallambie VIC
The Australian Radiation Protection and Nuclear Safety Agency (ARPANSA) is the Australian Government's primary authority on radiation protection and nuclear safety. Our purpose is to protect the Australian people and the environment from the harmful effects of radiation, through understanding risks, best practice regulation, research, policy, services, partnerships and engaging with the community.
  

The Finance section is responsible for ARPANSA’s financial reporting, financial service operations, travel management and procurement functions and for developing and maintaining robust financial systems that provide high-quality financial information that supports the Agency’s strategic objectives. The section ensures the accuracy of the Agency’s official accounting records; produces timely financial performance reports; manages internal controls to mitigate risk; and safeguards the Agency’s assets.
  

**Why work for us?**
  * Opportunities to engage with national and international stakeholders
  * Inclusive and diverse workplace
  * Competitive salaries with 15.4% superannuation
  * Generous leave conditions
  * Flexible work practices
  * Modern amenities and free parking at both our Yallambie and Miranda sites.


#### The key duties of the position include:
**Primary purpose of the role**
Oversee the finance management functions of the agency and provide input to strategic decision-making processes that have a financial impact. Reporting directly to the Head of OBS, the role will contribute to the overall financial sustainability of the agency by providing insights and recommendations on various aspects of the agency’s operations including efficiency reviews and keeping the currency of our cost-recovery and fee-for-services arrangements. 
  

Lead the oversight of various projects, funding applications, review of ARPANSA’s business models, and management of the agency’s operational and capital budgets. Provide financial advice to the Executive Group in developing the agency's future strategic direction and maintaining cost recovery through robust costing and pricing models.
  

To collaborate closely with internal and external stakeholders to implement financial strategies, deliver detailed financial reports, manage people, and conduct high-level operational reviews of Offices and Branches. This role is pivotal in enhancing the agency's financial performance, stability, and strength.
  

**Key accountabilities**
Leadership and Advisory:
  * Lead and manage the agency’s finance function, including finance policies, annual financial statements and external audit process, budgeting, procurement, risk, property valuations, meeting reporting requirements, and financial systems. This will include, building capability through coaching others, providing performance feedback and encouraging development.
  * Provide high-level advice and support to the CEO and the Executive Leadership Group, including development of annual operating and capital expenditure budgets, monthly and long-term forecasting, cost recovery arrangements and preparation of complex reports, reliable advice and insightful analysis to inform the agency’s strategic direction and deliver optimal performance outcomes; 
  * Provide strategic and operational leadership for the effective and efficient management of agency financial performance in alignment with Commonwealth Government policies and guidelines;
  * Partner with the Executive Group and relevant Government Departments/Agencies to develop ARPANSA’s financial and sustainability framework, including the development and support of projects that lead to enduring change in the agency; 
  * Promote a culture of high performance and continual improvement within ARPANSA through the provision of expert advice and creating positive working relationships by honouring the APS Values;


Financial Planning, Reporting and Analysis:
  * Develop and implement financial strategies and plans to maintain and improve agency’s overall financial sustainability. 
  * Conduct financial scenario analysis, including revenue forecasting, pricing analysis and reviews, sustainability assessment, and cost management.
  * Prepare accurate monthly and quarterly financial reports and annual financial statements, performance dashboards and presentations for executive group, audit and risk committee and external stakeholders in accordance with Australian Accounting Standards, Government accounting policy and regulatory and statutory requirements.
  * Provide strategic insights and recommendations on pricing strategies, product/service positioning, and market expansion in line with the Australian Government’s pricing framework.


Financial Risk Management:
  * Assess and mitigate financial risks associated with business activities, such as sustainability risk, customer concentration, and market volatility.
  * Provide review and input into ARPANSA’s project management framework and advisory group with respect to financial aspects of agency projects. 
  * Maintain and improve financial controls and compliance frameworks to ensure adherence to regulatory requirements.
  * Monitor key financial metrics and indicators to identify potential risks and recommend appropriate actions.


Stakeholder Engagement:
  * Build strong relationships with internal stakeholders, including executive group, business unit leaders, and other teams within the agency, to improve commercial acumen and provide business partnering services.
  * Engage with external stakeholders, including portfolio department, internal and external auditors.


#### Eligibility
To be considered, you must be a qualified Accountant and have extensive financial management experience (CPA/CA) preferably working as CFO or in a senior financial management role within the Australian Public Service. You must be comfortable with driving systems and process improvements in a business that will continue to change at a measured pace. 
  

We are looking for a motivated individual with a “can do attitude”, with highly developed interpersonal skills, and a proven ability to build positive working relationships across all organisational levels, both internally and externally. You will be an experienced finance professional with strong leadership capability gained within the Australian Public Service or in a similar organisational or agency context. 
  

You will have proven knowledge and extensive experience in successfully managing contemporary approaches to Finance, including an understanding of Commonwealth regulatory and legislative frameworks. You will have demonstrated the ability to proactively identify priorities and take ownership for the delivery of projects and other tasks.
  

You will also demonstrate strong analytical and problem-solving abilities to assist managers and staff across the agency to strengthen their commercial acumen, leveraging excellent communication and presentation skills, with the ability to convey financial information effectively to both financial and non-financial stakeholders.
  

You are a skilled influencer and communicator highly regarded for your ability to lead people, manage change and resources, and deliver results. You will be able to think creatively and work collaboratively within the agency and with the Department to create opportunities for new initiatives and funding.
  

You must hold Australian citizenship and be prepared to travel interstate from time to time as required. ARPANSA requires all new employees to obtain and maintain a national security clearance as a condition of engagement. You must also meet required pre-employment checks as necessary. 
  

**Selection criteria**
  1. A comprehensive knowledge and demonstrated experience in the implementation of the Commonwealth’s legislative and policy frameworks underpinning financial management, performance, governance and accountability including the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013 (PGPA Act). 
  2. Experience providing high-level advice and support to an Executive Group and external stakeholders in the development of future strategic directions, particularly in the analysis of program delivery options and long-term funding arrangements. 
  3. Highly developed analytical and problem-solving skills including the ability to recommend innovative solutions to address business issues and improve business processes through automation and the use of technology. 
  4. Highly developed communication and interpersonal skills including the ability to lead a high performing finance team that successfully partners with business stakeholders to resolve financial challenges and emerging financial or budgetary issues. 
  5. Extensive experience in and a demonstrated ability to implement Australian Government budget and policy frameworks, including implementing reform initiatives.
  6. Proven executive level leadership ability with high level experience in successfully managing a range of Finance strategies and frameworks within a corporate or public sector environment.


  

**Eligibility**
Employment with ARPANSA is subject to: 
  

  * conditions prescribed within the Public Service Act 1999.
  * Australian Government Security Vetting Agency (AGSVA) granting a security clearance at the required level.
  * evidence of citizenship status required.
  * evidence of qualifications required of the role.
  * evidence of a completed national police-check with no risks identified by ARPANSA.
  * the CEO of ARPANSA, or their Delegate, approving a Notice of Engagement.


The Australian Government Security Clearance Application Guide Book provides information on minimum eligibility requirements for holding a security clearance at the required level. Please ensure that you are able to provide the necessary documents if you apply for the position. More information on the security clearance vetting process is available on the AGSVA website.
