### Clinical Team Leader (Social Worker)
Brindabella Business Park ACT
#### The key duties of the position include:
**The Role**
You will lead and supervise a multidisciplinary team of psychologists, social workers and health practitioners delivering confidential, trauma-informed support through 1800 SeMPRO, Defence’s 24/7 sexual violence support and advice service. Working within established clinical governance and organisational frameworks, you will be accountable for the quality, safety and sustainability of service delivery, overseeing day-to-day operations and supporting staff responding to complex and highly sensitive matters.
  

You will work closely with senior leaders and stakeholders, providing operational advice and insights informed by service trends, emerging risks and frontline practice. This role offers the opportunity to apply advanced clinical leadership skills while being supported by strong supervision, wellbeing and governance structures.
  

In this role, you will:
  

  * Lead, supervise and support clinicians delivering 24/7 trauma-informed support and advice.
  * Coordinate workloads, monitor service demand and support sustainable service delivery.
  * Apply and embed clinical governance, legislation and policy in day-to-day operations.
  * Provide oversight and guidance on complex and high-risk matters, including escalation and incident response.
  * Contribute written advice, briefs and reports to support incident response and decision-making.
  * Build and maintain effective working relationships with internal and external stakeholders.
  * Contribute to continuous improvement of service delivery practices and workforce capability.


  

**About our Team**
The Sexual Misconduct Prevention and Response Office (SeMPRO) is a specialist organisation within Defence, established in 2013 to prevent and respond to sexual violence and support cultural change across the organisation. SeMPRO plays a central role in encouraging reporting, help-seeking and safe, effective responses to sexual violence within Defence.
  

Our multidisciplinary workforce includes psychologists, social workers, educators, policy specialists and data analysts who work together to support individuals, advise leaders and strengthen organisational capability.
  

The team delivers confidential support services, education and training, expert advice and accessible resources for Defence members, their supporters and managers. SeMPRO is committed to a culture of compassion, professionalism and integrity. We recognise the impact of trauma-exposed work and actively support staff through regular supervision, debriefing, reflective practice and proactive workload management.
  

Leaders prioritise open communication, shared learning and staff wellbeing, creating an environment where people feel supported to do meaningful, high-quality work. Working in SeMPRO offers the opportunity to make a tangible difference while growing professionally within a team that values both excellence in client care and the wellbeing of its people.
  

**Our Ideal Candidate**
You are an experienced clinician with demonstrated capability to lead people and services in complex, trauma-exposed environments. You exercise sound professional judgement, communicate clearly and support others to deliver high-quality, compassionate care within strong governance frameworks.
  

You will bring:
  

  * Experience supporting individuals impacted by sexual violence and/or critical incidents, including an understanding of complex trauma.
  * Demonstrated clinical leadership capability, including supervising, mentoring and guiding staff in complex decision-making.
  * Ability to apply clinical governance principles and manage risk in an operational service delivery environment.
  * Strong written and verbal communication skills, including contributing to reports, briefs and advice for senior stakeholders.
  * Ability to build positive, collaborative working relationships in multidisciplinary and multi-stakeholder settings.
  * Resilience, adaptability and a sound understanding of the impacts of trauma-exposed work, including the importance of wellbeing and sustainable practice.


#### Eligibility
**Security Clearance:**
Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain a security clearance at Baseline Vetting level.
RecruitAbility applies to this vacancy. Under the RecruitAbility scheme you will be invited to participate in further assessment activity for the vacancy if you choose to apply under the scheme; declare you have a disability; and meet the minimum requirements for the job.
