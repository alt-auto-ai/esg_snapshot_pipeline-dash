### ASD EL1 Lead Data Centre Administrator
Darwin NT, Geraldton WA
Access Foundation Branch within Mission Capability Division is a cross-functional, integrated team of Analysts, Developers, Engineers, Radio Frequency (RF) Technicians and Project Specialists working to deliver critical capabilities against some of ASD’s hardest SIGINT access problems.
  

Shoal Bay Receiving Station (SBRS) based in Darwin and Australian Defence Satellite Communications Station (ADSCS) based in Geraldton are remote intelligence facilities managed by ASD. Working at our sites will offer a unique and challenging work environment where experienced public sector professionals work alongside highly skilled technical experts to achieve exciting outcomes for ASD. 
  

We are seeking energetic, highly organised and task-orientated Lead Data Centre Administrators to undertake a broad range of work in support of ASD’s mission. You will play a critical role in supporting the internal infrastructure of one of the largest organisations within the National Intelligence Community.
  

You will be required to work closely between the two sites and other areas across the organisation.
  

Due to the unique nature of the work conducted at this location, staff may be provided the opportunity to obtain a range of additional qualifications including construction cards, truck and forklift licenses.
  

Are you an experienced Data Centre Administrator interested in enhancing your career opportunities? Would you like to work in a unique environment assisting the ASD in its core functions? Do you want to expand your skills across a wide range of technologies? 
  

The successful candidate will be responsible for the daily operations and maintenance of our data center facilities. This role ensures the high availability, reliability, and performance of our critical systems and network infrastructure.
  

You will be a hands-on professional, comfortable with everything from physical hardware installation and network cabling to monitoring environmental controls and responding to incidents.
  

**Key Responsibilities:**
  

**Infrastructure Management:**
  * Lead and develop the data center administration.
  * Promote a strong safety and continuous improvement culture across the two locations.
  * Coordinate the installation, configure, and maintain servers, storage arrays, network devices, and other data center hardware.
  * Manage rack space, power distribution, and cable management (copper and fiber) to ensure a clean, organized, and efficient environment.
  * Perform regular hardware maintenance, upgrades, and component replacements.


  

**Operations, Monitoring & Incident Management:**
  * Monitor the health and performance of all data center infrastructure, including power (UPS, PDUs), cooling (HVAC), and environmental systems.
  * Respond promptly to alerts and alarms, diagnosing and working with appropriate teams to resolve hardware and network issues to minimize downtime.
  * Maintain meticulous documentation of data center layouts, asset inventory, network diagrams, and operational procedures.


  

**Security & Compliance:**
  * Assist in security and compliance audits (https://e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2, PCI) by providing evidence and upholding standards.


  

**Incident & Vendor Management:**
  * Coordinate with Site resources to act as a "remote hands" technician for other geographically dispersed IT teams (https://e.g., network, server, storage) to assist with troubleshooting and physical tasks.
  * Coordinate with vendors and external technicians for hardware repairs, maintenance, and new installations.


  

**Capacity Planning:**
  * Track and report on power, space, and cooling usage.
  * Assist in planning for future capacity needs to support business growth.

#### The key duties of the position include:
Your knowledge and experience will be used to contribute to the design, analysis, evaluation and resolution of moderately complex data center matters. The successful candidates will take an equally active role in both project work and business as usual activities and help pave the way for ASD’s future facilities landscape.
  

**Our ideal candidate will:**
  

  * Develop strategies to ensure future requirements for data centre space can be forecasted and fulfilled.
  * Seek out and ensures use of industry best practice to ensure plans are aligned to meet corporate sustainability targets. 


  

#### Eligibility
**Citizenship** - Applicants must be an Australian citizen. Only in exceptional circumstances can this requirement be waivered. 
  

**Organisational Suitability Assessment** – The preferred applicant will be required to undergo an Organisational Suitability Assessment (see Organisational Assessment section).
  

**Security Clearance** - It is a condition of employment that applicants are willing and able to obtain and maintain the requisite security clearance for the duration of their employment with ASD.
  

**Health Assessment** - The preferred applicant may be required to undergo a medical examination conducted by the Agency’s preferred medical provider. 
  

Please ensure that you meet the eligibility requirements outlined in this Information Pack prior to submitting an application.
  

#### Notes
We are seeking candidates with experience in the following fields:
  * Proven experience (3+ years) in a data centre operations, network operations, or systems administrator role.
  * Strong hands-on experience with server hardware ([https://e.g.,](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https://e.g.,) Dell, HP, Cisco UCS), storage systems ([https://e.g.,](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https://e.g.,) NetApp, Pure, Dell EMC), and network equipment ([https://e.g.,](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https://e.g.,) Cisco, Arista, Juniper).
  * Excellent understanding of data centre power and cooling concepts (UPS, PDUs, CRAC units).
  * Proficient in network and server cabling standards and best practices (Cat6, fibre optics).
  * Familiarity with data centre monitoring tools ([https://e.g.,](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https://e.g.,) Nagios, Zabbix, SolarWinds, or DCIM software).
  * Strong problem-solving and critical-thinking skills.
  * Ability to perform physical tasks, including lifting and moving equipment up to 25 kg.
  * Meticulous attention to detail and a strong commitment to documentation.


  

Staff are required to have knowledge of and compliance to relevant legislative frameworks, government decision-making and Australian Signals Directorate’s mission and policy requirements.
