### Platform Enablement Manager - Products & Services
Canberra ACT
## **About the Branch**
The Digital Services Branch (DSB) enables the agency to achieve its mission by delivering innovative and adaptive digital and information and communications technology (ICT) capabilities. DSB provides modern tools, platforms, and services to facilitate a highly productive and digitally enabled environment for the agency and its stakeholders. DSB also runs a large-scale IT development and sustainment workstream to support the CER adopting new capabilities.
  

DSB innovates and influences the agency in embracing new and emerging technology to remain at the forefront of our sector.
  

## **About the Section**
The Products & Services Section enables the Clean Energy Regulator to operate as a technology-enabled organisation by providing secure, reliable and sustainable digital platforms and services. The section supports a democratised IT operating model, where technology capability is distributed across multiple delivery teams and business areas, by focusing on platform stewardship, vendor management, cyber security, and cross-cutting enablement.
  

The section promotes shift-left behaviours, automation and self-service to reduce duplication and reliance on bespoke solutions. By identifying opportunities for reuse and consistency across platforms, Products & Services improves organisational resilience, scalability and the effective use of technology to deliver agency outcomes.
#### The key duties of the position include:
Under broad direction, the Platform Enablement Manager will: 
  

  * Lead the Platform Enablement function to support the agency’s transition to democratised IT, enabling safe, consistent and scalable use of digital platforms across multiple delivery teams and business areas.
  * Maintain a cross-platform view of the agency’s technology landscape to identify systemic friction, duplication and opportunities for automation and reuse.
  * Identify, prioritise, or recommend cross-platform enablement and automation initiatives that improve self-service and reduce manual effort.
  * Translate architectural, security and governance intent into practical enablement artefacts, including guidance, patterns and automation-ready reference approaches.
  * Build and sustain effective working relationships across delivery teams, sustainment functions, governance areas and vendors to enable shared outcomes.
  * Provide technical insight and context to support vendor engagement, risk discussions and senior decision-making.
  * Strengthen organisational resilience by embedding institutional platform knowledge, patterns and lessons learned into shared practice.
  * Lead and develop staff to operate effectively as enablers within a distributed and evolving technology environment.
  * Contribute to section planning, reporting and continuous improvement activities in line with branch and agency priorities.


  

## **Knowledge, Skills and Attributes**
The ideal candidate will demonstrate: 
  

  * Experience working across complex digital platforms in a large or federated ICT environment.
  * Proven ability to identify cross cutting opportunities and translate strategic, architectural or governance intent into practical, reusable enablement outcomes.
  * Strong stakeholder engagement skills, with experience influencing outcomes through collaboration rather than authority.
  * Experience supporting or enabling automation, DevOps, service management or platform-based capabilities across multiple teams is highly desirable.
  * Experience working with outsourced service providers or vendors in a technology context is highly regarded.


  

Tertiary qualifications in information technology, engineering or a related discipline, or equivalent experience is highly regarded.

#### Getting to know the Clean Energy Regulator
The Clean Energy Regulator is the Government body responsible for administering legislation to reduce carbon emissions and increase the use of clean energy. The Clean Energy Regulator has administrative responsibilities for the: • National Greenhouse and Energy Reporting Scheme, under the National Greenhouse and Energy Reporting Act 2007 • Emissions Reduction Fund, under the Carbon Credits (Carbon Farming Initiative) Act 2011, • Renewable Energy Target, under the Renewable Energy (Electricity) Act 2000, and • Australian National Registry of Emissions Units, under the Australian National Registry of Emissions Units Act 2011, • Nature Repair Market, under the Nature Repair Act 2023, • Guarantee of Origin, under the Future Made in Australia (Guarantee of Origin) Bill 2024. Our purpose is to accelerate carbon abatement for Australia. We work with our stakeholders and clients including Australian Government departments and agencies, industry bodies, liable entities and the community to provide regulatory services of the highest standard. We offer challenging and rewarding work in administering legislation that will reduce carbon emissions and increase the use of clean energy. Our agency is committed to workplace diversity and aims to create an environment that values and utilises the contribution of people from different backgrounds, experiences and perspectives. We encourage applications from Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander People, people with a disability, people from diverse cultural and linguistic backgrounds and mature age workers
