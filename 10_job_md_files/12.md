Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry
### Contract and Finance Officer
Canberra ACT
**Why YOU are important to us and what impact will you have on the People, Property and Coordination Division and the departments’ goal.**
We support our people across the employment life cycle, enabling the effective management of people-related risk and building future ready leaders, people and culture. We prevent, detect and respond to misconduct, fraud and corruption, deliver objective and risk-based internal audit, assurance and advisory services, and provide support to our two independent Inspectors-General. We ensure the effective management of our property portfolio, facilities management, security, sustainability, facilities emergency management, and a range of corporate site services and commercial contracts nationwide.
  

We provide a safe, reliable, and efficient environment that enables staff to work effectively. We are customer-focused, proactive, and dedicated to delivering quality services and solutions.
  

As the **Contract and Finance Officer** you will undertake a range of contract administration, management accounting and financial processing activities, primarily supporting property, facilities and service contracts. In this role you will demonstrate initiative and attention to detail and provides accurate, timely and compliant contract and financial processing support to the Branch. Working under limited direction, you will support the maintenance of reliable contract and financial records and contributes to reporting, analysis and continuous improvement activities. The successful applicant will have a sound understanding of basic accounting principles, government financial frameworks and contract administration practices. Experience using TechnologyOne is desirable.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**This is where you play an important role.**
As the Contract and Finance Officer, you will:
  * Support the Property Contract Manager by undertaking contract administration, financial processing and reporting activities across the Property Services Branch.
  * Ensure contract documentation is complete, accurate and appropriately executed before being established and maintained in TechnologyOne and other record-keeping systems.
  * Engage with internal and external stakeholders to support property, facilities and service contracts that are:
    * compliant with the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013 and the Commonwealth Procurement Rules
    * supported by appropriate financial and contractual authority
    * accurately recorded and maintained.
  * Maintain and update property contract registers and prepare contract-related reports as required.
  * Establish new contract records and generate contract variations as needed.
  * Undertake financial processing associated with property contracts and month-end activities, including:
    * processing creditor payments accurately and in a timely manner
    * ensuring payments align with departmental and supplier terms
    * preparing and posting journals accurately and on time
    * maintaining financial records and supporting reconciliations
    * reconciling clearing accounts and resolving discrepancies.
  * Undertake branch cost recovery activities, including:
    * preparing and issuing customer invoices accurately and on time
    * ensuring invoices comply with departmental and customer requirements
    * monitoring receipt of payments and following up overdue accounts.
  * Working under limited direction, review and action Property Service Provider fund requests.


#### Eligibility
**Our ideal candidate will have:**
A sound understanding of:
  * the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013
  * the Commonwealth Procurement Rules
  * government financial management and contract administration principles.


Demonstrated ability to:
  * make decisions with some autonomy in relation to accounts payable, receivable and contract administration activities 
  * apply judgement and limited discretion when interpreting and applying legislation, policies, instructions and procedures
  * organise work, manage competing priorities and meet deadlines
  * undertake research and analysis and present findings clearly. 
  * communicate clearly in plain English
  * take responsibility for the completion of allocated tasks and manage workflow effectively.
