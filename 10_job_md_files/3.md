High Speed Rail Authority
### Senior Manager People & Culture
Canberra ACT, Sydney NSW
**Salary range as of 12 March 2026 - $149,636 - $168,156****+ 15.4% Super**
  

**About the Role**
This is a high impact senior leadership role shaping the organisation’s workforce strategy, culture and long term capability. You will lead a high performing People & Culture team to deliver strategic, organisation wide initiatives that attract, develop and retain a capable and engaged workforce.
  

As a trusted adviser, you will influence senior executives through authoritative, evidence based advice on complex and sensitive workforce matters, while overseeing critical programs that support performance, leadership and organisational sustainability. Working in close partnership with business leaders, you will deliver people centred, forward looking solutions aligned to HSRA values, government expectations and organisational priorities. This role offers the opportunity to shape long term workforce outcomes and build a contemporary, inclusive and high performing People & Culture function.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**Your Impact**
In this senior leadership role, you will:
• Lead the People & Culture function, shaping workforce strategy, organisational capability and culture aligned to APS values, merit principles and HSRA’s strategic priorities. 
• Provide authoritative advice to executives and the Board on workforce capability, culture, risk and compliance, ensuring defensible and contemporary people practices.
• Establish and oversee robust HR governance, policies and systems, ensuring compliance with APS legislation, workforce risk management and high‑quality service delivery. 
• Drive organisational development, performance and leadership capability initiatives to build a future‑ready, inclusive and high‑performing workforce.
  

**About You**
As a Senior People & Culture leader who brings:
• Demonstrated strategic leadership experience delivering organisation‑wide HR initiatives that achieve measurable and sustainable outcomes.
• Strong stakeholder influence, with the ability to build trust, navigate complexity and provide authoritative advice to senior executives.
• Sound judgement and high integrity, particularly in managing sensitive, complex or high‑risk people matters.
• A strong commitment to inclusion, wellbeing and positive workplace culture, with experience fostering high‑performing and engaged teams.
• Broad expertise across core HR domains, including workforce planning, organisational development, employee relations, talent management, change management and workforce analytics.
• Highly developed communication and negotiation skills, enabling you to influence decisions, support reform and drive collaborative outcomes.
  

#### Eligibility
**Position eligibility:**
In addition to the above key capabilities, to be eligible for this position you must:
• Be an Australian citizen 
• Baseline Security Clearance preferred
• Satisfy a National Coordinated Criminal History Check
• Demonstrate significant experience in senior HR or People & Culture leadership roles, preferably in government, large organisations or similarly complex environments. 
• Possess relevant qualifications in human resources, organisational psychology, business, management or a related field (highly desirable).

#### Getting to know the High Speed Rail Authority
About Us The High Speed Rail Authority, an independent corporate Commonwealth entity, has been tasked with developing a national high speed rail network. Stage 1 would connect Newcastle to Sydney on a dedicated new railway line with new world-class trains capable of travelling up to 320km/h. High speed rail stations would be built in central Newcastle, on the Central Coast and in central Sydney. Other stations are also under consideration. Journeys would be at least twice as fast as now – it would take about an hour to get from Newcastle to Sydney and about 30 minutes from the Central Coast to Newcastle or to Sydney. As the proposed network expands, further regions and communities would be connected by high speed rail. A journey from Canberra to Sydney would take about 90 minutes, while it would take about four hours to travel from Melbourne to Sydney or from Sydney to Brisbane.
