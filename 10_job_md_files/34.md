Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water (DCCEEW)
### Director CIS Payments Validation and Modelling
Adelaide SA, Brisbane QLD, Cairns QLD, Darwin NT, Hobart TAS, Melbourne VIC, Perth WA, Sydney NSW
The Clean Energy Investment and Facilitation Division is responsible for delivering on the Australian Government’s commitment to deliver more reliable, affordable and low-emissions energy for all Australians. Australia’s electricity system is going through unprecedented change, with the transition to net zero by 2050 requiring new ways of managing systems and markets. This is an exciting opportunity to make a difference by working on the Capacity Investment Scheme (CIS), which provides a national framework to encourage new investment in renewable capacity, such as wind and solar, as well as clean dispatchable capacity, such as battery storage. The CIS aims to support 9GW of clean dispatchable capacity and 23 GW of variable renewable capacity by 2030 – equivalent to about half the current capacity of the National Electricity Market (NEM). The CIS is a key policy lever for delivering the Australian Government’s ambitious 82% renewable energy target. This role will feature a dynamic working environment, a high level of senior executive attention and engagement, opportunities for development and leadership, and most of all, the opportunity to have a significant positive impact.
  

The CIS Delivery and Governance Branch aims to ensure all operational aspects of the CIS and being delivered in an efficient and effective manner. The Branch works collaboratively across Government and with service delivery partners and advisors to ensure scheme settings are robust and commercially effective.
  

The CIS Payments Validation & Modelling Team is responsible for managing the validation and processing of support payments for CIS-awarded energy projects, as well as to provide analytical and modelling services / advice to other CIS teams relating to complex electricity derivatives. This is a multifunctional team that will require capabilities and expertise in electricity markets, finance, data science / modelling, contract management and communications in order to manage an increasing volume of CIS payments to/from energy projects, along with other contractual matters, from the date these projects become operational. The team works collaboratively across Government and service delivery partners and advisors to ensure scheme settings are robust and commercially effective.
  

For more information about us please visit our [website](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.dcceew.gov.au%2F).
#### The key duties of the position include:
The role will involve: 
  * Analysis and modelling of electricity derivatives (including CIS Agreements and other bespoke OTC electricity instruments).
  * Independent estimation of settlement amounts for validation of CIS invoices submitted for payment, including bottom-up replication of electricity pool revenues, ancillary services, third party contracts (e.g. PPAs, offtake agreements), etc.
  * Adhering to and assisting with the management of strict protocols for the validation of payments and the provision of internal and external advice on technical matters relating to settlements, including direct communication with Projects.
  * Writing and reviewing clear and accurate guidance for external publication and contributing to the interpretation of complex energy market and contractual matters in collaboration with other CIS teams. 


  

This recruitment process may be used to fill future vacancies in the Clean Energy Investment and Facilitation Division where duties are of a similar nature.
