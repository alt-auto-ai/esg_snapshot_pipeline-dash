**ENVIRONMENTAL CONSULTANT – CONTAMINATED LAND & SITE ASSESSMENT**
**Newly Created Role Due to Growth  
Hybrid – On-site and In-office role (Brunswick / Essendon office)  
Technical Delivery Across Site Investigations & Remediation Projects  
Auditor Engagement Experience Required**
Join a fast-growing, high-performing business delivering specialised contaminated land and remediation projects across Victoria. This is a hands-on Environmental Consultant role focused on delivering site investigations, preparing technical reports, and working closely with EPA-appointed auditors to support project outcomes.
This is not a purely advisory role. It is a high-responsibility, technically focused position working alongside project delivery teams to ensure environmental compliance, accuracy, and quality across all projects.
* * *
**ABOUT THE ROLE**
This is a newly defined role created to strengthen the technical capability within the business.
You will take ownership of:  
· Detailed site investigations (core focus)  
· Technical reporting and documentation  
· EPA auditor engagement and support  
· Environmental compliance across projects
You will work closely with Project Managers and the Director to ensure all environmental and contamination-related components of projects are delivered to a high standard.
Over time, you will be expected to:  
· Take ownership of investigations from planning through to reporting  
· Lead interactions with auditors and regulators  
· Support remediation planning and validation processes  
· Contribute to building stronger technical systems and reporting standards across the business
* * *
**KEY RESPONSIBILITIES**
Site Investigation & Technical Delivery
· Plan and deliver Preliminary and Detailed Site Investigations (PSI / DSI)  
· Coordinate fieldwork including drilling, sampling, and site testing  
· Oversee subcontractors conducting investigation works  
· Identify contamination risks and site constraints  
· Ensure all work aligns with relevant guidelines and standards
* * *
**Technical Reporting**
· Prepare high-quality, compliant reports including:  
· Preliminary Site Investigations (PSIs)  
· Detailed Site Investigations (DSIs)  
· Remediation Action Plans (RAPs)  
· Validation and closure reports  
· Interpret environmental data and provide clear recommendations  
· Maintain strong attention to detail and documentation quality
* * *
**EPA Auditor & Stakeholder Engagement**
· Work directly with EPA-appointed environmental auditors  
· Support audit processes and respond to auditor queries  
· Prepare documentation for review and approval  
· Liaise with regulators, consultants, and clients
* * *
**Remediation Support**
· Provide technical input into remediation strategies  
· Support development of remediation methodologies  
· Assist with validation and project sign-off processes  
· Identify risks and practical solutions during remediation
* * *
**Project Support & Coordination**
· Work closely with Project Managers to align technical and delivery outcomes  
· Coordinate laboratories, contractors, and specialists  
· Track progress of investigations and reporting deliverables  
· Maintain structured project documentation and records
* * *
**WHAT WE’RE LOOKING FOR**
**Experience (Essential)**
· 3–5+ years’ experience in contaminated land / environmental consulting  
· Strong experience in:  
· Site investigations (PSI / DSI)  
· Technical environmental reporting  
· EPA Victoria guidelines and frameworks
**Proven experience:  
** · Delivering investigations from planning through to reporting  
· Preparing structured, high-quality technical reports  
· Working with or supporting EPA-appointed auditors  
· Operating in project-based, site-driven environments
* * *
**Capability**
· Strong technical understanding of contaminated land assessment  
· Ability to interpret environmental data and identify risks  
· High attention to detail in reporting and documentation  
· Strong organisational and follow-through ability  
· Ability to work independently while coordinating with project teams
* * *
**Personal Attributes (Critical for Success)**
· Detail-oriented and technically precise  
· Calm and professional when dealing with auditors and regulators  
· Strong written and verbal communication skills  
· Practical and solutions-focused  
· High ownership and accountability  
· Not reactive or easily overwhelmed  
· Comfortable working in evolving, fast-moving environments
* * *
**WORKING ENVIRONMENT**
This role is suited to someone who is:  
· Hands-on and comfortable working between site and office  
· Confident operating in technically demanding environments  
· Able to work closely with project teams and external stakeholders  
· Comfortable in a business that is still building structure
This role is not suited to someone who:  
· Prefers purely desk-based or advisory work  
· Avoids accountability or ownership  
· Struggles with deadlines and reporting quality  
· Requires highly structured corporate environments
* * *
**REMUNERATION & GROWTH**
· Salary: $130,000 - $150,000 + superannuation  
· Opportunity to work on complex and varied contaminated land projects  
· Exposure to EPA auditors and regulatory processes  
· Opportunity to grow into a senior technical role within the business
* * *

**ENVIRONMENTAL CONSULTANT – CONTAMINATED LAND & SITE ASSESSMENT**
**Newly Created Role Due to Growth  
Hybrid – On-site and In-office role (Brunswick / Essendon office)  
Technical Delivery Across Site Investigations & Remediation Projects  
Auditor Engagement Experience Required**
Join a fast-growing, high-performing business delivering specialised contaminated land and remediation projects across Victoria. This is a hands-on Environmental Consultant role focused on delivering site investigations, preparing technical reports, and working closely with EPA-appointed auditors to support project outcomes.
This is not a purely advisory role. It is a high-responsibility, technically focused position working alongside project delivery teams to ensure environmental compliance, accuracy, and quality across all projects.
* * *
**ABOUT THE ROLE**
This is a newly defined role created to strengthen the technical capability within the business.
You will take ownership of:  
· Detailed site investigations (core focus)  
· Technical reporting and documentation  
· EPA auditor engagement and support  
· Environmental compliance across projects
You will work closely with Project Managers and the Director to ensure all environmental and contamination-related components of projects are delivered to a high standard.
Over time, you will be expected to:  
· Take ownership of investigations from planning through to reporting  
· Lead interactions with auditors and regulators  
· Support remediation planning and validation processes  
· Contribute to building stronger technical systems and reporting standards across the business
* * *
**KEY RESPONSIBILITIES**
Site Investigation & Technical Delivery
· Plan and deliver Preliminary and Detailed Site Investigations (PSI / DSI)  
· Coordinate fieldwork including drilling, sampling, and site testing  
· Oversee subcontractors conducting investigation works  
· Identify contamination risks and site constraints  
· Ensure all work aligns with relevant guidelines and standards
* * *
**Technical Reporting**
· Prepare high-quality, compliant reports including:  
· Preliminary Site Investigations (PSIs)  
· Detailed Site Investigations (DSIs)  
· Remediation Action Plans (RAPs)  
· Validation and closure reports  
· Interpret environmental data and provide clear recommendations  
· Maintain strong attention to detail and documentation quality
* * *
**EPA Auditor & Stakeholder Engagement**
· Work directly with EPA-appointed environmental auditors  
· Support audit processes and respond to auditor queries  
· Prepare documentation for review and approval  
· Liaise with regulators, consultants, and clients
* * *
**Remediation Support**
· Provide technical input into remediation strategies  
· Support development of remediation methodologies  
· Assist with validation and project sign-off processes  
· Identify risks and practical solutions during remediation
* * *
**Project Support & Coordination**
· Work closely with Project Managers to align technical and delivery outcomes  
· Coordinate laboratories, contractors, and specialists  
· Track progress of investigations and reporting deliverables  
· Maintain structured project documentation and records
* * *
**WHAT WE’RE LOOKING FOR**
**Experience (Essential)**
· 3–5+ years’ experience in contaminated land / environmental consulting  
· Strong experience in:  
· Site investigations (PSI / DSI)  
· Technical environmental reporting  
· EPA Victoria guidelines and frameworks
**Proven experience:  
** · Delivering investigations from planning through to reporting  
· Preparing structured, high-quality technical reports  
· Working with or supporting EPA-appointed auditors  
· Operating in project-based, site-driven environments
* * *
**Capability**
· Strong technical understanding of contaminated land assessment  
· Ability to interpret environmental data and identify risks  
· High attention to detail in reporting and documentation  
· Strong organisational and follow-through ability  
· Ability to work independently while coordinating with project teams
* * *
**Personal Attributes (Critical for Success)**
· Detail-oriented and technically precise  
· Calm and professional when dealing with auditors and regulators  
· Strong written and verbal communication skills  
· Practical and solutions-focused  
· High ownership and accountability  
· Not reactive or easily overwhelmed  
· Comfortable working in evolving, fast-moving environments
* * *
**WORKING ENVIRONMENT**
This role is suited to someone who is:  
· Hands-on and comfortable working between site and office  
· Confident operating in technically demanding environments  
· Able to work closely with project teams and external stakeholders  
· Comfortable in a business that is still building structure
This role is not suited to someone who:  
· Prefers purely desk-based or advisory work  
· Avoids accountability or ownership  
· Struggles with deadlines and reporting quality  
· Requires highly structured corporate environments
* * *
**REMUNERATION & GROWTH**
· Salary: $130,000 - $150,000 + superannuation  
· Opportunity to work on complex and varied contaminated land projects  
· Exposure to EPA auditors and regulatory processes  
· Opportunity to grow into a senior technical role within the business
* * *

**ENVIRONMENTAL CONSULTANT – CONTAMINATED LAND & SITE ASSESSMENT**
**Newly Created Role Due to Growth  
Hybrid – On-site and In-office role (Brunswick / Essendon office)  
Technical Delivery Across Site Investigations & Remediation Projects  
Auditor Engagement Experience Required**
Join a fast-growing, high-performing business delivering specialised contaminated land and remediation projects across Victoria. This is a hands-on Environmental Consultant role focused on delivering site investigations, preparing technical reports, and working closely with EPA-appointed auditors to support project outcomes.
This is not a purely advisory role. It is a high-responsibility, technically focused position working alongside project delivery teams to ensure environmental compliance, accuracy, and quality across all projects.
* * *
**ABOUT THE ROLE**
This is a newly defined role created to strengthen the technical capability within the business.
You will take ownership of:  
· Detailed site investigations (core focus)  
· Technical reporting and documentation  
· EPA auditor engagement and support  
· Environmental compliance across projects
You will work closely with Project Managers and the Director to ensure all environmental and contamination-related components of projects are delivered to a high standard.
Over time, you will be expected to:  
· Take ownership of investigations from planning through to reporting  
· Lead interactions with auditors and regulators  
· Support remediation planning and validation processes  
· Contribute to building stronger technical systems and reporting standards across the business
* * *
**KEY RESPONSIBILITIES**
Site Investigation & Technical Delivery
· Plan and deliver Preliminary and Detailed Site Investigations (PSI / DSI)  
· Coordinate fieldwork including drilling, sampling, and site testing  
· Oversee subcontractors conducting investigation works  
· Identify contamination risks and site constraints  
· Ensure all work aligns with relevant guidelines and standards
* * *
**Technical Reporting**
· Prepare high-quality, compliant reports including:  
· Preliminary Site Investigations (PSIs)  
· Detailed Site Investigations (DSIs)  
· Remediation Action Plans (RAPs)  
· Validation and closure reports  
· Interpret environmental data and provide clear recommendations  
· Maintain strong attention to detail and documentation quality
* * *
**EPA Auditor & Stakeholder Engagement**
· Work directly with EPA-appointed environmental auditors  
· Support audit processes and respond to auditor queries  
· Prepare documentation for review and approval  
· Liaise with regulators, consultants, and clients
* * *
**Remediation Support**
· Provide technical input into remediation strategies  
· Support development of remediation methodologies  
· Assist with validation and project sign-off processes  
· Identify risks and practical solutions during remediation
* * *
**Project Support & Coordination**
· Work closely with Project Managers to align technical and delivery outcomes  
· Coordinate laboratories, contractors, and specialists  
· Track progress of investigations and reporting deliverables  
· Maintain structured project documentation and records
* * *
**WHAT WE’RE LOOKING FOR**
**Experience (Essential)**
· 3–5+ years’ experience in contaminated land / environmental consulting  
· Strong experience in:  
· Site investigations (PSI / DSI)  
· Technical environmental reporting  
· EPA Victoria guidelines and frameworks
**Proven experience:  
** · Delivering investigations from planning through to reporting  
· Preparing structured, high-quality technical reports  
· Working with or supporting EPA-appointed auditors  
· Operating in project-based, site-driven environments
* * *
**Capability**
· Strong technical understanding of contaminated land assessment  
· Ability to interpret environmental data and identify risks  
· High attention to detail in reporting and documentation  
· Strong organisational and follow-through ability  
· Ability to work independently while coordinating with project teams
* * *
**Personal Attributes (Critical for Success)**
· Detail-oriented and technically precise  
· Calm and professional when dealing with auditors and regulators  
· Strong written and verbal communication skills  
· Practical and solutions-focused  
· High ownership and accountability  
· Not reactive or easily overwhelmed  
· Comfortable working in evolving, fast-moving environments
* * *
**WORKING ENVIRONMENT**
This role is suited to someone who is:  
· Hands-on and comfortable working between site and office  
· Confident operating in technically demanding environments  
· Able to work closely with project teams and external stakeholders  
· Comfortable in a business that is still building structure
This role is not suited to someone who:  
· Prefers purely desk-based or advisory work  
· Avoids accountability or ownership  
· Struggles with deadlines and reporting quality  
· Requires highly structured corporate environments
* * *
**REMUNERATION & GROWTH**
· Salary: $130,000 - $150,000 + superannuation  
· Opportunity to work on complex and varied contaminated land projects  
· Exposure to EPA auditors and regulatory processes  
· Opportunity to grow into a senior technical role within the business
* * *
