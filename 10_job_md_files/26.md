### Corporate Compliance Officer
Adelaide SA, Brisbane QLD, Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC, Sydney NSW
  * **Do you want to work at an agency where each day reveals new opportunities to work on some of the biggest issues facing consumers and businesses and have opportunities to pursue new and exciting pathways?**
  * **Do you want to work at a place where smart and dedicated people from all walks of life collaborate to work on meaningful matters?**
  * **Do you want to join a small, collaborative team where you will deliver compliance oversight, support internal audits, promote integrity across the AER?**


  

**Then this is the place for you!** At the AER, we work to make energy consumers better off, now and in the future. We focus on ensuring a secure, reliable and affordable energy future for Australia as it transitions to net zero emissions. Our high performing culture is built on a foundation of care, support and inclusion. It’s why we’re driven by connection and collaboration, sharing our skills, knowledge and support with each other freely and frequently. It’s why we prioritise the wellbeing of our people and empower them to work autonomously, flexibly and courageously. We believe the best version of you is the one that’s true to who you are – and that’s the you we want working alongside us.
  

We value flexibility, this role can be performed at any of the AER offices located in Adelaide, Brisbane, Canberra, Melbourne, or Sydney and are available on a full-time, part-time or job-share basis.
  

**Applicants from diverse backgrounds including First Nations peoples, people with disability and those from different cultural backgrounds are encouraged to apply.**
#### The key duties of the position include:
  * Work closely with the Manager, Assurance and colleagues in the Legal and Assurance Branch to ensure the AER’s assurance and compliance activities align with the _PGPA Act 2013_ and the Commonwealth Risk Management Framework. This includes supporting the management of the AER’s internal audit program, collaborating with the internal audit partner, and assisting with secretariat services for the AER’s Risk and Audit Committee.
  * Provide policy advice and carry out fraud and integrity control initiatives across the AER workforce, including Public Interest Disclosures and National Anti-Corruption Commission referrals.
  * Engage with the Australian National Audit Office (ANAO), assisting the Manager, Assurance to coordinate input into ANAO’s audit program and briefing the AER senior management team on findings from ANAO reports.
  * Contribute to the planning and delivery of both internal and external audits and reviews.
  * Build and maintain professional relationships at all levels within the AER, supporting the Legal & Assurance Branch in delivering responsive services to internal clients.
  * Assist in developing internal resources and delivering training to staff on relevant compliance and integrity matters.


  

#### Eligibility
In order to succeed in the role, you will have:
  * Demonstrated capability and experience in supporting the range of services and functions outlined above.
  * Demonstrated understanding of Australian government audit and risk assurance practices, including experience with the _PGPA Act 2013_ and the Commonwealth Risk Management Framework (or relevant State/Territory equivalents).
  * Strong strategic thinking and analytical skills to contribute positively to the AER and experience in handing complex and/or sensitive matters.
  * Excellent communication and interpersonal abilities, with the capacity to provide informed advice to the AER.
  * Advanced relationship-building and stakeholder management skills.


  

To find out more about us and the role, please read the Candidate Kit.

#### Getting to know the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission
The ACCC is an independent statutory authority that administers the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 and other Acts. The ACCC works to promote effective competition and fair trading in the market place to benefit consumers, business and the community, and efficiency in the delivery of certain infrastructure services. The AER is a part of the ACCC. The AER is Australia's national energy market regulator and has an independent board. The AER shares employees, resources and facilities with the ACCC.
