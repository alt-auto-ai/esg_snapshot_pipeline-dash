### Fleet Governance Knowledge Manager
Potts Point NSW
#### The key duties of the position include:
**The Role**
The Knowledge Manager is responsible for understanding and interpreting complex to highly complex information to support the design, implementation, and optimisation of structured and unstructured Fleet knowledge repositories. This role enhances knowledge creation, sharing, and retention across Fleet systems within Objective and the Defence Intranet.
The position leads the development, promulgation, and enforcement of Fleet-wide business rules and protocols relating to Knowledge Management, ensuring consistency, compliance, and best-practice application. The Knowledge Manager also implements and oversees procedures for the ongoing maintenance, accuracy, and usability of knowledge management systems.
  

A key function of the role is the assessment and evaluation of lessons learned to identify insights and opportunities that drive continuous improvement across Fleet operations.
  

**About our Team**
The Fleet Governance Directorate performs a central role in assuring the effective, safe, and accountable management of Navy’s fleet capability across its operational life cycle. The team develops and applies governance frameworks, policy, and decision/support processes that enable senior leadership to understand and manage risk related to seaworthiness, safety, compliance, and sustainability. Through independent assurance, coordination with technical authorities, and engagement with Fleet Command, the Directorate ensures that responsibilities are clearly defined and exercised appropriately.
This work aligns directly with the Organisation’s mission by enabling informed, defensible decisions that balance operational readiness with statutory, safety, and stewardship obligations. By providing consistent governance oversight and trusted advice, the team supports the delivery of resilient maritime capability, protects Navy personnel and platforms, and underpins confidence in Navy’s ability to operate effectively now and into the future.
  

**Our Ideal Candidate**
Our Ideal Candidate:
  * Has an in depth knowledge of compliance legislative frameworks, government decision making and Defence’s mission and policy requirements. 
  * Can operate with delegated authority to make decisions within assigned scope, accountable for the quality, timeliness and impact of outcomes delivered. 
  * Applies sound judgement, problem solving skills and analytical thinking. 
  * Communicates effectively, including preparing high quality written products. 
  * Builds productive relationships with stakeholders across diverse disciplines. 
  * Manages competing priorities and work with initiative and accountability.


#### Eligibility
**Security Clearance:**
Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain a security clearance at “Negative Vetting Level 1”.
RecruitAbility applies to this vacancy. Under the RecruitAbility scheme you will be invited to participate in further assessment activity for the vacancy if you choose to apply under the scheme; declare you have a disability; and meet the minimum requirements for the job. #### Getting to know the Department of Defence
The Department of Defence employs around 18,000 Australian Public Service (APS) employees. Defence APS employees work in a wide variety of critical roles, Australia wide, enabling our three services Navy, Army and Air Force, to do their job.
