Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water (DCCEEW)
### Directors - Regulatory and Policy
Various locations - ACT ACT, Various locations - NSW NSW, Various locations - NT NT, Various locations - QLD QLD, Various locations - SA SA, Various locations - TAS TAS, Various locations - VIC VIC, Various locations - WA WA
The Environment Regulation Division within the Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water is a dynamic, exciting place to work. As the key regulator for matters protected under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999, the division holds a central place in the Department and works closely with state and territory governments to ensure development is sustainable and achieves the best protection for the environment. Following the passing of the Australian Government’s Environment Protection Reform Bills in November 2025, the Environment Regulation Division will transition into the National Environmental Protection Agency on 1 July 2026. The National EPA will be a strong, independent regulator. It will have a clear focus on ensuring better compliance with and stronger enforcement of Australia’s new environmental laws.
  

The National EPA will be responsible for: 
  * education, compliance, and enforcement;
  * issuing permits and licences;
  * assessing projects and setting approval conditions. 


  

For more information on the reforms please visit Environment protection reforms - DCCEEW. The Environment Regulation Division directly supports Australia’s need to develop and grow to maintain our quality of life, while ensuring nature and the ecological processes that we rely on are maintained and protected. This safeguards Australia’s future prosperity, and the quality of life for future generations.
  

Our activities include: 
  * Engaging with the regulated community so they understand legal obligations and can reduce environmental impacts of proposed development to the greatest extend possible.
  * Reviewing environmental impact assessments, providing robust and informed regulatory advice and ensuring all key decisions are completed effectively and within statutory timeframes.
  * Supporting regulatory functions through the provision of high-quality governance, policy advice, guidance, training, business systems process improvement. 
  * Promoting constructive and collaborative relationships with developers, other areas of the department, Commonwealth agencies, ministers’ offices and other key stakeholders.
  * Upholding a culture that is supportive, collaborative, diverse and resilient. 


  

We are looking to fill multiple positions to work in and lead teams responsible for regulation and policy development across our five branches: 
  1. Environment Assessments (Queensland) 
  2. Environment Assessments (New South Wales, Australian Capital Territory) 
  3. Environment Assessments (Western Australia, South Australia and Northern Territory) 
  4. Environment Assessments (Victoria, Tasmania) and Post Approvals 
  5. EPBC Regulatory Support (Policy, stakeholder engagement, training and referrals gateway) 


These positions include in teams who actively assess proposed developments by providing recommendations to decision makers, as well as teams who provide and develop guidance material, data analysis, system development and other support functions.
  

**The merit pool created from this recruitment process will be used to fill positions within the National EPA.**
#### The key duties of the position include:
In this role you will: 
  * Manage a workforce, dispersed across Australia, responsible for the development proposals and research their environmental impacts in accordance with legislation, associated policies, and procedures. 
  * Lead a team to prepare high-quality, legally robust materials for the Minister, senior executives, other government departments, proponents and the community including ministerial decision briefs, senate estimate briefs, Questions on Notice, ministerial correspondence, meeting papers and technical documentation. 
  * Prepare recommendation briefs for decision makers, using guidelines to analyse and present what information is important. 
  * Lead stakeholder engagement through written, online and face-to-face activities. 
  * Apply a sound understanding of the context and operation of relevant Commonwealth and State legislation and policy frameworks. 
  * Identify broader factors, trends and influences that may impact on the work of the division.


  

**Desirable qualifications**
  * Tertiary qualifications or equivalent professional experience in a relevant discipline, such as natural resource management, environmental or biological science, fisheries management or science, law or economics.


  

**For detailed information about the job-specific capabilities for this role, please view the Job Description** which can be downloaded from our website - the **Apply Now** link will take you there. 
#### Eligibility
**Citizenship -** to be eligible for employment with the Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water you must be an Australian citizen.
  

**Security Clearance -** this position requires a **Baseline Vetting** security clearance. You will be required to obtain and maintain a clearance at this level. 
  

**Pre-employment checks -** your suitability for employment will be assessed through a pre-employment screening process. This process includes a requirement to undergo and satisfy a National Police Check, referee checks, character clearance and where required a pre-employment medical assessment, specified mandatory qualification(s) validation and a probation period.
