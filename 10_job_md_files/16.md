Climate Change Authority
### Social Media and Communication Officer
Canberra ACT
**Please read the full APSJobs ad and 'how to apply' instructions below.**
  

**About this role**
The Climate Change Authority (the Authority) is an independent statutory body established under the _Climate Change Authority Act 2011_ to provide expert advice to the Australian Government on climate change policy and how Australia is progressing towards a prosperous and resilient economic transition to net zero emissions.
  

The Communication & Engagement team of the Outreach & Enabling Branch makes an important contribution to the Authority by communicating its work in strategic, effective and engaging ways to diverse audiences across a range of channels and formats. The team is also responsible for consulting and engaging with stakeholders and communities to gather diverse insights, amplify different voices and involve them in climate policy development, in close collaboration with colleagues across the organisation. 
  

The Social Media and Communication Officer plays a key role in enhancing the Authority’s digital presence and visual communication. This position combines skills in social media execution and graphic design to support the delivery of high-quality, accessible, and engaging content across platforms.
#### The key duties of the position include:
  * Implementing social media content plans and visual style aligned with Authority’s strategic priorities.
  * Creating visual assets including data visualisations, reports, presentations, and digital collateral.
  * Monitoring and analysing social media performance, providing insights to improve engagement.
  * Drafting and publishing website content, and assisting with any website enhancements. 
  * Ensuring all content complies with accessibility standards and government communication guidelines.
  * Collaborating with internal teams to translate complex climate policy into engaging formats.
  * Supporting the team to work closely with the Authority Chair, Executives and Members to maximise their social media profiles and presence on Authority channels. 
  * Maintaining brand consistency across all visual and digital outputs.
  * Supporting the Communication & Engagement team to help achieve the Authority’s work program, which may include other communication activities such as event assistance. 


  

Our idea candidate will have:
  * Demonstrated experience in managing social media platforms (e.g. Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn) including content creation, scheduling, and analytics.
  * Proficiency in graphic design tools (e.g. Canva, Adobe Creative Suite) and an ability to produce high-quality visual style guides and content.
  * Strong written and visual communication skills, with an ability to tailor content for diverse audiences.
  * Understanding of accessibility requirements and experience applying them to digital content.
  * Ability to work collaboratively in a multidisciplinary team and manage competing priorities.
  * Qualifications in communication, graphic design, media, or a related field, or equivalent professional experience.


  

The following are desirable but not essential:
  * Experience working in a government or policy environment.
  * Familiarity with or interest in climate change or environmental policy issues.
  * Skills in short-form video production, editing or animation tools.


#### Eligibility
  * **Citizenship:** to be eligible for employment with the Authority you must be an Australian citizen.
  * **Security Clearance:** this position requires an Australian Government Security Vetting Agency (AGSVA) Baseline security clearance. You will be required to obtain and maintain a clearance at this level.
  * **Pre-employment checks** : your suitability for employment will be assessed through a pre-employment screening process. This process includes a requirement to undergo and satisfy a National Police Check, identity checks and referee checks.


#### Notes
**How to apply**
You are required to submit a 500-word pitch explaining how your skills, knowledge and experience will be relevant to the role and why you are the best candidate for the position.
  

Your application should include your current CV, including the names and contact details for two referees (CVs must be in .doc, .docx, or .pdf format).
  

Applications close **Sunday, 31 May 2026, 11:59pm AEST** and should submitted to [recruitment@cca.gov.au](https://www.apsjobs.gov.au/s/external-link?url=mailto%3Arecruitment%40cca.gov.au). Late applications after this closing date will not be accepted.
