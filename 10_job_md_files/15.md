Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry
### Property Services and Capital Projects Managers
Adelaide SA, Brisbane QLD, Cairns QLD, Darwin NT, Melbourne VIC, Mickleham VIC, Perth WA, Sydney NSW
**Why YOU are important to us and what impact will you have on the People, Property and Security Division and the departments’ goal.**
  

We support our people across the employment life cycle, enabling the effective management of people-related risk and building future ready leaders, people and culture. We prevent, detect and respond to misconduct, fraud and corruption, deliver objective and risk-based internal audit, assurance and advisory services, and provide support to our two independent Inspectors-General. We ensure the effective management of our property portfolio, facilities management, security, sustainability, facilities emergency management, and a range of corporate site services and commercial contracts nationwide.
  

We provide a safe, reliable, and efficient environment that enables staff to work effectively. We are customer-focused, proactive, and dedicated to delivering quality services and solutions.
  

The Property Services Manager and Capital Projects Manager roles are responsible for supporting the delivery of property capital projects and facilities services for the property portfolio, including the oversight of Property Service Provider performance in the delivery of facilities management, capital projects, and accommodation services, under the Whole of Government Property Service Provider Arrangements. These roles also support the management of the day-to-day activities of small teams, which include the management of capital projects, national corporate contracts, and providing business support services.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**This is where you play an important role.**
  

As the **Property Services Manager,** the duties of the role include, but are not limited to:
  * Supporting the department’s facilities management services, ensuring safety and compliance, and optimising occupancy.
  * Overseeing development and delivery of preventative maintenance for the department’s sites, including building services, inspections, and condition assessment reports.
  * Work in collaboration with the National Capital Works team to ensure project delivery through the department's annual Property Capital Works Program.
  * Manage services delivery in alignment with national contracts.
  * Undertaking procurements of goods and services, in alignment with Commonwealth Procurement Rules.
  * Engaging and collaborating with stakeholders to achieve required outcomes, support positive relationships and ensure communication of upcoming works or procedures.
  * Managing transition and handover of project and business-as-usual activities.
  * Actively participating in relevant governance meetings and represent the Department during working group meetings.
  * Providing issue resolution and communication for internal and external stakeholders and escalate issues that have a wider departmental impact on staff or business continuity.
  * Providing leadership to Property Services Branch staff, including coaching, capability development and problem solving.


  

As the **Capital Projects Manager,** the duties of the role include, but are not limited to: 
  * Work collaboratively to assist in delivering the department’s Property Capital Works Program.
  * Contribute to the development and implementation of capital works projects, practices and processes.
  * Develop project plans, including evaluation and financial reporting.
  * Coordinate risk assessment, risk management and induction activities. 
  * Actively participate in relevant governance meetings that support the delivery of capital works projects. 
  * Perform quality assurance of risks and treatments for area of responsibility.
  * Perform independent research and analysis, including the preparation of draft reports. 
  * Support project and facilities administration, record keeping, compliance and reporting. 
  * Engage and collaborate with both internal and external stakeholders, to achieve required outcomes, support positive relationships and ensure effective communication.


#### Eligibility
**Our ideal candidate will have:**
  * Well-developed interpersonal skills to manage and resolve situations requiring sensitivity to individual and/or workgroup concerns.
  * Communicate in plain English, ensuring appropriate audience impact.
  * Lead, coach and mentor staff.
  * A team player with strong organisational, written and verbal communication skills.
  * Ability to respond and adapt to change.
  * Proven ability to deliver accurate and timely outcomes in a busy environment.
  * Well-developed computer skills and experience using Microsoft Suite applications.
  * Self-motivated, cooperative team-player with integrity, drive and resilience.
  * Demonstrated experience in a service centred role with a strong focus on customer service and stakeholder management.
  * Work independently and collaboratively, with a focus on customer service and maintaining open lines of communication.
  * Effectively communicate with internal and external stakeholders (oral, written, interpersonal and negotiation).
  * Coordinate work and prioritise tasks to meet deadlines.
  * Demonstrate analytical, interpretive and problem-solving skills.

Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Authority
### Property Services Support Officer
Townsville QLD
**The Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Authority (the Reef Authority)** is a Commonwealth non-corporate entity, and a statutory agency established by the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Act 1975 (Marine Park Act), reporting to the Minister for the Environment and Water. Our objective is the long-term protection, ecologically sustainable use, understanding and enjoyment of the Great Barrier Reef for all Australians and the international community through the care and development of the Marine Park.
  

Managing a reef roughly the size of Italy demands many skills and talents, so you don't have to be a marine biologist to contribute to our mission. From finance to marketing roles, we have a team almost as diverse as the ecosystem we protect. Our commitment and dedication to the Great Barrier Reef and the Australian public have a long-lasting impact, and we are looking for individuals who can bring their unique skills, experience, and knowledge to our high-performing and inclusive organisation.
  

**The Position**
  

We are seeking an experienced Support Officer to join the Property and Security team located at the Reef Authority’s main office in Townsville. This position provides support to the Business Operations team in all aspects of sustainable property management, protective security, and business continuity. This role provides a corporate service to the whole-of-agency.
  

The position supports the delivery of a range of matters related to the Reef Authority’s sustainable property management requirements. The position is one of a team that provides frontline services to internal and external stakeholders contacting the Reef Authority with a variety of requests. It encompasses day-to-day tasks, including supporting the administration of the corporate vehicle fleet, assisting with asset and facilities management and overall services to the Reef Authority staff to enable their day-to-day work. 
  

This is an **office-based position** located in Townsville providing frontline services to staff and stakeholders contacting the Reef Authority with a variety of requests. Due to operational requirements, there are no working from home arrangements available for this position. 
  

**About You**
  

  * You can maintain and use workflow and information databases including data entry and data retrieval tasks to support program and project objectives.
  * You can exercise independent judgement and resolve issues with a degree of guidance and monitoring from supervisor.
  * You can actively cultivate and maintain relationships with internal and external stakeholders. 
  * You are an excellent communicator, both in written and verbal forms across all agency levels.
  * You have previous work experience in a similar role or transferrable skills.


#### The key duties of the position include:
  * Facilitate property services requests and queries across a range of interfaces including shared mailboxes, workflow software, Property Services Provider work order platforms, phone and in person requests.
  * Review and assist with requests, or escalate as required, such as directing customers to appropriate sections, relocation of small office furniture (e.g. office chairs) or other workstation equipment, directing deliveries, placing requests for maintenance and escorting contractors and visitors to work area.
  * Perform administrative and functional tasks in relation to the Reef Authority’s fleet vehicles and inventory, including delivering vehicles for detailing, fuelling, charging and maintenance.
  * Direct staff to appropriate property services facilities and training, assist with training packages and deliver in-person training to staff (such as vehicle and equipment inductions).
  * Assist with maintaining security administration documents and day-to-day administrative processes including assistance in the preparation of procurement and finance requests and completing forms and documentation.
  * Provide team member support for sustainability initiatives, assist with sizing and issuing corporate uniforms, ordering stationery, and organising postage.
  * Demonstrate personal initiative and problem-solving skills in a team environment, a commitment to teamwork and flexibility in supporting others to within the organisation.
  * At times you may be required to undertake hazardous manual tasks including lifting, lowering, pushing, pulling or carrying loads of varying sizes and weights.


#### Eligibility
  * Have a Queensland Driver’s licence and be able to drive corporate vehicles (including electric vehicles) and use electric vehicle charging equipment. 
  * Ability to travel on occasions to other office sites.
  * To be eligible for employment, candidates must be an Australian citizen at the time of application.
  * The position requires a Baseline security clearance. Successful candidates will be required to obtain and maintain a clearance at this level. 
  * The successful candidate will be willing to provide identity documents and undergo an identity pre-employment check through a Document Verification Service.
