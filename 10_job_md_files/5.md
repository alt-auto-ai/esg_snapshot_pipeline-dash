### Senior Investigator
Brisbane QLD, Canberra ACT, Melbourne VIC, Perth WA, Sydney NSW
**Why YOU are important to us and what impact will you have on the People, Property and Security Division and the departments’ goal.**
  

We support our people across the employment life cycle, enabling the effective management of people-related risk and building future ready leaders, people and culture. We prevent, detect and respond to misconduct, fraud and corruption, deliver objective and risk-based internal audit, assurance and advisory services, and provide support to our two independent Inspectors-General. We ensure the effective management of our property portfolio, facilities management, security, sustainability, facilities emergency management, and a range of corporate site services and commercial contracts nationwide.
  

The Security, Integrity and Assurance branch is responsible for the delivery of the department’s integrated protective security and integrity frameworks. We prevent, detect and respond to security incidents, suspected misconduct, fraud and corruption and develop unique insights to proactively identify and mitigate risks to the department and its operations. Responsible for the provision of independent, objective and risk-based internal audit, assurance and advisory services, we also provide independent support to the Inspector-General of Biosecurity and Inspector-General of Animal Welfare and Live Animal Exports.
  

The Internal Investigations Section conducts investigations into departmental employee’s misconduct relating to suspected breaches of the APS Code of Conduct, Fraud and Corruption and Security Incidents. The Section also manage review of actions, including responding to applications lodged with external review entities such as the Fair Work Commission and the Office of the Merit Protection Commissioner. In addition, the Section manages the department’s Public Interest Disclosure (PID) framework and the National Anti-Corruption Commission (NACC) referrals and compliance obligations.
#### The key duties of the position include:
**This is where you play an important role.**
  

The**Senior Investigator** role is multi-faceted to align with the responsibilities of the Section. 
Key duties of the role will include but not limited to the following: 
  * Conducting investigations into allegations of misconduct, fraud and corruption, and security incidents across the department and ensuring compliance with the Australian Government Investigation Standards (AGIS).
  * Undertaking primary reviews of action.
  * Collecting and analysing complex streams of information to form assessments that will support the evidence required to effectively manage the risk across the department.
  * Engaging with law enforcement partners and relevant stakeholders, including the National Anti-Corruption Commission (NACC), the Australian Federal Police (AFP), and the Commonwealth Director of Public Prosecution (CDPP) on matters that may lead to criminal briefs of evidence or referrals to other law enforcement agencies.
  * Leading the mentoring and management of junior investigative staff.
  * Contributing to the development of investigative policies, procedures, and projects.
  * Partnering with a range of internal stakeholders to deliver robust, innovative, and timely investigative outcomes, including lessons learnt from vulnerabilities identified.
  * Building and maintaining relationships across government to harness opportunities and build capability and knowledge across the investigative program.
  * Providing investigation and/or coordination support to the Public Interest Disclosure scheme. 


#### Eligibility
**Our ideal candidate will have:**
  

Senior Investigator:
  * Demonstrated experience undertaking administrative and/or criminal investigations. 
  * Demonstrated experience interpreting policy/legislation and applying that knowledge to investigations and/or review of actions. 
  * Demonstrated experience in producing accurate records and reports relating to complaints, integrity matters and investigation outcomes. 
  * Demonstrated experience in the preparation of Executive level briefs and detailed documentation including Notices relevant to employee misconduct. 


  

The successful applicant for the role is expected to hold or be willing to obtain a Certificate IV Government Investigations (minimum) within 12 months of commencement.
