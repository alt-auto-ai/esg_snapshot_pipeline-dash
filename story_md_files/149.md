# Consumer duty gets ministerial support
Rule change request coming as push for more principles-based regulation continues
By [Charis Palmer](https://theenergy.co/person/charis-palmer)
A rule change request agreed to by energy ministers on Friday paves the way for delivery of a principles-based [consumer duty](https://theenergy.co/article/push-for-consumer-duty-as-energy-market-grows-more-complex) in energy, according to consumer group Energy Consumers Australia.
ECA has been pushing for a consumer duty as the energy market becomes more complex with new entrants expected on the back of the rising tide of consumer energy resources.
Such a duty, requiring providers to “act in the interests of consumers and ensure good consumer outcomes”, was also recommended by the Australian Energy Regulator in 2023.
Energy retailers have been somewhat supportive of the idea, hoping it will kill off compliance checklists, but others including consumer energy resources expert Gabrielle Kuiper would prefer to see a purpose-built energy code under the Australian Consumer Law.
Energy ministers have now agreed to request a rule change that would require retailers to “provide support more tailored to a customer’s individual needs, including cultural, language and access requirements, where these can be reasonably known by a retailer”.
ECA chief Brendan French welcomed the decision as the first major step towards principles-based regulation.
"Energy providers should have a duty to ensure good outcomes for consumers – rather than consumers continuing to bear responsibility for navigating an increasingly complex and rapidly changing energy market,” he said.
“ECA’s research shows that many consumers find the energy market complex and difficult to navigate, and these challenges can be even more pronounced for people with diverse communication and access needs.”
