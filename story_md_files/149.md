# Applications are open for the 2026 First Nations Exporter Award
22 May 2026 
![A lady holds an award smiling](https://www.indigenous.gov.au/sites/default/files/styles/1162x680_focalp/public/images/2026-05/australianexportawards.jpg.webp?itok=W7wrX7q6)
First Nations businesses are invited to apply now for the 64th Australian Export Awards.
The First Nations Exporter Award is an opportunity for First Nations businesses to receive recognition for their international success. 
At a challenging time in global trade, Australia’s First Nations exporters remain remarkably resilient. The awards provide an opportunity for recognition for Australian exporters who are successfully navigating these challenges and are Australia’s longest running export awards program.
Eligible First Nations businesses for the First Nations Exporter Awards can be operating in any industry and must have been exporting for 3 years or more.

# New defence manufacturing facility to boost Victoria’s sovereign capabilities
News
**Published:** 25 May 2026 
![Representatives from Corvus Technology Solutions and the Victorian Government standing formally in a warehouse with the Australia and South Koren flags hanging from the roof.](https://djsir.vic.gov.au/__data/assets/image/0011/2481095/Corvus-facility-opening-news-hero.png)
The opening of Corvus Technology Solutions’ new Bayswater North manufacturing facility.
Corvus Technology Solutions has opened a new state-of-the-art defence manufacturing facility in Melbourne’s southeast. It is creating 49 new high skilled jobs.
The facility will produce in-arm suspension units for armoured vehicles. Assembly at the Bayswater North facility expects to generate significant flow-on benefits for the state’s supply chain. This is valued at an estimated $45 million over 6 years.
The suspension units will be fitted to the Australian Army’s AS21 Redback vehicle that are being manufactured at [Hanwha Defence Australia](https://www.hanwha-defence.com.au/)’s Armoured Vehicle Centre of Excellence in Avalon. The units improve under vehicle protection and enhance ride, comfort and manoeuvrability.
We're supporting Corvus to establish its new facility and strengthen its capabilities. This is through the Supply Chain Investment and Supply Chain Uplift Programs under the $10 million [Victorian Land Systems Fund](https://business.vic.gov.au/grants-and-programs/victorian-land-systems-fund-supply-chain-uplift-program). The fund helps local businesses join Hanwha’s supply chain to:
  * drive growth in defence manufacturing
  * create high-skilled jobs across Victoria.


The [Victorian Defence Vision Statement](https://defence.vic.gov.au/publications/the-victorian-defence-vision-statement-2030) outlines our vision to leverage the state’s nation-leading capabilities in:
  * advanced manufacturing
  * workforce talent
  * defence-related research and development.


The sector contributes around $10.9 billion annually to our economy and employ over 29,000 workers across Victoria.
