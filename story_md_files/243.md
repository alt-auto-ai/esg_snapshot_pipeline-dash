# Secretary-General Urges Reinvigorated Commitment to End Colonialism, Build Just, Inclusive Future, as Caribbean Regional Seminar on Decolonization Begins
Following is UN Secretary-General António Guterres’ message for the opening of the Caribbean Regional Seminar on Decolonization, in Managua today:
It is my pleasure to greet the Special Committee on Decolonization, and all those gathering for the 2026 Caribbean Regional Seminar. I am grateful to the Government and people of Nicaragua for hosting this year’s discussion.
Since its earliest days, decolonization has been a core objective of the United Nations. Our world has come a long way in eight decades, with over 80 nations gaining independence since 1945 through the decolonization process.
Today, there are 17 Non-Self-Governing Territories. This year’s seminar offers an important opportunity to exchange ideas, build on progress, and identify innovative approaches.
Today also begins the International Week of Solidarity with the Peoples of Non-Self-Governing Territories, a chance to strengthen partnerships for decolonization across the globe.
Together, let us reinvigorate our commitment to bring an end to colonialism, and build a just, inclusive future for all. I wish you a productive seminar.
