# APRA consults on amendments to reporting standards for life insurers
Friday 24 April 2026
[Print](https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-consults-on-amendments-to-reporting-standards-for-life-insurers)
The Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA) has released a consultation package on the [transition of life insurance data collections](https://www.apra.gov.au/migrating-d2a-collections-support-material "Migrating D2A Collections Support Material") from Direct to APRA (D2A) to APRA Connect. APRA proposes to update the reporting standards to ensure consistency with other collections in APRA Connect. 
Written submissions are due by 3 July 2026.
The consultation package and the letter to industry can be viewed on APRA's website at: 
  * [Letter to Industry](https://www.apra.gov.au/transition-of-life-insurance-d2a-data-collections-to-apra-connect "Transition of Life Insurance D2A data collections to APRA Connect")
  * [Consultation page](https://www.apra.gov.au/transition-of-life-insurance-d2a-data-collections-to-apra-connect "Transition of Life Insurance D2A data collections to APRA Connect")

The Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA) is the prudential regulator of the financial services industry. It oversees banks, mutuals, general insurance and reinsurance companies, life insurance, private health insurers, friendly societies, and most members of the superannuation industry. APRA currently supervises institutions holding $9.8 trillion in assets for Australian depositors, policyholders and superannuation fund members.
