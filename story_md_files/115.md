Gas supply and market reforms
Consultation on the Domestic Gas Reservation Scheme – draft Design Framework opened on 25 May 2026.
The Australian Government has announced it will introduce new legislation to give effect to a domestic reservation scheme. This scheme will commence from 1 July 2027. Under the scheme, entities intending to export liquefied natural gas (LNG) will need to supply the domestic market with a volume of gas equivalent to 20 per cent of their LNG exports on an annual basis. This is their ‘domestic supply obligation’.
We have developed a draft Design Framework incorporating feedback on the reservation scheme design from earlier this year. It provides further details on the reservation scheme, including:
  * regulatory framework
  * reservation design, including how the supply obligation is calculated
  * reservation obligations, compliance and other operational elements
  * integrated market reforms.


The consultation hub will be open for submissions until **30 June 2026**. We invite you to [read the draft Design Framework and have your say(external link)](https://consult.dcceew.gov.au/domestic-gas-reservation-scheme-draft-design-framework).
