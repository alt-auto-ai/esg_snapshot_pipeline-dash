As councils across Australia roll out food organics and garden organics (FOGO) bins, they face a big challenge: contamination. One solution is using artificial intelligence (AI) to detect contamination.
AI can check bins as they are collected, which is cheaper and safer than current methods. It also helps councils teach residents what belongs in FOGO bins and manage households that have contaminants in their waste.
## Why Use AI?
Most councils check bins by hand or review camera footage later. These methods are slow, unpleasant, and sometimes unsafe. They often don’t catch problems in time. AI can complete the same tasks in seconds using sensors and GPS.
In 2020, the City of Canterbury–Bankstown tested AI to monitor contamination in recycling bins. The trial showed AI could detect common contaminants more than 80% of the time. This success led to the independent development of CleanBins and the 2024 FOGO Pilot Study.
## The CleanBins AI Pilot
CleanBins, an Australian AI company, worked with 3 Victorian councils to test AI ability to find contamination in FOGO bins. Cameras were mounted on waste trucks to check bins during collection. The study included:
  * researching council needs
  * installing cameras and data systems in trucks
  * developing and training the AI system
  * building a user-friendly dashboard.


The AI system learned to identify waste types and contaminants using thousands of images. It improved over time as more data was added. Quality checks showed the system was accurate and scalable.
The pilot software gave councils real-time dashboards, which allowed councils to monitor contamination, track performance of different waste streams and identify contamination hotspots. Councils could track trends, set rules for alerts, and create cases for repeat offenders.
The successful pilot was completed in December 2024. CleanBins is confident that AI will give councils better, faster, and in the long-term, cheaper data than manual audits.
