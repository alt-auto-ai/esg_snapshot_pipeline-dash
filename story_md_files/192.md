# Australia and UK partner to strengthen AI safety and security
25 May 2026
**Joint media release with Minister for Industry and Innovation and Minister for Science, Tim Ayres**  
  
The Australian and UK Governments have today signed a new Memorandum of Understanding (MoU), deepening cooperation on the responsible development, deployment, governance and use of safe and trustworthy AI.
The MoU was signed during the visit to Australia by UK Minister for AI and Online Safety, Kanishka Narayan MP and Australia’s Assistant Minister for Science, Technology and the Digital Economy, Dr Andrew Charlton MP. 
The new arrangement will deepen cooperation between the Australian AI Safety Institute and the world-leading UK AI Security Institute. 
Under the MoU, Australia and the UK will collaborate on AI safety and security by:
  * Sharing information and expertise on emerging AI capabilities and risks, and best-practices for testing AI systems
  * Conducting joint research, including novel approaches to measure, test and manage risks
  * Supporting the International Network for Advanced AI Measurement, Evaluation and Science, of which Australia and the UK are both founding members. 


The MoU supports the National AI Plan’s goal of keeping Australians safe by strengthening international cooperation on AI risks and safety. 
Both Australia and the UK are committed to harnessing the opportunities of AI, by understanding and mitigating its risks. 
The work progressed between our AI Safety and Security Institutes can help governments keep pace with rapidly evolving technologies so the benefits can be broadly shared. 
As part of the National AI Plan the Government will establish an AI Safety Institute. The AI Safety Institute will monitor, test and share information on emerging AI capabilities, risks and harms, and ensure Australia has the capability to keep up with developments in AI technologies.
Further information about the MOU [is available here](https://www.industry.gov.au/publications/overview-memorandum-understanding-artificial-intelligence-safety-and-security-collaboration-between-australia-and-uk).

The Allan Labor Government is undertaking a winter blitz of works to slash congestion and make life easier. 
Melton will be a step closer to becoming boom gate free, when the Ferris Road level crossing is removed this winter. 
Crews will finish building the new road bridge over the rail line and continue work to build the new Melton Station – with Ferris Road to be closed to traffic from 23 June to 15 July. 
Coaches will replace trains on sections of the Ballarat Line from 22 July until last service on 26 July. There will be further changes to train services in August, with dates to be confirmed closer to the time.
Level crossing removal works will also progress at:
  * Coburns and Exford roads in Melton – with – with road closures expected at times throughout August and September
  * Maddox Road in Newport, closed between 18 June to 1 July


Buses will also replace trains on sections of the Belgrave line for a week in late July to upgrade Boronia Station, set to reopen to passengers in spring.
Over the weekends of 13 to 14 June and 27 to 28 June, buses will replace sections of the Frankston, Cranbourne, Sunbury and Pakenham lines, including all Metro Tunnel services, to carry out vital maintenance works near Armadale Station. 
To make the most of the quieter July school holidays, the Metro Tunnel will also close to remove temporary tracks from 9pm Tuesday, 7 July until last train on Sunday, 12 July. 
Flinders Street will be closed to westbound traffic between Russell Street and Swanston Street/St Kilda Road from 9:00pm Thursday, 6 August to Tuesday, 11 August, to reinstate the road and kerbs. 
As part of North East Link works, the Eastern Freeway will close between Chandler Highway and Tram Road from 10:00pm Friday, 19 June to 5:00am Monday, 22 June to asphalt new freeway lanes – with further monthly weekend closures to be confirmed closer to the time. 
There will be lane closures at the intersection of Whitehorse Road and Station Street in Box Hill as demolition works begin to prepare for the construction of the new Suburban Rail Loop station, and tunnelling to begin later this year.
The Point Cook Road and Central Avenue Intersection Upgrade will close the Point Cook Road bridge over the Princes Freeway from 10:00pm, Sunday 28 June until mid-September. Delays of up to 30 minutes are expected in peak periods.
In Clyde North, the Thompsons Road and Berwick-Cranbourne Road intersection will be closed from 26 June to 9 July while crews will ramp up work on the intersection upgrade.
Work on road projects at Ison Road in Werribee, Ballan Road in Wyndham Vale, Ferntree Gully Road, and Clyde Road in Berwick will mean closures at times throughout winter.
For information about the planned disruptions over winter visit bigbuild.vic.gov.au/disruptions.
