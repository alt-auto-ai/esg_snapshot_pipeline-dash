#  New release: Energy in Europe - 2026 edition 
![Solar panels on the green fiels bellow wind turbines.](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/documents/4187653/16281667/Nuttsue_Shutterstock_1912980682_RV.jpg)
© Nuttapon/stock.adobe.com
Energy is the foundation of modern societies, powering our homes, schools, hospitals, industries and transport. A reliable and sustainable energy supply helps us maintain our quality of life and foster economic growth. But how much do we know about where our energy comes from, how we use it, and what is its impact on the environment?
The 2026 edition of [Energy in Europe](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/web/interactive-publications/energy-2026) provides an overview of the latest energy trends in the [EU](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/index.php?title=Glossary:European_Union_\(EU\)), covering energy production, energy consumption, and its relationship with the environment. Through short text and interactive visuals, you can gain a complete picture of the energy market, including energy production and imports, electricity production and prices, greenhouse gas emissions, and the rise of [renewable energy sources](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/index.php?title=Glossary:Renewable_energy_sources).
Did you know that in 2024, 48% of the energy produced in the EU came from renewable sources? Or that 31% of energy used in the EU was consumed by transport activities? Did you know that, in the first half of 2025, the electricity prices for households ranged from €10 per 100 kWh in Hungary to €38 in Germany? Would you like to know how your country compares? Explore [Energy in Europe](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/web/interactive-publications/energy-2026) and learn more.
