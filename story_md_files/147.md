In this context, the Government faces many hard decisions. Even considering just the narrow scope of decarbonising transport, the Government has at least five consequential and controversial issues to contend with: subsidies for electric vehicles (EVs), subsidies for EV charging infrastructure, network pricing for behind-the-meter and rapid EV charging, road user charges for EVs, and fuel excise.
Each of these is part of the bigger question: how do all Australians receive an equitable share of the benefits and carry an equitable share of costs of the transition to a clean transport system?
In addressing this question, it is worth reflecting upon the experience of decarbonising Australia’s electricity system. In particular, the importance of building and maintaining people’s trust in the transition as a project that serves individual, community and national interests, with equitably distributed costs and benefits.
