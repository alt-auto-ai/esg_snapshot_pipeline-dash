# Statement to the Senate Finance and Public Administration Committee
  * Date: 01.06.2026
  * Type: Speeches 


**Statement by Dr Raphael Arndt, Chief Executive Officer of the Future Fund Management Agency, to the Senate Finance and Public Administration Committee**
**Budget Estimates, 28 May 2026, Parliament House, Canberra**
****
Since the Future Fund’s inception in 2006, investment returns have added $208.5 billion to its value. The Future Fund is delivering against its long-term investment mandate with a 10-year return of 8.6% per annum, well above the mandate target of 7.1% per annum.
In the 12 months to 31 March 2026, the Future Fund delivered an investment return of 11.7%, adding $28.3 billion to the portfolio. The value of the Future Fund now sits at a record $269.1 billion. The Future Fund’s strong investment performance has continued in an environment of geopolitical unrest and significant market upheaval in recent months. This reflects the efforts we have undertaken to increase the portfolio’s resilience during periods of market volatility, as discussed in our position papers.
The value of the six other funds managed by the Future Fund Board of Guardians has now increased to $68.1 billion. Total funds managed by the Board have risen to over $337 billion.
On the operational front, we are making prudent changes to position the Future Fund Management Agency for ongoing success. Following a period of investment in the Agency’s data and technology capabilities, we are now realising the benefits. The Agency expects to deliver material cost savings of around $10 million to $15 million in the 2026-27 financial year and further savings in subsequent years.
Since we last appeared before this Committee, we were pleased to announce the appointment of Richard Brandweiner as the Fund’s Chief Investment Officer. We look forward to Richard taking up his position on 1 July.
We also recently welcomed news of the appointment of two new members of the Board of Guardians, Ms Fiona Trafford-Walker and Mr Adam Tindall. Ms Trafford-Walker commenced her term on 12 May and Mr Tindall will commence his term in September. We would like to take this opportunity to thank outgoing Board members Mrs Patricia Cross AM and Dr Deborah Ralston for their valued service to the Future Fund over the past five years.
I welcome your questions.
