#  Aquaculture success on the South Coast
The NSW Government is partnering with 10 aquaculture businesses to invest $14.3 million into strengthening the South Coast’s oyster, kelp and seafood sector—driving sustainability, productivity and regional jobs.
![](https://www.investregional.nsw.gov.au/sites/default/files/2026-05/Seaweed%20product%20-%20landscape.jpg)
This co-investment is modernising operations, accelerating innovation and unlocking new economic opportunities across coastal NSW.
Funded projects include:
**Ocean2Earth – $1.88 million**   
scaling marine waste recovery and biofertiliser production to industrial levels, creating jobs and enabling circular economy outcomes.
**Wapengo Rocks Wild Organic Oysters – $1.28 million**  
modernising oyster farming with automated infrastructure, solar power and expanded premium production.
**Tollgate Oysters – $959,000**  
developing shared oyster farming infrastructure, including floating systems, wet storage and processing facilities.
**Auskelp Pty Ltd – $897,000**  
advancing offshore kelp farming through a specialised research vessel and large-scale production trials.
**Ritchie Oysters Pty Ltd – $684,000**  
boosting productivity with automated systems and expanded onshore processing.
**Sandingroove Oysters – $544,000**  
expanding automation, cold-chain and solar capability—doubling production and supporting jobs.
**Sapphire Coast Oysters – $465,000**  
upgrading nursery and farm infrastructure to improve spat survival and double output by 2030.
**OysterLife Unit Trust – $348,000**  
transitioning to net-zero operations through electrification and cleaner energy solutions.
**Ballantyne Commercial Fishing – $211,000**  
enhancing refrigeration systems to improve product quality, safety and storage capacity.
**Glasshouse Rock Oysters – $139,000**  
introducing automated grading and new floating infrastructure to improve resilience and quality.
The NSW Government is actively promoting the opportunities within the aquaculture sector via the Aquaculture Vision Statement which aims to double the sector’s value to $300 million by 2030— and programs like the $20 million Aquaculture Industry Development Program already delivering results.
Complementing this work, the NSW Seaweed Investment Prospectus (released in 2024) highlights the significant potential of the state’s seaweed industry - showcasing opportunities in food production, bioproducts, carbon reduction and export growth, and positioning NSW as a future leader in sustainable aquaculture.   
  
Together, these initiatives are building a more resilient, innovative and sustainable seafood sector—delivering high-quality, low-carbon products for domestic and global markets.
For in-depth data around the opportunity, read the [NSW Seaweed Investment Prospectus](https://www.investregional.nsw.gov.au/sites/default/files/publications/2025-04/Seaweed_prospectus.pdf).
