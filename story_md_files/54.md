Updated: 12 March 2026 
AFCA has published its updated Rules and Operational Guidelines (OGs), which come into effect on 12 March 2026 following extensive consultation with industry, consumer groups, paid representatives and other stakeholders, and subsequent ASIC approval.
The updated Rules and OGs reflect changes designed to strengthen fairness, transparency and efficiency across the AFCA scheme.
AFCA incorporated stakeholder feedback received through 39 formal submissions and wide engagement activity during the 2025 public consultation period.
**What has changed?**
  * An amendment to AFCA’s jurisdiction to include the ability to investigate scam-related complaints involving receiving banks and unauthorised accounts opened by scammers in the complainant’s name. This followed a change to AFCA’s Authorisation Conditions by the Federal Government which introduced this change. _[Read more](https://www.afca.org.au/members/members-resources/receiving-banks-and-unauthorised-opening-of-accounts/member-faqs)._
  * The introduction of Rule A.11.6, allowing AFCA to publish the names of financial firms that fail to comply with AFCA Determinations. _[Read more](https://www.afca.org.au/members/member-faq#Rule%20A.11.6%20%E2%80%93%20publishing%20names%20of%20financial%20firms%20that%20fail%20to%20comply%20with%20AFCA%20Determinations)._
  * New obligations for paid representatives.
  * Removal of legacy complaints provisions (Section F).
  * A minor clarification in the Operational Guidelines referring to how AFCA interprets the definition of a small business credit facility in the AFCA Rules. The change is to ensure the Operational Guidelines align with AFCA Rule C.1.3(b).


You can find the updated Rules and Operational Guidelines [here](https://www.afca.org.au/about-afca/rules-and-guidelines).
