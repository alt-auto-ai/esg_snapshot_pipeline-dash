With deep sadness, and on behalf of Ms. Shaikha Al Nuwais, Secretary-General of the World Tourism Organization (UN Tourism), the global tourism community joins the UN family to mourn the passing of Mr. Rafeeuddin Ahmed. A distinguished diplomat, his career of more than three decades was defined by an unwavering dedication to multilateralism and the ideals of the United Nations.

Mr. Ahmed brought to his work an exceptional command of the intricacies of intergovernmental processes. Throughout his long service, including his role as Special Representative to the United Nations in New York, he became a vital bridge between institutions, Member States and Secretariat entities.
Beyond titles and milestones, it was Mr. Ahmed’s character that left the deepest impression. A respected representative of Pakistan, to colleagues and friends, he was simply “Raffee” — gracious, principled, and unfailingly generous with his time and wisdom. Colleagues recall his thoughtful counsel, his ability to navigate complex negotiations with composure and tact, and his unwavering respect for the diversity within the UN family. He embodied the very spirit of international civil service: impartial, principled and guided by the common good.
His legacy lives on in the strengthened voice of tourism within the global development agenda And the impact of his work continues to resonate in conference halls, negotiated texts and partnerships forged in the service of sustainable development.
As we pay tribute to Mr. Rafeeuddin Ahmed (*), we express our profound gratitude for his contribution, commitment and professionalism. He touched the lives of many within the United Nations and beyond, leaving behind a legacy defined not only by institutional achievements, but by humanity, wisdom and grace.
**May he rest in peace.**
