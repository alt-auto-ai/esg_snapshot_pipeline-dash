# Secretary-General, in Video Message to Global Counter-Terrorism Compact, Urges Response Anchored in International Law, Human Rights Vital amid Growing Terrorist Threat
Following is the text of UN Secretary-General António Guterres’ video message to the twelfth meeting of the Global Counter-Terrorism Compact, in New York today:
Thank you for taking part in this twelfth meeting of the Global Counter-Terrorism Coordination Compact.
The United Nations stands for peace, human rights, and sustainable development. Terrorism stands against it. It destabilizes societies, fuels polarization, and inflicts suffering on communities in every region. As the global terrorist threat grows more complex, our response must remain firmly anchored in international law, human rights, and prevention.
The Global Counter-Terrorism Coordination Compact is central to this effort. It helps ensure that the United Nations acts with unity and coherence — bringing together 46 entities to design and deliver tailored support to Member States. The Compact has also attracted increasing engagement from Member States themselves. This reflects a shared recognition that coordinated, networked, and multi-partner approaches are essential to meet the scale and nature of today’s threats.
Working in a more agile, integrated, and effective way is also the spirit of the UN80 Initiative. The new system-wide Unified Results Framework will help us demonstrate — with clarity and transparency — the collective impact of our counter-terrorism efforts.
Looking ahead, we have important milestones: The fourth High-Level Conference on Counter-Terrorism and the ninth review of the Global Counter-Terrorism Strategy as it marks its twentieth anniversary. Together, let’s keep working — with determination and resolve — to build a future free from terrorism.
