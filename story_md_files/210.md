# NPWS hazard reduction burn in Morton National Park
24 April 2026 
Department media release Hazard reduction burn Fire National Parks and Wildlife Service Morton National Park News and media releases
The NSW National Parks and Wildlife Service (NPWS) is undertaking a large hazard reduction burn in the Shoalhaven Region within Morton National Park, tomorrow, Saturday 25 April.
Fire crews will be undertaking the 1,680 hectares burn, approximately 15 kilometres north of Nerriga, in the vicinity of Tolwong and Touga roads.
The aim of this burn is to reduce the spread of bushfire within the park and increase protection for neighbours and the surrounding community.
Motorists are reminded to drive to conditions and follow any safety signs and directions from fire crews as smoke may impact the area.
NPWS, along with other agencies, has numerous prescribed burns planned across NSW when conditions allow.
For their safety, people must not enter closed areas while the operations are underway.
Roads and trails will be reopened once the areas are assessed as safe. Visitors must check [NPWS Alerts (link is external)](https://www.nationalparks.nsw.gov.au/alerts/alerts-list)for up-to-date information on closures.
People with known health conditions can sign up to receive air quality reports, forecasts and alerts via [email or SMS from the Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water(link is external)](https://www.airquality.nsw.gov.au/subscribe-to-air-quality-updates).

More information on hazard reduction activities is available at [NSW Rural Fire Service(link is external)](https://www.rfs.nsw.gov.au/) and the NSW Government's [Hazards Near Me website and app(link is external)](https://www.nsw.gov.au/emergency/hazards-near-me-app).
