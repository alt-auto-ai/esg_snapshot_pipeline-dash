Labor is upgrading public libraries across the state so families can enjoy more free spaces close to home. 
The Government has opened a new grant round for the Living Libraries Infrastructure Program. 
Libraries give Victorians free access to the tools and spaces they need to study, work, learn and stay connected.
Councils can apply for grants ranging from $10,000 to $1 million for projects of all sizes. 
Funding will support refurbishments, expanded children’s areas, technology hubs, meeting pods and accessibility upgrades.
Labor has already delivered 176 library infrastructure projects — building new libraries and upgrading existing ones. 
Applications will open on 1 June and close on 17 July. 
For more information and to apply, visit [localgovernment.vic.gov.au](http://www.localgovernment.vic.gov.au/lgv-funding-programs/living-libraries-infrastructure-program). 
**Quotes attributable to Minister for Local Government Paul Hamer**
_“Libraries make life easier — giving families free access to technology, learning and essential local services.”_
_“We’re upgrading libraries across the state so more Victorians can access safe, modern and welcoming spaces close to home.”_
**Quote attributable to Victorian Public Library Ambassador Andrea Rowe**
_“Libraries are welcoming spaces where people of all ages can learn, create and gather, showing the vital role libraries play in supporting literacy, access to knowledge and community connection.”_
