Updated: 10 March 2026 
AFCA has published a new e-learning module that provides insight into AFCA’s processes for complaints that include proximate cause as an issue.
Proximate cause is a general insurance term used to describe an event that is considered the dominant cause of loss or damage. For example, if a tree branch falls during a storm and damages a roof, which then results in water entering the home and causing further damage, the storm can be considered the proximate cause of all the damage.
Insurance complaints received by AFCA are often related to disagreements between consumer and insurer over the proximate cause of damage, and whether it is covered by the policy. Often, the consumer will believe that the proximate cause of any loss or damage is covered by their insurance policy, while the insurer will dispute this or say the proximate cause was an excluded event.
Completing the module will support understanding of how AFCA approaches proximate cause and weighing evidence, the relevant evidence and submissions to make when making claim decisions involving proximate cause issues. This should help to reduce complaints, or resolve them earlier, and improve timeliness and fairness for consumers and our members.
The module takes around 15 minutes to complete and includes case examples and interactive questions.
You can access the module on the member resources page of our website or [here](https://www.afca.org.au/members/members-resources/elearn/proximate-cause).
AFCA has also published its Approach to proximate cause of damage, which can be read [here](https://www.afca.org.au/what-to-expect/how-we-make-decisions/afca-approaches).
