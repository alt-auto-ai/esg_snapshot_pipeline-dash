#  Building resilient Reef wetlands

We are investing $50 million over 5 years to enhance the health of wetlands across the Reef catchment.
This will build on Australia’s efforts to improve Reef water quality.
Wetlands are important for water quality. They are natural filters for water flowing from the land to the Reef.
The Reefwise Wetlands Program will support projects that:
  * rehabilitate and restore large-scale coastal wetlands and riparian systems
  * use targeted, whole-of system restoration techniques that deliver significant water quality benefits
  * manage invasive weeds and animals, such as wild pigs
  * revegetate mangroves, native sedges, grasses and riparian vegetation
  * restore and rehabilitate land to help manage erosion and prevent run-off.


The Australian Government's Reef Trust funds the Reefwise Wetlands Program. The program contributes to Reef 2050 Plan priorities.
It is part of a suite of Reefwise Programs. These programs are accelerating efforts to improve water quality.
  * The Reefwise Farming Program is reducing nutrient and pesticide run-off from farms
  * The Reefwise Urban Program will reduce water pollution from urban sources
