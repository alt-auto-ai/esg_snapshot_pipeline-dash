The Australian and Queensland governments have commenced a review of the Reef 2050 Long‑Term Sustainability Plan.
The review is being conducted by the Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water in collaboration with the Queensland Government and the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Authority.
The Reef 2050 Plan has been successfully guiding investment and management actions across the Reef since 2015.
The review will ensure sure the updated plan is a strong and forward focused framework to help the Reef respond to key threats and climate change.
It will also build on ongoing work, such as:
  * improving water quality
  * supporting Traditional Owner partnerships
  * world class marine park management.


The Reef 2050 Plan is supported by major investment. This includes the Australian Government’s $1.2 billion commitment to protect the Reef. Together with Queensland Government funding, more than $5 billion has been invested in the Reef since 2014.
The updated plan will guide long‑term action to protect one of Australia’s most important natural and cultural places.
