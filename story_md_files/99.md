Consultation is now open on the National Hydrogen Regulatory Guidebook: Pipelines.
This document is the third part of an anticipated series of four guidance documents.
Pipelines are crucial infrastructure for transporting large-scale hydrogen from production to demand centres. But industry knowledge of the regulation of hydrogen pipelines is still developing.
The guidebook focusses on their:
  * design
  * construction
  * maintenance repurposing.


This includes:
  * regulatory obligations
  * Australian and international standards
  * industry guidance.


These Guidebooks will support the hydrogen industry to grow by:
  * providing a roadmap to navigating Australia’s regulatory frameworks
  * identifying and clarifying existing regulatory obligations that apply to the hydrogen industry
  * providing environmental and safety assurance.


We are seeking stakeholder feedback on whether the Guidebooks are:
  * accurate and correct
  * comprehensive
  * usable.


The Guidebooks deliver on regulatory outcomes identified by the [2024 National Hydrogen Strategy](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/energy/publications/australias-national-hydrogen-strategy "Australia’s National Hydrogen Strategy").
Submissions can be made to the [Pipeline Guidebook consultation(external link)](https://consult.dcceew.gov.au/national-hydrogen-regulatory-review-guidebooks). Submissions close at **5:00 pm (AEST) on Monday 13 April 2026.**
