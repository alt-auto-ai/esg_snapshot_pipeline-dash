# Submission on the Australian tyre industry
![](https://alga.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/tyres.jpg)
In 2022-2023, councils handled an estimated 300,000 end-of-life tyres — at a cost of $6.5 million each year to local governments.
Local government is keen to see a truly circular tyre industry which improves recovery rates, prevents illegal dumping, and seizes the opportunities of using crumb rubber at scale.
The Australian Local Government Association’s [submission](https://alga.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/2026_ALGA-Submission_HoR-Tyres-Inquiry.pdf) to the Senate Standing Committee on Industry, Innovation and Science’s inquiry into the current state of the Australian tyre industry outlines the challenges and costs local governments bear for end-of-life tyres, and the criticality of a national mandatory product stewardship scheme to drive improvement.
