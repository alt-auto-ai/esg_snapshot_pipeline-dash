#  Energy upgrades roll out across social housing in TAS

![a person installing an air conditioning unit](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/sites/default/files/styles/cards_responsive/public/images/energy-upgrades-roll-out-across-social-housing-tas-news-tile.jpg?itok=A39pT4Eh)
Tasmanian social housing residents will soon start to see the benefits of energy upgrades. This work is now underway across the state.
These upgrades will help keep homes warmer during Tasmania’s cold winters. They will also help households reduce their energy bills during the cost-of-living crisis.
So far, Tasmania has delivered energy upgrades to 1,700 social housing properties.
Approximately 1,200 more homes will soon receive upgrades, including:
  * double glazing
  * electric heat pump hot water systems
  * thermal insulation
  * LED lighting.


Many social housing homes in Tasmania are among the least energy efficient in the state. Most were built before minimum energy standards were in place.
The Australian Government has invested $14.6 million for these additional upgrades through the expanded Social Housing Energy Performance Initiative (SHEPI).
SHEPI is a $1.1 billion nationwide program. The Australian Government is working in partnership with states and territories to improve the energy efficiency of around 100,000 public and community housing properties.
More than 26,000 properties in social or community housing nationwide have so far benefited from the SHEPI program.
