# Activities of Secretary-General in Japan, 17-20 May
The Secretary-General arrived in Tokyo, Japan, on the afternoon of Sunday, 17 May.
On Monday, 18 May, the Secretary-General attended an event at UN University with the Minister for Foreign Affairs of Japan, [Motegi Toshimitsu](https://dam.media.un.org/Assets/V2/ChFVTjdTNTAwMDAwMDAwMDAwMxIPVFIxX1dBVEVSTUFSS0VEGjVcVFIxX1dBVEVSTUFSS0VEXDMwXDM3XDVmXGU2XDgzXHYyX1VON1xVTjcxMTM5MjEwLmpwZyIECAEQD0ABYgpVTjcxMTM5MjEw~/2AM9LOZJSI5Z/_@SG_1ysPLcKuqOI/UN71139210.jpg), entitled “Celebrating 70: Japan—UN Cooperation, Expo 2025 & the Future of Multilateralism”, held as Japan marks the seventieth anniversary of its admission to the United Nations.
In his [remarks](https://press.un.org/2026/sgsm23134.doc.htm), the Secretary-General said that he has visited Japan more than 20 times in his capacity as UN High Commissioner for Refugees and as Secretary-General. He has always found strategic cooperation and support that benefited millions of people around the world. The Secretary-General added that the spirit of working together for human progress was on full display at Expo 2025 and the UN Pavilion in Osaka in Japan.
Also on Monday, 18 May, the Secretary-General held a meeting with the Prime Minister, [Takaichi Sanae](https://dam.media.un.org/Assets/V2/ChFVTjdTNTAwMDAwMDAwMDAwMxIPVFIxX1dBVEVSTUFSS0VEGjVcVFIxX1dBVEVSTUFSS0VEXDNiXGQ5XGE0XGY1XGIzXHYxX1VON1xVTjcxMTM5MjUzLmpwZyIECAEQD0ABYgpVTjcxMTM5MjUz~/2AM9LOZJ3HAC/iieMfq2q0QW8G8lK/UN71139253.jpg)**.** In the [meeting](https://www.un.org/sg/en/content/sg/readouts/2026-05-18/readout-of-the-secretary-generals-meeting-he-ms-takaichi-sanae-prime-minister-of-japan), they exchanged views on global and regional peace and security, including the situations on the Korean Peninsula and in the Middle East, as well as on UN reforms.
On Tuesday, 19 May, the Secretary-General chaired the biannual session of the UN System Chief Executives Board for Coordination, also known as the CEB. These sessions bring together the heads of UN system organizations. The Chief Executives Board members are reflecting on current world affairs as they affect and are related to the UN system, while engaging in deliberations on “Megatrends: Transforming the World” and the UN80 Initiative. During the meetings, the Secretary-General and the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights, Volker Türk, launched the United Nations Human Rights Group, which will strengthen the promotion and coordination of human rights across the UN system.
On Wednesday, 20 May the Secretary-General concluded chairing the biannual meeting of the Chief Executives Board (CEB). Later in the day, the Secretary-General had an audience with His Majesty the Emperor of Japan. He also held a meeting with the Minister for Foreign Affairs Motegi Toshimitsu, where discussions focused on the UN–Japan partnership, with the Secretary-General underscoring that the Organization has benefited enormously from Japan’s generosity and commitment to the multilateral system.
Later in the day on Wednesday, the Secretary-General held a press conference at the Japan Press Club in Tokyo. In [remarks](https://www.un.org/sg/en/content/sg/press-events/2026-05-20/secretary-generals-remarks-the-press-conference-tokyo), the Secretary-General said that he is determined to further strengthen that partnership for the future, noting that the true hallmark of a partnership is revealed in times of crisis.
He underscored the importance of the UN-Japan partnership, and said that among the people of Japan, “we've witnessed an enormous generosity in helping the UN operate in some of the world's most troubled places and support the people of the world with life-saving aid”.
He added that it is essential to immediately reestablish the freedom of navigation in and around the Strait of Hormuz, and to end all ceasefire violations and create conditions for a political solution to the conflict.
The Secretary-General left Japan in the early evening of Wednesday, 20 May.
