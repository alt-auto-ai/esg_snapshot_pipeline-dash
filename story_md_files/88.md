#  Review of NGER Method 2 for open-cut coal mines

![Image is of clouds against a blue sky, representing how reducing industrial emissions can clean the air.](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/sites/default/files/styles/cards_responsive/public/images/clouds-4-news-tile.jpg?itok=dWAWnCrh)
We have released the [terms of reference for the review of the National Greenhouse and Energy Reporting (NGER) scheme Method 2 for open-cut coal mines](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/climate-change/emissions-reporting/national-greenhouse-energy-reporting-scheme/review-nger-method-2 "Review of NGER Method 2 for estimating fugitive emissions from open-cut coal mines").
Stakeholders use Method 2 for estimating fugitive greenhouse gas emissions from open-cut coal mines in Australia. We want to ensure the method remains:
  * fit for purpose
  * reflects the most up to date science, data, technologies and practices.


This review progresses implementation of the [Australian Government’s response to the 2023 Climate Change Authority review of the NGER Scheme](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/about/reporting/obligations/government-responses/2023-cca-review-nger-legislation "Australia Government response to the 2023 CCA review of the National Greenhouse and Energy Reporting Legislation").
The terms of reference set out the issues under review. Targeted stakeholder consultations and submissions received from the [2025 NGER scheme public consultation(external link)](https://consult.dcceew.gov.au/national-greenhouse-and-energy-reporting-nger-scheme) helped identify these issues.
Learn about the [review’s process and timing](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/climate-change/emissions-reporting/national-greenhouse-energy-reporting-scheme/review-nger-method-2#process-timing "Review of NGER Method 2 for estimating fugitive emissions from open-cut coal mines"). We will also publish updates and details of the May 2026 public webinar on that page.
