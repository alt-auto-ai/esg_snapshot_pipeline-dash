# Blue Economy CRC Hosts European Union Economic and Trade Delegation
Blue Economy CRC Hosts European Union Economic and Trade Delegation
**From 2–6 March 2026, Tasmania welcomed a delegation from the European Union as part of a program led by the Tasmanian Government to explore opportunities for collaboration across advanced manufacturing, renewable energy, maritime industries, agritech and scientific research.**
During their visit, delegates met with the Tasmanian Government, research institutions and leading businesses to learn more about the state’s capabilities and innovation. These conversations are helping strengthen connections between Tasmania and the European Union, and open the door to future partnerships, trade and investment.
The delegation consisting of 22 participants from 17 European countries including Austria, Belgium, Croatia, Cyprus, Finland, France, Germany, Hungary, Latvia, Lithuania, Malta, the Netherlands, Poland, Spain, Ireland, Italy and Sweden, led by Ms Joanna Pocztowska, Head of Trade and Economic Affairs at the EU Delegation in Canberra, visited the Blue Economy CRC as one component of the broader Tasmanian program, to learn about our world-leading research and innovation in offshore renewable energy, aquaculture, and marine technologies.
Commencing the visit, our CEO, Angela Williamson, along with Research Director Prof Irene Penesis and Blue Policy and Planning Coordinator, Kelly Hoareau, provided an overview of the CRC’s mission, research programs and industry partnerships supporting the sustainable development of offshore ocean industries, followed by an opportunity for delegates to engage informally with BE CRC representatives and ask questions about the work underway.
> _“Hosting the EU Delegation allowed us to highlight how the Blue Economy CRC brings research, industry and government together to turn emerging ideas into solutions with real impact for sustainable offshore industries. It was a valuable opportunity to share the strength of our collaborative model with international visitors.”_ Angela Wiliamson, Blue Economy CRC CEO.
The following day, the delegation visited the Blue Economy CRC’s Hydrogen Production and Research Facility in Lutana, including a presentation and guided tour, providing delegates with insights into research being undertaken to support the development of offshore hydrogen production and integrated renewable energy systems. The session highlighted how research and industry collaboration can accelerate the deployment of new technologies to support decarbonisation and sustainable ocean industries.
> _“It was a pleasure to host the EU Delegation at the BE CRC Hydrogen Production and Research Facility. Opportunities like this highlight the importance of international collaboration as we advance offshore renewable energy and hydrogen innovation.”_ Professor Irene Penesis, Blue Economy CRC Research Director.
![](https://blueeconomycrc.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/EU-delegation--1024x683.png?x99404)
**Strengthening International Collaboration**
This program comes at a time of renewed focus on strengthening economic ties between Australia and Europe. Through hosting the delegation, the Blue Economy CRC provided a platform for European representatives to engage directly with Australian researchers and industry leaders working at the forefront of the blue economy.
The visit highlighted the potential for deeper collaboration between Europe and Australia in advancing sustainable offshore industries and emerging clean energy technologies. Their visit reflects growing international interest in Tasmania’s expertise in marine science, renewable energy and aquaculture, as well as opportunities for research partnerships and investment.

Commencing the visit, our CEO, Angela Williamson, along with Research Director Prof Irene Penesis and Blue Policy and Planning Coordinator, Kelly Hoareau, provided an overview of the CRC’s mission, research programs and industry partnerships supporting the sustainable development of offshore ocean industries, followed by an opportunity for delegates to engage informally with BE CRC representatives and ask questions about the work underway.
> _“Hosting the EU Delegation allowed us to highlight how the Blue Economy CRC brings research, industry and government together to turn emerging ideas into solutions with real impact for sustainable offshore industries. It was a valuable opportunity to share the strength of our collaborative model with international visitors.”_ Angela Wiliamson, Blue Economy CRC CEO.
The following day, the delegation visited the Blue Economy CRC’s Hydrogen Production and Research Facility in Lutana, including a presentation and guided tour, providing delegates with insights into research being undertaken to support the development of offshore hydrogen production and integrated renewable energy systems. The session highlighted how research and industry collaboration can accelerate the deployment of new technologies to support decarbonisation and sustainable ocean industries.
> _“It was a pleasure to host the EU Delegation at the BE CRC Hydrogen Production and Research Facility.
