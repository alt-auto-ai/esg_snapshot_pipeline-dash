Updated: 1 June 2026 
AFCA is consulting on amendments to its Rules from Monday 1 June to Friday 26 June 2026. The proposed amendments are a result of recent legislative changes about genetic testing protections in life insurance.
### Background
On 8 April 2026 the  _Treasury Laws Amendment (Genetic Testing Protections in Life Insurance and Other Measures) Act_ was passed, and it introduced a ban on life insurers soliciting or using protected genetic information when offering life insurance. The ban comes into force from 8 October 2026. AFCA is expected to handle complaints about the use of adverse genetic test results and to enable this, we’re proposing to update our Rules.
### Proposed changes
AFCA is proposing changes to Rule C.1.4b) and d) to enable it to consider complaints specifically about the use of adverse genetic testing results.
The consultation period runs from **Monday 1 June to Friday 26 June 2026**.
Subject to consultation and regulatory approval, amendments are expected to take effect from 8 October 2026.
For further details on the consultation and how to make a submission, please visit the [AFCA consultation page](https://www.afca.org.au/news/consultation).
