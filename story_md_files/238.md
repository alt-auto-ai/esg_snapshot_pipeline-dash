# Creole Culture ‘Tells the Story of Humankind’, Secretary-General Says in Video Message to International Event
Following is the text of UN Secretary-General António Guterres’ video message for the International Event on Creole Culture, in Praia today:
It is a pleasure to greet all those gathered in Praia for the International Event on Creole Culture.
Creole culture tells the story of humankind. A story of journeys and memory. Of centuries of encounters between peoples. Of the resilient creativity that emerges when cultures meet and mix. Creole is a living reminder that identity is not diminished by exchange. It is enriched by it.
In a world too often divided by fear and mistrust, the International Creole Conference shines as a beam of light, promoting a language of encounter, dignity and belonging. It shows us that diversity is not a line that separates us, but a chance to build stronger bonds and to reaffirm our common humanity.
I commend Cabo Verde for bringing together voices from many horizons in this spirit of dialogue and celebration.
Cabo Verde knows how culture can cross oceans and connect peoples — carrying the rhythms, traditions and stories that bind communities across the world.
May this meeting inspire us to keep building bridges, celebrating diversity and advancing peace and prosperity through mutual understanding.
