# Time to give up on VPPs?
By [Charis Palmer](https://theenergy.co/person/charis-palmer)
[Get The Energy in your inbox](https://theenergy.co/subscribe)
Australia’s energy sector hasn’t worked out whether it’s integrating consumer energy resources or managing them, battery storage and grid integration expert Lachlan Blackhall says, using a panel discussion at the Energy Consumers Australia conference to call time on virtual power plants (VPPs).
“ So 15 per cent of battery holders are currently participating in VPPs. And so my conjecture is that consumers have actually fundamentally rejected the value proposition of VPPs," Blackhall said.
"And the fact that they are choosing not to enroll in VPPs when they're being offered subsidies directly to do so, and instead they're taking subsidies where they simply get to have the asset, to me means that the trade off between public benefit and private good is simply not there.
"The age of the VPP might be over, and if that's true, that has really significant consequences for how we might operate the system.”
Earlier in the day EnergyAustraliia CEO Mark Collette also raised the red flag on VPPs, acknowledging the lack of trust by consumers in the energy sector was the major factor stemming uptake.
“We're asking customers to go through one of the biggest transitions they're ever going to see in an environment where they're not sure who they can trust across the world, across any of the participants,” Collette said.
“Where this comes to life is in things like virtual power plants…AEMO did this forecast saying: Hey, great, we've got all this distributed energy and it will be controllable by a virtual power plant, so we'll be able to build less centralised stuff and everybody wins.”
When consumers didn’t make their batteries available to the market, Colette said, governments decided it was “a money thing” adding incentives and subsidies.
“It's not a money thing, it's a trust thing, and it's a what's the right product that demonstrates I have value, to get there. That's the problem that retailers have to solve.”
He added that if the industry gets the right products and right structures in place to demonstrate value to customers, “and don't just say hey trust us,” then it will get the right outcome.
Meanwhile, Essential Energy chief executive officer John Cleland told the audience “maybe the VPP will be replaced by the VVPP, which is the virtual virtual power plant.”
“Where a lot of these functions start happening in a far more amorphous manner because of the emergence of digital tools and the internet of things and AI and everything that we are all currently grappling with.”
