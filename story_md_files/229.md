What if learning about soil could be as exciting as watching a story unfold? 
That’s the idea behind **Happy Toppy** , a new initiative helping children discover the hidden world beneath their feet and understand why it matters. 
Developed through the **SOILSTORIES project (Empowering Rural Youth Through Soil Literacy)** , the initiative is working with young people in rural communities to turn complex soil science into practical knowledge and everyday awareness. 
In Dağkadı Village in Bursa, Türkiye, around 110 primary and secondary school students took part in workshops to explore soil health. Using simple scientific methods, they analysed local soil samples - testing pH levels and key nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium - and learned how these affect crops, ecosystems, and food production. 
But the project goes beyond science alone. By combining experimentation with storytelling, SOILSTORIES introduced characters like Happy Toppy, Wiggly Wiggy, and Zinny, helping students connect with the subject in a fun and memorable way. A documentary capturing the experience brings these learning moments to a wider audience.
### Tackling a hidden challenge 
The project addresses a real problem in agriculture: a lack of trust. In many rural areas, farmers can be hesitant to adopt new technology or share data. By focusing on children, who are essentially the future farmers, the initiative is building long-term change.
When students learn about soil health, they go home and talk to their parents about it. This creates a ripple effect of trust and new ideas within the wider farming community.
So far, the initiative has reached more than **700 children** , including those from disadvantaged backgrounds, and continues to expand.

### Fresh agriculture discourse 
At its core, the project is about making agriculture and environmental science more accessible and more appealing to young people. 
Through regular videos, songs, and storytelling, Happy Toppy introduces topics such as soil health, biodiversity, and sustainable farming in ways that are easy to understand and engaging for children. 
The long-term vision is to build soil trust circles, where young people and experienced farmers can exchange knowledge, combining traditional farming wisdom with modern digital skills and citizen science.
By making agriculture and environmental science fun and accessible through songs and stories, Happy Toppy is doing more than just teaching a lesson. It's helping a new generation feel proud of their farming roots and giving them the tools to look after the land for years to come.
