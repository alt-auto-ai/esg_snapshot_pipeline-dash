Ministerial media release
# Griffith Base Hospital’s new carpark and main entry set to open 

Published:
    12 March 2026 

Released by:
    Minister for Regional Health
* * *
A new carpark and main entry to the new Griffith Base Hospital is set to open next week, providing patients, staff and visitors in the region with more convenient access to the new hospital.
From Tuesday, 17 March, the Griffith community will have direct access to the Emergency Department (ED) and Main Entry, delivered as part of the final stages of the $250 million Griffith Base Hospital Redevelopment.
The community will also benefit from improved and more accessible onsite parking near the new hospital’s main entrance and ED that has dedicated drop-off zones, disability parking and accessible parking for patients with limited mobility.
Commencing 17 March, the main driveway will be closed temporarily and public access to the hospital car park will be via Gate 7 on Noorebar Avenue. Changes to the existing main carpark will also take effect to improve traffic flow.
Patients and visitors will access the hospital via the new Main Entry instead of the temporary tunnel route through the old hospital. Signage is in place to assist drivers and pedestrians accessing the hospital.
Construction is progressing across the campus including work to complete the final stage of the new carpark, as well as the hospital forecourt and landscaping. A refurbishment of the Ambulatory Care Hub is also nearing completion and is expected to open next month.
The Griffith Base Hospital Redevelopment remains on track for completion in 2026, following the opening of the new hospital building in June last year.
For more information, watch the campus changes video: [nsw.gov.au/griffith-base-hospital-redevelopment](https://www.nsw.gov.au/health-and-wellbeing/health-infrastructure-projects/griffith-base-hospital-redevelopment "Griffith Base Hospital Redevelopment")
**Quotes attributable to Minister for Regional Health, Ryan Park:**
“The opening of the new carpark and main entry at the $250 million Griffith Base Hospital is a game changer for the community, making it faster and easier to access this state-of-the-art health facility.
“We’ve listened to the community and understand that close and convenient access to the hospital is extremely important for staff, patients and visitors. I look forward to seeing the final stages of the redevelopment completed later this year.”
**Quotes attributable to Member for Murray Helen Dalton:**
“The completion of this project is incredibly important to the people of Murray.
“This car park will be particularly important for those patients and families who have to travel significant distances to receive medical care at Griffith Base Hospital.”
**Quotes attributable to Labor Spokesperson for Murray Bob Nanva MLC:**
“The Minns Government is investing in both health services and infrastructure.
“Extra car parking near the main entrance will be particularly important for older people and people with disabilities, making it easier for them to get access to vital hospital services.”
