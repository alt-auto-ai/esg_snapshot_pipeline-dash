# Victoria heats up with major manufacturing expansion
News
**Published:** 1 June 2026 
![A spacious industrial facility with high ceilings and bright overhead lights. The area contains multiple large air conditioning units placed uniformly on wooden palates. The overall environment is clean and well-organised.](https://djsir.vic.gov.au/__data/assets/image/0011/2482193/Rinnai-Australia-expansion-news-hero.png)
Rinnai Australia is expanding its Braeside advanced manufacturing facility, which will secure 110 local jobs and many more across the supply chain.
The boost in R&D and manufacturing capabilities at the world-class facility will drive innovation across the company’s product range. This will increase their capacity to develop new:
  * heating
  * cooling
  * hot water technologies.


It will also scale-up the design and manufacture of energy-efficient appliance solutions. This will help advance Victoria’s clean energy transition.
Rinnai employs 442 staff across its 3 Victorian sites. The company is a major supplier of heating, cooling and hot water appliance solutions. They deliver high-quality products and services to local and global markets.
The company recently participated in our [Digital Jobs program](https://business.vic.gov.au/grants-and-programs/digital-jobs-program). The program helped staff build advanced digital and AI skills to strengthen manufacturing capability and support high-skilled jobs in Victoria.
Victoria has the largest manufacturing workforce in Australia, employing more than 260,000 people.
The [Victorian Industry Policy](https://djsir.vic.gov.au/priorities-and-initiatives/industry-policy) and the $150 million [Victorian Investment Fund](https://www.rdv.vic.gov.au/grants/victorian-investment-fund) are helping to grow the state’s industrial base. It secures investment in targeted areas like advanced machinery manufacturing, creating jobs and opportunities for Victorian businesses and workers.
