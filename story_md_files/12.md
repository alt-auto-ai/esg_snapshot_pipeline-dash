Updated: 12 May 2026 
AFCA has introduced call recording across case‑handling teams, as part of our ongoing work to improve the quality, accuracy and efficiency of how we investigate and resolve complaints.
### **What this means**
Calls that include certain AFCA staff are being recorded to ensure we have accurate records of conversations, reduce misunderstandings, and support our case work.
If your call is impacted, you will hear AFCA’s privacy notice before speaking to the AFCA staff member.
If you do not want your call to be recorded, you can let us know at the start of the call and the recording will be stopped.
Our full privacy policy and privacy notice, including information about how we use AI‑enabled tools, are available on our website at [afca.org.au/privacy](http://www.afca.org.au/privacy).
## FAQs
### **Will all calls to and from AFCA be recorded?**
We are introducing call recording to our case-handling teams in stages. All calls to AFCA’s Customer Service team are currently recorded.
From July 2026, all calls to and from case workers, ombudsmen, and our Rules, Systemic Issues and Service Complaints teams will be recorded.
Any time a call is going to be recorded, you will be alerted by a privacy statement before the call commences.
### **Can I opt out?**
Yes. If you do not want your call to be recorded, you can let us know at the start of the call and the recording will be stopped.
###  **What****happens to call recordings?**
Call recordings are stored as internal case records. This is so that we will have accurate records of our conversations with consumers and financial firms as we investigate a complaint.
We may also review calls for quality and training purposes to improve our service. 
### **Can I have access to the call recording?**
We do not share call recordings or transcripts between consumers and financial firms as part of our regular information exchange process, unless special circumstances apply. 
If you would like a copy of the call as part of a privacy access request, we can provide this, in line with our current privacy procedures. 
You can find out more about accessing your personal information on our website at [www.afca.org.au/privacy](https://www.afca.org.au/privacy).
### **Are financial firms and complainants both having calls recorded?**
Yes. If an AFCA staff member has call recording enabled, all calls to or from them will be automatically recorded (unless the party opts out.)
### **Can financial firms get access to a consumer’s calls, or vice versa?**
No. We are not sharing recordings or transcripts between complaint parties as part of our regular information exchange process, unless special circumstances apply.
