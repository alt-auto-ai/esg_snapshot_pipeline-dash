Biosecurity Queensland has confirmed the first detection of varroa mite in Queensland with resistance to synthetic pyrethroid treatments, including Bayvarol and Apistan, in South East Queensland.
Genetic testing by the Elizabeth Macarthur Agricultural Institute (EMAI) on 24 February 2026 identified mutations linked to resistance. These mutations are associated with reduced effectiveness of synthetic pyrethroid products. Testing at several Queensland sites—including locations where treatment failure was reported—confirmed reduced effectiveness of Bayvarol in those areas.
At this stage, the distribution of resistant mites appears limited. Further tracing and testing are underway to better understand the extent of spread and to guide management advice for industry. This is the first confirmed case of pyrethroid-resistant varroa mite in Queensland. While spread appears limited at present, there is potential for further movement over time.
Scientists are looking into how the resistance started. There are 3 possible reasons:
  * The mutation might have been in Australia all along but became more common now that varroa mite is endemic in the eastern states of Australia.
  * The mutation might have happened naturally because of the overuse of pyrethroids.
  * The mutation might have come from a new group of mites brought into Australia via illegal importation.


Virus screening is underway to determine if the resistant mites are the result of a new introduction into Australia along with additional testing of historic samples to determine if the gene mutation may have been present at low levels previously.
It is extremely important that beekeepers monitor their mite levels both before and after treatments to ensure mite populations have reduced.

Beekeepers should 
Beekeepers can help stop the spread of varroa mite by:
  * Checking mite levels before and after treatments to make sure they're working.
  * Following all instructions on treatment labels.
  * Not using expired or unapproved products.
  * Keeping hives clean and checking them regularly.
