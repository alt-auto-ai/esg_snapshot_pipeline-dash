Prime Minister Anthony Albanese will welcome the Prime Minister of Solomon Islands, The Hon Matthew Wale CBE, to Canberra for a Leaders’ Meeting on Wednesday, 3 June 2026.
The visit will mark Prime Minister Wale’s first official international visit since taking office in May 2026, and follows a phone call between leaders on Friday, 15 May 2026.
Prime Minister Wale will be accompanied by senior Cabinet ministers from the Solomon Islands Government.
Australia and Solomon Islands are committed to advancing a peaceful and stable region through enhancing our development and economic partnership, strengthening our security cooperation, and bolstering climate resilience.
Prime Minister Albanese will host Prime Minister Wale and his delegation at Parliament House to discuss opportunities for further economic, development and security cooperation.

### Topics
[Competition and exemptions](https://www.accc.gov.au/search?f%5B0%5D=topics%3A327)
The ACCC has issued a draft determination proposing to authorise the Clean Energy Council, members of the New Energy Tech Consumer Code Council and current and future signatories of the New Energy Tech Consumer Code. The authorisation would allow them to agree, sign up to, comply with and follow the code as amended.
We also propose to authorise the parties to continue operating under the current New Energy Tech Consumer Code while they transition to the proposed amended code.
Authorisation is granted for Division 1 of Part IV of the _Competition and Consumer Act 2010_ and sections 45, 46 and 47 of the Act.
The New Energy Tech Consumer Code is a voluntary industry code. It sets minimum standards of good practice and consumer protections that code signatories must meet when supplying new energy tech products and services to consumers. These include solar photovoltaic (PV) systems and battery storage systems.
The ACCC may grant an authorisation for proposed conduct where the likely public benefit resulting from the conduct outweighs the likely public detriment.
Authorisation protects the authorised parties from legal action under certain competition provisions of the Act when they engage in the authorised conduct.
We are satisfied that the likely public benefits from the proposed conduct are likely to outweigh any public detriment. However, the assessment of likely public benefits and likely public detriments is finely balanced.
The ACCC proposes to grant authorisation with conditions for 5 years.
The ACCC is seeking submissions in response to the draft determination by **Thursday 25 June 2026,** before making our final decision.
Further details about the application and how to make a submission are available on the ACCC’s[ public register](https://www.accc.gov.au/public-registers/authorisations-and-notifications-registers/authorisations-register/new-energy-tech-consumer-code-0).
