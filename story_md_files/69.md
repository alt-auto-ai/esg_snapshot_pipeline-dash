## About the ACCU Scheme
The [Australian Carbon Credit Unit (ACCU) Scheme](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/climate-change/emissions-reduction/accu-scheme/reviews-and-reforms/independent-review-accus "Independent Review of ACCUs 2022") supports landholders, communities and businesses to deliver projects that cut greenhouse gas emissions and increase carbon storage.
It plays a key role in Australia’s [net zero](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/about/news/setting-2035-target-path-net-zero "Setting our 2035 target and path to net zero") transition and mitigation efforts. Projects under the Scheme can deliver abatement through a range of activities, including changes to:
  * land management practices
  * technologies used
  * growing trees or regenerating native forests to store carbon.


We released an [ACCU Scheme progress report](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/about/news/reforming-accu-scheme-2023-25-progress-report "Reforming the ACCU Scheme: 2023–2025 progress report") in February 2026.
## The proposed amendments
The Australian Government is proposing amendments to the ACCU Scheme legislation to further improve the Scheme and bolster Scheme integrity in response to multiple independent [reviews](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/climate-change/emissions-reduction/accu-scheme/reviews-and-reforms "Reviews and reforms to the ACCU Scheme"), including:
  * the [Independent Review of ACCUs 2022](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/climate-change/emissions-reduction/accu-scheme/reviews-and-reforms/independent-review-accus "Independent Review of ACCUs 2022") (the Chubb Review)
  * regular 


These amendments are in the Carbon Credits and Other Legislation Amendment (Integrity and Transparency) Bill (the Bill) exposure draft. It includes changes to both the 
  * boost integrity
  * improve transparency
  * improve accountability
  * streamline the administration of the Schemes.


The final proposed legislation package will take account of feedback from this consultation and other stakeholder responses.
