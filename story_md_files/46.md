Updated: 26 May 2026 
The Australian Financial Complaints Authority (AFCA) has published the details of 4 members that have been expelled from AFCA.
In accordance with resolutions made by the AFCA Board of Directors on 21 May 2026:
  * AFCA has expelled 3 members due to the members being insolvent (as per section 3.4(a) of the [AFCA Constitution](https://www.afca.org.au/about-afca/corporate-information/constitution))
  * AFCA has expelled 1 member for a failure to comply with AFCA’s Constitution and/or Applicable Rules, a failure to pay monies owed to the company, and being insolvent (as per section 3.4(a) of the [AFCA Constitution](https://www.afca.org.au/about-afca/corporate-information/constitution)).


Members of AFCA are required to pay an annual membership levy, along with fees for any complaints received about them.
Being a member of AFCA ensures financial firms and Authorised Credit Representatives (ACRs) meet their legal obligations when they are operating as an Australian Financial Services Licensee, Australian Credit Licensee, are a Superannuation Trustee or are an ACR.
If an expelled member is still required by law to be an AFCA member, we encourage them to contact our Membership Team as soon as possible to discuss reinstatement of their AFCA membership. It is at the absolute discretion of the AFCA Board of Directors or its delegate whether to accept a request for reinstatement. Reinstatement is conditional on the member having rectified the matters which led to its expulsion, and subject to any other conditions deemed appropriate by the Board of Directors or delegate to address any concerns about the member’s past conduct.
