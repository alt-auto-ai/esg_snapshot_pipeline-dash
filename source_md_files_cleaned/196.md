14 May 2026 
Jo Palmer, Minister for Education 
The 2026–27 Tasmanian Budget includes $195 million for Libraries Tasmania to continue to provide welcoming community hubs that support learning, connection and opportunity for Tasmanians of all ages.
Libraries Tasmania is one of the most widely used public services in the state, with almost six in 10 Tasmanians accessing library services or programs each year and 95.5 per cent of users satisfied or very satisfied with the service.
Minister for Education, Jo Palmer, said this investment recognised the important role libraries continue to play as places to learn, connect, access services and feel supported.
“Libraries Tasmania is delivering real outcomes for communities across the state, and this funding ensures it is able to remain strong, modern and accessible,” Minister Palmer said.
“We are also increasing access to our libraries, with Devonport and Penguin libraries successfully trialling after-hours access for members.
“Lilydale Library will expand its access through the Libraries Open Access initiative later this year.”
The investment will support library services across Tasmania’s statewide network of public libraries, collections, digital access, programs and facilities that respond to changing community needs.
Libraries Tasmania is playing a key role in lifelong learning and digital inclusion, with almost 90 per cent of users reporting they learned something new through library programs or support, and more than 96 per cent finding the information they needed when using library services.
“We are delivering a strong economy and caring community, with libraries supporting Tasmanians of all ages to learn and connect,” Minister Palmer said.
“Our nation and the world are facing uncertain times, and that’s why we are focussed on delivering the services Tasmanians need.
“Our libraries are much more than books, providing free access to information, digital services, learning programs and safe community spaces, which is particularly important as more services move online.”
The $195 million investment supports the Government’s commitment to delivering services that strengthen communities, support participation and opportunity, and deliver practical benefits for Tasmanians across the state.
