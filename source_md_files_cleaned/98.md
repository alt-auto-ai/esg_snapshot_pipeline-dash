Regional NSW businesses are well positioned to benefit from the Australian Government’s $1.5 billion Future Made in Australia (FMA) Innovation Fund, a major investment designed to accelerate the development of new clean energy industries.

While the program supports national priorities, many of its focus areas align closely with the industries, infrastructure and workforce already present across regional NSW.
The fund is designed to strengthen Australia’s industrial capability while supporting the transition to a low-carbon economy. Key objectives include:
- Growing Australia’s manufacturing capability
- Strengthening domestic supply chains
- Building workforce skills in emerging industries
- Accelerating the commercialisation of new technologies
For regional NSW businesses, the program represents an opportunity to participate in nationally significant projects while expanding local industry capability.
Many of the fund’s priority areas match the existing industries, infrastructure and workforce found across regional NSW communities. 
**Green Metals ($750M)**  
Regional NSW is home to mining, heavy industry, freight networks and export pathways, all critical to the shift toward low-emissions iron, steel, alumina and aluminium production. 
**Renewable Energy Technology Manufacturing ($200M initial tranche)**  
Regional centres offer industrial land, skilled trades, and logistics access that suit the manufacturing of renewable energy components and supporting technologies. 
**Low Carbon Liquid Fuels ($250M)**  
With strong agricultural sectors and growing interest in bioenergy, regional NSW is well placed to support feedstocks, processing facilities and demonstration projects for sustainable aviation fuels and renewable diesel. For regional NSW businesses from manufacturers and processors to project developers, researchers and supply chain operators, this is a chance to be part of the next wave of nationally significant industry.
