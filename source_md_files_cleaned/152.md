Five Victorians share how TAFE is helping them reach their goals
TAFE can open doors to real opportunities. From apprenticeships and trades to caring and teaching, people across Victoria are following their dreams through TAFE.
When Jo lost both her dad and her best friend to cancer, she saw firsthand just how valuable a nurse’s work is. And when she discovered she could study nursing through Free TAFE, it felt like a door was opening to her.
Read how [free TAFE made Jo’s dream of becoming a nurse real](
Chris returned to study as a mature-aged student and is now an IT teacher, thanks to Free TAFE. After going to TAFE to add to his existing skills as an IT professional, he decided to gain his qualification in training and assessment. Now, he works at Chisholm Institute.
Read how [free TAFE helped student become teacher](
Apprentice engineer Madi and her classmates are gearing up to complete their TAFE training. They have the help of specialist teachers qualified in providing support with literacy, numeracy and digital skills. The support is integrated into their course for free, and is tailored to meet industry needs.
Read how [maths support helps future engineers find their groove](
Sahin and Alex both work in roles that support apprentices to complete their training. They’re passionate about helping apprentices with personal, learning and workplace challenges. ‘It's rewarding to know that you've had an impact or you've been able to help someone in their lives in some way,’ Sahin says.
