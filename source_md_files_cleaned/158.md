  * FIFA World Cup matches to be broadcast live at Northbridge Piazza
  * Providing a vibrant gathering place for supporters and building excitement ahead of Perth hosting AC Milan, FC Internazionale Milano, Juventus and Palermoin August
  * Cook Labor Government committed to supporting local businesses and vibrancy in the Perth CBD to keep the economy strong
Western Australian football fans will be able to experience the excitement of the FIFA World Cup in the heart of Perth, with matches to be broadcast live on the big screen at Northbridge Piazza throughout the tournament.
The Northbridge Piazza live site will provide a vibrant gathering place for supporters to cheer on the Socceroos and enjoy key matches featuring football powerhouses from around the globe, creating opportunities for WA's diverse multicultural communities to come together and celebrate the world game.
The initiative is expected to attract thousands of visitors to Northbridge, supporting local restaurants and businesses while showcasing the precinct as an entertainment destination.
It will also build excitement ahead of Perth hosting blockbuster matches between Italian clubs AC Milan, Serie A and Coppa Italia Champions FC Internazionale Milano, Juventus and Palermo in August as part of WA's Winter of Unmissable Sport.
To cater for the influx of patrons expected in venues across Perth during the World Cup, approved licensed venues will also be able to take advantage of extended trading arrangements, allowing fans to enjoy the atmosphere before and after games.
The Piazza will feature a range of fan engagement activities with Perth Glory and Football West during Socceroos broadcast matches. 
The FIFA World Cup, which gets underway on June 11, remains the most-watched sporting event on the planet and the Northbridge Piazza live site will provide local fans with a memorable way to experience the occasion.

**Comments attributed to Sport and Recreation Minister Rita Saffioti:**
"We're excited to bring the electric atmosphere of the FIFA World Cup to the Northbridge Piazza which will add vibrancy to our city centre and provide a boost for surrounding businesses.
"Events like this are an opportunity for Perth's avid football fans to come together and builds excitement ahead of Perth hosting AC Milan, FC Internazionale Milano, Juventus and Palermoin August.
"Whether you're supporting the Socceroos or cheering on another nation, the Piazza will be a place where our community can come together and be a part of one of the world's biggest sporting events.
"Celebrating major events such as the FIFA World Cup also help to inspire the next generation of football players, coaches and volunteers and encourage more people to get involved in sport."
**Comments attributed to Racing and Gaming Minister Paul Papalia:**
"Major sporting events create valuable opportunities for hospitality businesses, supporting local jobs and delivering economic benefits to the broader community.
"Extended trading arrangements will help local venues make the most of the increased visitation expected throughout the tournament.
"We encourage fans to enjoy the World Cup and take advantage of the fantastic dining and entertainment options available throughout Northbridge and the City."
**Comments attributed to City of Perth Lord Mayor Bruce Reynolds:**
"Northbridge Piazza is the perfect venue for football fans to gather and share the excitement of the FIFA World Cup.
"We expect the live broadcasts to create a fantastic atmosphere in Northbridge and the City, attracting visitors from across Perth and providing a welcome boost for local businesses.
"We encourage visitors to make a day or night of it by exploring Northbridge and the City's outstanding restaurants, bars and entertainment venues before and after the matches."
