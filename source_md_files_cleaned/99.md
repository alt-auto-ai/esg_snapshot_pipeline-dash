Regional New South Wales is set to benefit from a major boost to clean manufacturing, with the NSW Government investing $52 million to support renewable manufacturing projects across the state.
The investment forms part of the $480 million [Net Zero Manufacturing Initiative]( a program designed to fast-track emissions reductions while building the technologies needed for a low-carbon future.
The latest funding will unlock more than 100 construction jobs and create 67 ongoing roles in regional communities, while strengthening local supply chains and manufacturing capability.
Importantly, the initiative is already delivering significant results. Across NSW, more than 1,100 jobs are being supported through 40 projects that are helping position the state as a leader in clean and renewable manufacturing.

Building regional capability for the energy transition
The investments demonstrate how regional NSW is playing a central role in Australia’s transition to a low-carbon economy.
Beyond funding, the program focuses on building capability in regional communities and supporting manufacturers to scale their operations. By backing innovative projects and strengthening local supply chains, the initiative is helping ensure that the technologies powering Australia’s energy future are developed and manufactured in NSW.
Invest Regional NSW has supported project development and investment attraction by providing customised investment prospectuses, regional and site-selection expertise, alignment with NSW Government funding programs, and targeted connections with councils, industry and workforce and training providers.
Regional NSW isn’t just participating in the energy transition, it’s helping lead it.
