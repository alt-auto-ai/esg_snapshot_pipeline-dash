03 March 2026
The NSW Independent Planning Commission has today approved the Novus on Victoria build-to-rent development application (SSD-63324208) located at 
Novus Management Pty Ltd (the Applicant) sought approval for the demolition of existing buildings on the Site, bulk earthworks and construction of a 46-storey housing development comprising 260 build-to-rent apartments above retail premises on the ground and first floors. Public submissions on the proposal when it was exhibited by the Department of Planning, Housing and Infrastructure included 140 objections from the public. Willoughby City Council also opposed the development application.
The two-member Commission Panel, comprising Andrew Mills (Panel Chair) and Simon Smith, met with key stakeholders, conducted a site inspection and locality tour, held a public meeting where the Panel heard from eight speakers, and received 154 written submissions. The Commission approved the application as the delegate of the Minister for Planning and Public Spaces.
In its [Statement of Reasons for Decision]( "Statement of Reasons for Decision Novus on Victoria - Build-to-Rent \(SSD-63324208\)"), the Commission found that the application “supports the National Housing Accord, which sets a national target of delivering 1.2 million new homes over 5 years from mid-2024, with NSW’s share being 377,000 well-located homes”. The Commission noted that the proposed build-to-rent apartments would provide long-term tenancies in a highly accessible location supported by quality communal spaces for use by residents.
Key issues raised in submissions received by the Commission included local concerns about built form, residential amenity, view impacts, construction impacts (including to the Chatswood Mall markets) and future loading and servicing of the building. The Commission has imposed a range of conditions of consent to manage and mitigate impacts of these issues. These include conditions that require the Applicant to:
  * amend the design of the loading dock entry to ensure that delivery vehicles can be wholly contained within the site boundary and that no vehicles block the public footpath;
  * provide details of traffic management during construction to ensure that pedestrian connectivity and safety are maintained and how vehicle access to the markets will be managed;
  * prepare a carparking, loading and servicing management plan;
  * undertake public domain works to ensure the development does not have negative impacts to the surrounding roads and footpaths; and
  * work with Council to improve traffic congestion on Victor Street during construction and operation of the development.
