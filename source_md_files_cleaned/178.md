With possible increases to the cash-rate front of mind for Victorians, Labor is delivering support here and now.
Minister Staikos visited _Mortgage Stress Victoria_ today – Australia’s only free service exclusively dedicated to supporting people in mortgage stress to maintain secure housing.
The service – which received $4 million in the _Victorian Budget 2025/_ 26 – hosts a team of lawyers, social workers and financial counsellors who provide free advice to Victorians experiencing mortgage stress.
The Labor Government's investment will expand Mortgage Stress Victoria’s free services from 1 May. 
The changes mean households with an income of up to $90,000 will be able to access the free legal and financial support – up from $75,000 currently.
Mortgage Stress Victoria can help at any stage of mortgage stress, from situations in which payments are up to date to those in which there are court orders for repossession of a home.
The service started as a pilot in Melbourne’s western suburbs and before being expanded statewide in 2022.
As well as direct mortgage stress support, the Labor Government is providing real support where it's needed most while investing in the frontline services families rely on.
We know the cost of living is hitting families, which is why we launched free public transport for kids across the state this year, saving families more than $700 per child.
We're also providing support at school, including our School Breakfast Clubs, Affordable School Uniforms, Glasses for Kids, the Camps, Sports and Excursions Fund, free dental checkups and free swimming lessons in government schools.
