CINEA published the latest project data from the [second Union list of Projects of Common Interest (PCIs) and Projects of Mutual Interest (PMIs)]( on the PCI-PMI [Transparency Platform](
The list, [adopted by the European Commission on 9 April 2026]( includes key projects to develop cross-border energy infrastructure in the EU. It covers both PCIs (located within EU territory) and also PMIs, which connect the EU with other neighbouring countries.
The platform displays now the 235 new projects from the second list, compiling:
  * **113 electricity, offshore and smart electricity grid projects** , essential for integrating the growing share of renewables;
  * **100 hydrogen and electrolysers projects, as well as 3 smart gas grids projects** , helping decarbonise EU industry and transport, supporting the shift away from fossil fuels;
  * **17 CO 2 network projects**, further contributing to the decarbonisation of EU industry by transporting CO2 from carbon capture and storage facilities towards permanent underground storage;
  * **2 projects under Article 24 of the TEN-E Regulation** (natural gas interconnections with Cyprus and Malta).
The projects on the list will benefit from streamlined permit-granting procedures and regulatory support and will be eligible to apply for EU funding under the CEF Energy programme, including the 2026 call to be launched on 30 April, with a deadline for applications by the end of September 2026.
Through the PCI-PMI Transparency Platform, stakeholders and citizens can access detailed information on PCI and PMI projects from all Union lists so far, including their geographic location, technical description, implementation timeline, project status and expected societal benefits.
In addition, the platform allows users to explore projects through multiple search criteria, view information on CEF and other EU funding received by the projects and generate customised maps using enhanced export and visualisation features.
