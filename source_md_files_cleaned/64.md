Updated: 5 March 2026 
The Australian Financial Complaints Authority (AFCA) has decided that no financial firm (licensees) in liquidation known to be involved in the collapse of the Shield and First Guardian Master Funds will be expelled from AFCA at this time.
Any previously identified membership end dates for these firms have been deferred to a date to be fixed.
This decision is designed to ensure consumers are not prevented from lodging a complaint due to expulsion decisions or fixed membership end dates.
AFCA considers it inappropriate to take steps that would close off access to external dispute resolution while the full extent of consumer harm is still emerging as this would risk unfairly excluding impacted consumers.
ASIC has indicated that around 12,000 investors were impacted by the collapse of the Shield and First Guardian Master Funds, yet only around 2,000 complaints have been lodged with AFCA to date. It is not clear why many impacted investors have not complained.
This decision is consistent with the policy position adopted by the AFCA Board in September 2025, when United Global Capital (in liquidation) (UGC) was reinstated and the expulsion of Next Generation Advice (in liquidation) membership was deferred. The Board has determined that, in the current circumstances, maintaining open membership across all involved firms best gives effect to that intent.
AFCA has also published a short video from Shail Singh, Lead Ombudsman Investments and Advice, explaining this update.
**What this means for specific firms**
  * United Global Capital (in liquidation) will remain an AFCA member and its previously scheduled membership end date of 31 March 2026 will no longer apply, and
  * Next Generation Advice (in liquidation) will not be expelled from AFCA membership on 18 April 2026.
The Board has also determined that expulsion should be deferred for any firm known that is in liquidation and known to be involved in the Shield and First Guardian collapse. This currently includes, but is not limited to, licensee firms identified by ASIC. These firms are listed [here](
While AFCA may consider further membership decisions in the future, AFCA will, for now, not expel these firms.
Any future changes to membership end dates or complaint deadlines will be communicated clearly and with appropriate advance notice to all parties, including impacted investors.
This decision is about access, fairness and transparency, not outcomes. Every complaint lodged with AFCA will continue to be assessed independently and on its merits. Maintaining AFCA membership does not prevent liquidation processes from continuing and does not interfere with the role of liquidators.
AFCA continues to work closely with ASIC, CSLR, consumer representatives, liquidators and government stakeholders to support impacted consumers and provide clear and accurate information.
If you received financial advice from a firm involved in the Shield and First Guardian collapse and have concerns about that advice, you can lodge a complaint with AFCA.
