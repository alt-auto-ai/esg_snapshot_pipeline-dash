The [Australian Carbon Credit Unit (ACCU) Scheme]( "Independent Review of ACCUs 2022") supports landholders, communities and businesses to deliver projects that cut greenhouse gas emissions and increase carbon storage.
It plays a key role in Australia’s [net zero]( "Setting our 2035 target and path to net zero") transition and mitigation efforts. Projects under the Scheme can deliver abatement through a range of activities, including changes to:
  * land management practices
  * technologies used
  * growing trees or regenerating native forests to store carbon.
We released an [ACCU Scheme progress report]( "Reforming the ACCU Scheme: 2023–2025 progress report") in February 2026.
The Australian Government is proposing amendments to the ACCU Scheme legislation to further improve the Scheme and bolster Scheme integrity in response to multiple independent [reviews]( "Reviews and reforms to the ACCU Scheme"), including:
  * the [Independent Review of ACCUs 2022]( "Independent Review of ACCUs 2022") (the Chubb Review)
  * regular 
These amendments are in the Carbon Credits and Other Legislation Amendment (Integrity and Transparency) Bill (the Bill) exposure draft. It includes changes to both the 
  * boost integrity
  * improve transparency
  * improve accountability
  * streamline the administration of the Schemes.
The final proposed legislation package will take account of feedback from this consultation and other stakeholder responses.
