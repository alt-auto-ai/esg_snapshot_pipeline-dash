> #### Expert view
> "While this, as well as the Critical State significant infrastructure pathway, have always been available as a more streamlined alternative, we hope that legislating this power for PEPs will encourage greater use of the SSI planning pathway to support faster delivery of clean energy projects.
> Similarly, the new powers for the Planning Minister to set maximum public contributions could also help to reduce the time spent negotiating voluntary planning agreements, which would reduce post-approval timeframes.
> Whether the reforms will have an impact in practice will be highly reliant on one or both of the Energy and Planning Ministers exercising their new powers.
> Use of the exiting SSI pathway for renewable projects such as wind, solar and battery projects would be a move in the right direction. That is a simpler, more flexible pathway, especially for projects with linear components. But that alone won’t be enough. As the results of our recent Pulse Survey showed, the sector also needs greater streamlining of 'requests for information', more prescriptive environmental assessment requirements, and limits on merit appeal risk."
