The Cook Labor Government is pleased to announce the appointment of Mark Crees as the new CEO of the Arts and Culture Trust (ACT). 
  * Mark Crees appointed as new CEO of the Arts and Culture Trust 
  * New CEO has extensive experience in government and cultural institutions
  * Mr Crees takes up position from outgoing CEO Janis Carren
  * Cook Labor Government thanks outgoing CEO Janis Carren and acting interim CEO Courtney Fiddian for their leadership
The Cook Labor Government is pleased to announce the appointment of Mark Crees as the new CEO of the Arts and Culture Trust (ACT).
Mark Crees is a senior executive leader with extensive experience in government and cultural institutions and has championed the value of investment in creative industries, achieving significant strategic, operational, and policy outcomes across three states and territories.
He brings to the role extensive experience across organisational leadership and development, executive strategy, business and industry development, major cultural infrastructure development and delivery, and whole-of-government policy and service development and implementation.
In his most recent role, he led the development then implementation of New South Wales' whole-of-government creative industries policy 'Creative Communities', and has also led the development of several sector specific strategies and plans including the NSW Screen and Digital Games Strategy.
Commencing on 8 June, Mr Crees takes the position from outgoing CEO Janis Carren, the first independent CEO of the ACT, formerly the Perth Theatre Trust.
Ms Carren was instrumental in bringing to fruition the Board's inaugural Strategic Plan and Business Plan, organisational structure review, and Operator Agreement for the Perth Film Studios with Home Fire Operations.
Since Ms Carren stepped down in February, ACT's Director of Strategy and Partnerships Courtney Fiddian has been acting CEO.
Mr Crees appointment followed a competitive international recruitment process. 
**Comments attributed to Creative Industries Minister Simone McGurk:**
"Mark has significant experience working in cultural industries and his focus has been on demonstrating the value of investment in creative industries, achieving significant strategic, operational, and policy outcomes. 
"Mark's past experience with the NSW Government makes him the perfect candidate to lead the implementation of the Arts and Culture Trust's strategic vision, to grow and support Western Australia's creative industries through the optimisation of venues, expanding access and growing participation for Western Australians.
"I would like to thank outgoing CEO Janis Carren for her dedication and leadership, and the interim acting CEO Courtney Fiddian for leading the organisation during the recruitment process."
