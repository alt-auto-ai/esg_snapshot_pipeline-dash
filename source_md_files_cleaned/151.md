The Future Fund Management Agency (Agency) today announced the appointment of Richard Brandweiner as Chief Investment Officer.
Richard was previously Chief Executive Officer of Pendal Group, Australia and prior to that Chief Investment Officer at First State Super. His other experience includes senior investment roles with Perpetual and, more recently, a variety of non-executive roles.
The appointment follows a global search process in line with Australian Public Service requirements.
Richard starts his new role on 1 July 2026 and will also join the Agency’s Senior Leadership Team.
Hugh Murray, the Future Fund’s Deputy Chief Investment Officer, will continue as interim Chief Investment Officer until Richard starts his new role.
Dr Raphael Arndt, the Agency’s Chief Executive Officer, said: “I am delighted to welcome Richard to the Future Fund. He is an experienced investor and executive and has significant experience in leading investment teams to achieve their mandates.
“I thank Hugh Murray for serving as interim Chief Investment Officer and for his leadership of the investment program through a period of very significant market uncertainty over recent months.”
Richard Brandweiner said: “The Future Fund has a compelling purpose as Australia’s sovereign wealth fund. Its investment approach and capabilities are globally respected. I look forward to working with the team and the Board of Guardians to continue to deliver strong risk-adjusted returns for Australia.”
ENDS
**About Richard Brandweiner**  
2024 - 2026 Independent Director and Consultant  
2018 - 2023 Pendal Group, Chief Executive Officer, Australia  
2013 - 2017 First State Super (Aware Super), Chief Investment Officer  
2001 - 2013 Perpetual, Group Executive
Richard Brandweiner has spent over thirty years working across all asset classes in the investment management industry. He was Chief Executive Officer of Pendal Australia (formerly BT), Chief Investment Officer of First State Super (now Aware Super) and Group Executive at Perpetual Investments. During his executive career he contributed to a number of the 2 industry’s professional bodies including FSC and ASFA and was President of the CFA Society of Sydney.
In recent years, Richard has served on a number of boards and investment committees. He is Chair of Impact Investing Australia and sits on the Investment Committees of a number of family offices. He is also on the Investment Committee of Aboriginal Investment NT and is Chair and Co-Founder of OpenDirector. Richard is currently nominated to serve as Governor on the CFA Institute Board.
He has a Bachelor of Economics from UNSW and is a CFA Charterholder.
