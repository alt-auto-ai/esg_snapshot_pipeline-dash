Updated: 25 May 2026 
AFCA is pleased to announce Paul Woodburn has been appointed as the new Lead Ombudsman Superannuation, commencing 25 May 2026 on a five-year term.
Paul has more than 20 years’ experience in senior roles across Superannuation, funds management and financial services. This experience includes legal, regulatory and senior executive roles within the superannuation sector.
“We are very excited to welcome Paul to AFCA. Paul is a highly regarded leader with deep technical legal and superannuation expertise and a strong track record of working constructively with diverse stakeholders. This is a significant appointment that reinforces our commitment to achieve fair and effective outcomes across our superannuation work.” Dr June Smith, Deputy Chief Ombudsman, said.
Mr Woodburn said, “It is a privilege to join AFCA as the Lead Ombudsman for Superannuation and to have the opportunity to contribute to the superannuation industry for the benefit of many Australians. I look forward to working with talented and dedicated colleagues to serve all participants in a fair, independent and transparent way on our journey towards an Australia free from financial disputes."
In making this announcement, Dr Smith thanked Heather Gray for her significant contribution to the role of Lead Ombudsman Superannuation, which she had held since 2020.
