Are you a recent graduate looking to kick-start your career? Start your career with Finance where you can see more, do more, and be more. 
Applications are now open for the [2027 Department of Finance Graduate Program]( – your opportunity to gain valuable experience, develop skills and contribute to meaningful work in a central government agency. 
A 10-month hands-on program where you will complete two work placements tailored to your skills and capabilities. You will gain exposure to Finance’s high priority work and different areas of government – ensuring a strong foundation for your career. 
Our graduates build foundation skills as they participate in project management, undertake analysis and research assignments, and contribute to the development and implementation of policy advice and programs. Graduates help prepare briefs, submissions, reports and ministerial correspondence, and engage in team and departmental activities including meetings, training programs and information sessions. 
**We support our graduates throughout the program, with:**
  * training, face to face workshops, simulations and a major project
  * workplace coaching
  * an Executive mentor for guidance and support
  * a strong professional network of your graduate cohort, graduates from other agencies, peers and mentors
  * a range of [culture and diversity networks]( to build support, guidance and camaraderie between staff outside your graduate network.
  1. **Work on projects that shape government policies and make a real impact.**

  1. **Gain unique insights into how government operates from a central agency.**

  1. **Build a strong professional network with peers, mentors and senior executives.**
> _“Finance has allowed me to make new friends and expand my professional network. The people that I work with are not only professional and great at their jobs, but they are friendly and genuinely want me to succeed."_ – Rohan, former graduate
Apply now through the [Finance website]( before applications close on **13 April 2026**. 
We encourage applications from Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, people with disability, LGBTQI+ people, and people from culturally and linguistically diverse backgrounds. Diversity is important to us, because different points of view help us provide advice to government that reflects the values of all Australians.
Affirmative measures positions are open to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples and people with disability. 
Don’t miss this opportunity to launch your APS career – start your application today!
